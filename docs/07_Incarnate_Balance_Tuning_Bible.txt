Incarnate Balance Tuning Bible

Purpose

This is the operational source document for Incarnate balance tuning. It
consolidates current balance/tuning doctrine, current state, locked
assumptions, Balance Environment details, formula architecture, scenario
interpretation, and next-step sequencing.

This document does not replace the combat rules bible or the power system spec.
Use it as the practical guide for keeping tuning work pointed in the right
direction across chats, Codex sessions, scripts, and reports.

SECTION 5 - Locked Monster Tier Doctrine

At equal level, in a non-hard-counter matchup, assuming meaningful offensive
actions and excluding extreme low attack rolls or extreme defence rolls:

- Minion: usually dies in 1 meaningful attack/power.
- Soldier: usually dies in 2-3 meaningful attacks/powers.
- Elite: usually dies in 5-8 meaningful attacks/powers.
- Boss/Legendary: usually requires 16+ meaningful attacks/powers.

Encounter equivalence:

- 1 Elite ~= 80% of 1 PC.
- Non-hard-counter PC vs Elite duel target: 55-65% PC win rate.
- 1 PC ~= 2-3 Soldiers.
- Soldiers are standard bad guys.
- Minions are fast-cut enemies.
- Bosses/Legendaries are party-scale threats.

Clarifications:

- Soldier solo tests are diagnostics, not fair duel targets.
- Elite duels are the near-peer tests.
- Boss/Legendary tests are party-scale tests.


SECTION 6 - Action Economy Doctrine

Both Characters and Monsters may feasibly make up to two offensive attacks in
one turn:

- one weapon/natural attack;
- one Power attack, if available/off cooldown.

Therefore:

- attacks-to-defeat is the primary durability metric;
- turns-to-defeat and rounds-to-defeat are secondary metrics;
- one actor turn may contain multiple meaningful offensive actions;
- a Soldier expected to die in 2-3 attacks/powers may die in 1-2 turns if the
  attacker lands both a weapon/natural attack and a Power attack;
- party focus fire can compress boss round count dramatically.

Balance reports must eventually distinguish weapon/natural attack contribution
from Power attack contribution.


SECTION 7 - Current Formula Architecture Doctrine

Cost and outcome should be based on expected table impact, not tag accumulation.

Current formula doctrine:

- payload x expected successes x expected target count x uptime x delivery risk
  x action economy x reliability/flexibility.
- target count and AoE are force multipliers, not tiny flat add-ons.
- potency feeds payload per success.
- duration/repeat effects multiply expected output over meaningful
  applications.
- cooldown affects availability but should not make burst cheap.
- Signature Moves use the same formula as ordinary powers but a separate pool.
- unsupported mechanics should not receive full balance credit.

Current state:

- Character Builder ATTACK pricing Phase 1 has been implemented.
- Target/range/AoE now scale ATTACK payload.
- Extra damage types are coverage/flexibility premium, not duplicated full
  payload by default.
- Non-attack formula normalization is still future work.
- Secondary packet full dependency costing is still future work.
- Forge formula/value enforcement is still future work.
- Monster Outcome Calculator Phase 1 expected-output corrections are
  implemented for supported attack outputs.


SECTION 8 - Monster Outcome Calculator Doctrine

Important design correction:

Mental damage does not automatically imply Intellect roll.

Doctrine:

- Roll attribute answers how reliable the action is.
- Threat lane answers what kind of harm or pressure the action creates.
- Natural/weapon-style attacks generally use Attack, even if they deal mental
  wounds.
- Intellect matters for problem solving, control/domination, information
  extraction, analysis, and authored powers whose roll theme is Intellect.

Current Monster Outcome Phase 1 status:

- uses expected tiered successes instead of any-success probability for
  supported attack outputs.
- natural/equipped attacks use more Combat Lab-compatible semantics.
- protection survivability monotonicity has been addressed.
- preliminary C14/Legendary durability recognition exists.
- structured reroll helper exists, but trait wiring remains pending unless
  canonical structured trait semantics exist.
- some non-attack power/control/debuff outcome logic remains later-phase work.


SECTION 9 - Movement Policy

Pure Movement powers are valid authored powers but excluded from current
automated balance evidence until Movement Abstraction V1 exists.

Calibration assets should not spend meaningful budget on pure Movement-only
powers.

Movement-flavoured powers may be used only if they resolve through currently
supported mechanics:

- Dodge Pool
- Block Pool
- Resist Pool
- Augment
- Debuff
- Assist pressure
- Healing
- Cleanse
- other currently simulated effects

Do not build full map/LOS/pathfinding now.

SECTION 12 - Evidence Ladder For Tuning

Evidence sequence:

1. Static formula examples.
2. Formula smoke/harness tests.
3. Synthetic deterministic canaries for mechanical truth.
4. Campaign-scoped smoke runs.
5. Soldier solo diagnostics.
6. Soldier group-pressure tests.
7. Elite duel tests.
8. Boss/Legendary party tests.
9. Medium baselines.
10. Human playtest.

Human playtesters are finite premium resource and should not be used until
simulation knocks off obvious problems.


SECTION 13 - What Must Not Happen

Anti-patterns:

- Do not tune Soldiers to 1v1 Elite standards.
- Do not treat Soldier solo losses as automatic monster weakness.
- Do not treat Soldier solo wins against PCs as acceptable unless hard-counter
  intent is clear.
- Do not run huge batches before smoke diagnostics are clean.
- Do not tune off unscoped assets.
- Do not tune from old pre-formula-refactor numbers.
- Do not ignore action economy.
- Do not count pure Movement as full balance value yet.
- Do not fix formula shape by only changing scalar.
- Do not mutate Balance Environment assets without updating seeders.
- Do not manually fix seeded assets in UI without fixing reproducible scripts.

SECTION 15 - First Tuning Pass Instruction

Before the first actual tuning pass, open a new chat.

The new chat should start by:

- reading this Tuning Bible;
- confirming current git status/latest commit;
- confirming active docs;
- confirming Balance Environment campaign assets;
- reviewing current Soldier solo diagnostic results;
- selecting one narrow first tuning target;
- not randomly changing multiple values.

First tuning pass should not begin by globally raising or lowering all monster
damage.

The first likely decision should be whether to:

- add reporting for attacks-to-defeat first;
- create Soldier group-pressure/Elite duel presets first;
- or adjust a clearly isolated outlier such as Mental Wailer only after evidence
  supports it.
