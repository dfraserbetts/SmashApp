Purpose

This is the active source document for power grammar, packet model,
chassis/timing/duration structure, Power Tuning cost integration, defensive pool
pricing, and durable natural/Forge parity principles.

Combat rule law lives in docs/01_Locked_Rules_And_Combat_Bible.txt. This
document explains power grammar, costing interpretation, and build/cost
implications. If wording conflicts, 01 controls rule resolution.


Monster Tier Time-To-Kill And Action Economy Doctrine

At equal level, in a non-hard-counter matchup, monster durability should be
judged primarily by attacks-to-defeat or meaningful offensive
actions-to-defeat. Do not treat attacks-to-defeat as identical to
turns-to-defeat or rounds-to-defeat.

A meaningful offensive action is an attack or power output that had a real
chance to advance defeat, including a weapon/natural attack or a Power attack.
Both Characters and Monsters may feasibly make up to two offensive attacks in
one turn:

- one weapon/natural attack
- one Power attack, if available/off cooldown

If an actor uses both a weapon/natural attack and a Power attack in the same
turn, both count toward attacks-to-defeat. A Soldier expected to die in 2-3
Medium Strength Attacks may die in 1-2 turns if the attacker lands both a
weapon/natural attack and a Power attack. A Boss/Legendary requiring 16+ Medium
Strength Attacks may still fall faster in party rounds when multiple heroes
focus it.

Turns-to-defeat is a secondary metric: it is affected by whether one actor can
deliver same-turn burst through a weapon/natural attack plus a Power attack,
cooldown availability, and action-selection behavior.

Rounds-to-defeat is also secondary: it is affected by number of actors, party
focus fire, encounter shape, initiative/turn order, and whether multiple
attackers can concentrate meaningful offensive actions on one target.

Monster tier defeat expectations at equal level are calibrated against an
Average Medium Strength Attack, not every possible attacker profile. A Medium
Strength Attack means a typical/average attack profile, not an attack specialist
and not a low-investment attacker. PASS / LOW / HIGH judgement should primarily
use this Medium Strength Attack profile unless a report explicitly says it is
using a different ruler.

- Minion: should usually die in 1-2 Medium Strength Attacks.
- Soldier: should usually die in 2-3 Medium Strength Attacks.
- Elite: should usually die in 4-6 Medium Strength Attacks.
- Boss/Legendary: should usually require 16+ Medium Strength Attacks.

Low and High attack profiles are diagnostic range checks only. A high attack
specialist killing an Elite in fewer attacks than the Medium band is not
automatically poor balance. A low attack character taking longer to kill a
Minion is also not automatically poor balance. Reports should distinguish
attacks from turns wherever possible; if a tool assumes one attack per turn, it
must state that assumption.

Encounter equivalence:

- 1 Elite ~= 80% of 1 PC.
- A non-hard-counter PC vs Elite duel should usually land around 55-65% PC win
  rate.
- Soldiers are below Elites and represent typical bad guys.
- 1 PC should roughly equal 2-3 Soldiers.
- Minions are below Soldiers and can be cut down quickly, sometimes multiple in
  one strong attack/AoE.
- Bosses/Legendaries are party-scale threats and should survive multiple brutal
  injury phases or equivalent pressure before defeat.

Damage, defence, health, protection, weapons, natural attacks, and powers should
be calibrated against these attacks-to-defeat bands. Pricing follows table
impact, not the other way around. Once a level's outputs feel correct, builder
costs should be assigned so that the correct level-equivalent outputs
correspond to fair spend. Then scaling can be extended across levels.

Runtime Balance North Star

Official balance conclusions require runtime-resolved evidence: authored
Balance Environment assets must be loaded through the relevant live adapter and
resolved by Combat Lab/runtime. Balance Environment is the authored test-data
scope, not the simulator. Synthetic fixtures, formula harnesses, calculators,
spreadsheets, and simplified probes are diagnostic only unless explicitly routed
through the runtime path and labelled with provenance.

The required balance order is:

1. Attack string calibration: establish typical level-specific attacks by
   changing authored attack values such as weapon skill / dice count, die size,
   potency or wounds per success, physical strength, mental strength, and attack
   profile fields in the app / Balance Environment.
2. Defence string calibration: establish typical level-specific defences by
   changing authored defence values such as armour skill, dodge / guard,
   physical protection, mental protection, natural protections, physical
   defence, mental defence, and defence profile fields.
3. Attack/defence offset: tune attack and defence together until mitigation is
   meaningful while preserving the Attacks-to-Kill bands.
4. Apply to real assets: tune characters, monsters, powers, Forge items,
   Summoning Circle monsters/summons, and other authored combat assets only
   after the level's attack and defence strings are calibrated.
5. Cost calibration: align Outcome Calculator values, Power Builder costs,
   Forge item values, Summoning Circle costs/ratings, and other point systems
   only after runtime values feel correct. Cost values follow runtime truth.

Candidate Level 3 attack strings such as 4 dice at 3 wounds per success or 3
dice at 4 wounds per success are candidates only. They are not final doctrine
until accepted through runtime evidence.


Core Power Grammar

The shared power model is:

- Power-Level Fields
- one Descriptor Chassis
- one or more Effect Packets

Power-Level Fields define use context and shared rules grammar. Examples include
name, description, range, targeting, dice, Counter, Lifespan where relevant,
Commitment Modifier, and shared cast context.

Descriptor Chassis is structural. It changes sentence structure, required
fields, broad resolution pattern, and timing constraints. It is not flavour.

Effect Packets define mechanical outcomes. Packets own intention, potency,
timing, duration, recipient class, and packet-specific effect details.

Power Intention Roadmap: Summoning And Transformation

SUMMONING and TRANSFORMATION exist as PowerIntention values, but full gameplay,
tooling, and costing implementation remains future work. Do not treat their
presence in enums or builder lists as final table law.

Summoning backlog:

- Summon must support a range from many small / weak summons to fewer larger /
  powerful summons.
- Costing must scale by table impact. Summoning something huge and
  battle-defining should be powerful and expensive; summoning a tiny fragile
  distraction should be weak and cheap.
- Future design must define interaction, control, action economy,
  vulnerability / destruction, object / item / monster boundaries, duration,
  field presence, and cleanup.
- Summon costing is OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION. Do not lock numeric
  formula values yet.

Transformation backlog:

- Transform must support self / ally beneficial transformation and hostile
  transformation of targets.
- Hostile transformation should be resistible.
- Future design must define attribute replacement or modification, power
  replacement / suppression / addition, duration, reversion, injury and status
  carryover, gear interaction, and target consent.
- Transform costing is OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION. Do not lock numeric
  formula values yet.

Summon and Transform must preserve shared power-builder legality. Avoid creating
character-only or monster-only forks unless a future explicit rule requires a
scoped exception.


Primary And Secondary Packets

Authoritative packet-resolution law lives in 01. This section preserves
power-building and costing implications only.

Packet 1 is always the Primary Packet.

Only the Primary Packet creates the defended entry point.

If the Primary Packet does not land, contingent Secondary Packets do not land.

Secondary Packets do not create separate defended entry points unless a future
explicit rule reopens that model.

Packet 1 lands if, after its Trial or Clash resolution and prevention gates, it
applies at least one operative result unit.

For wound-based Primary results, secondary scaling uses Applied Success Bands =
CEILING(Actual Wounds Suffered / Wounds Per Success).

For default wound-band scaling, Wounds Per Success means the Primary Packet's
total simultaneous wounds per success across all damage components. Multi-damage
Primary Packets increase the denominator / band size. They do not create
separate free wound-band engines per damage type unless explicitly authored and
priced.

Actual Wounds Suffered means wounds that remain after defence, prevention,
pool/prevention spending, and the target's remaining-health cap. Overkill wounds
do not count for default wound-band scaling. Overkill-scaling is allowed only as
an explicit premium exception if authored and costed.

Major Injury overflow severity is a separate defeat-model rule in
docs/01_Locked_Rules_And_Combat_Bible.txt. Do not use Major Injury overflow as
a default wound-band or costing source unless a power explicitly authors and
prices an overkill-scaling exception.

Examples:

- Single damage type: 6 physical Slashing wounds per success creates one band
  per 6 applied Primary wounds, rounding up.
- Three simultaneous damage types: Potency 3 deals 6 Fire, 6 Ice, and 6
  Slashing wounds per success, for 18 total simultaneous wounds per success. A
  default wound-band Secondary uses one band per 18 wounds in the Primary
  result, rounding up.
- Component-specific exception: "for every 6 Slashing wounds in the Primary
  result" is allowed only as an explicit component-specific rider.
- Per-component additive exception: "each damage component generates its own
  wound bands" is a special multi-component band engine and requires additional
  pricing.
- Action-level multi-target exception: when a Secondary explicitly scales from
  the whole action across multiple Primary targets, pool actual wounds suffered
  across valid Primary targets once, then calculate bands from that total.
- Target-local default: when the Secondary affects the Primary Target, calculate
  bands from that target's own actual Primary wounds suffered only.

Vulnerability, Resistance, and Protection modify the defence roll against an
incoming packet when their triggering damage types are present. They do not
modify wounds directly and do not alter Primary wound-band denominators. For
Secondary Packet wound-band scaling, resolve the Primary Packet normally,
including any Vulnerability / Resistance / Protection effects on the defence
roll, then calculate wound bands from the resulting Primary wound total using
the Primary Packet's authored total simultaneous wounds per success as the
default band size.

Vulnerability applies a negative defence-roll modifier. Resistance applies a
positive defence-roll modifier. Protection adds defence dice. Multiple
triggering damage types on one packet may trigger multiple V/R/P entries, but
they all apply before the one defence roll against that packet; they do not
split the packet into separate damage-type defence rolls.

There is no active general Immunity rule. Damage immunities are future Monster
Trait design debt and must define their interaction with defence rolls, wound
results, and Secondary wound-band scaling if implemented.

For non-wound Primary results, operative result is measured in net applied
successes, stacks, heals, or other effect units.

Where a Secondary Packet scales from the Primary Packet, it scales from the
Primary Packet's applied result value rather than bypassing the defended entry
point.

Secondary Packet timing and dependency are locked:

- Secondary Packets are always contingent on the Primary Packet result.
- If the Primary Packet does not apply an operative result, the Secondary Packet
  does not fire even if the Secondary Packet's authored timing later occurs.
- Same-timing independent Secondary Packets resolve under the simultaneous
  result-bundle law in 01. They may be rendered as compact riders, but they do
  not resolve in authored order and do not modify one another unless explicit
  sequential, dependent, or triggered language says otherwise.
- A different-timing or triggered Secondary Packet must render as a dependent
  timed sentence that states both the dependency and the timing.
- Secondary Packets own their own Timing and Duration. They do not inherit the
  Primary Packet duration, the parent power duration, or the chassis lifespan as
  their own Effect Duration.
- Packet 2 with Instant duration must not render as lasting for 1 turn.

Do not price ordinary packet order as a hidden balance lever. Authored order is
presentation order unless the power explicitly buys sequential, dependent, or
triggered wording. Special mechanics such as lifesteal, execute riders,
shield-break follow-ups, or conditional post-cleanse bonuses require clear
authoring and future pricing treatment.

When independent Secondary Packets in the same timing window apply numeric
effects to the same target and same pool/lane, price and describe them as a
single simultaneous result bundle. Same-lane damage/healing nets before final
state checks; different lanes or different targets do not net.

For Field, Attached, Triggered, and other recurring Primary delivery, the
default is one roll per round:

- the first matching Primary trigger in a round rolls once and stores that roll
  for that round
- later matching Primary triggers in that same round reuse the stored roll
- each affected Primary Target still defends separately
- if a Secondary Packet targets the Primary Target, use that target's own
  applied Primary result
- if a Secondary Packet targets Self, Allies, the Field, an attached host's
  allies, or another non-primary recipient, the first successful Primary
  application in that round sets the Secondary payload for that round
- a fully defended first trigger sets no payload; the next successful trigger
  that round may set it
- once set, later same-round Primary triggers do not replace, improve, reduce,
  or add to the payload unless explicitly authored

Descriptor wording for those non-primary Secondary payloads should reference the
power's Primary result this round, not cumulative wounds or successes from all
same-round triggers.

Field trigger descriptors are target-local by default. A target entering,
starting in, ending in, or leaving the area affects that target unless the packet
is explicitly authored as area-wide. An ally starting or ending their turn in the
area affects that ally unless explicitly authored as all-allies delivery.

The one-roll-per-round stored Primary result rule is a global rule. Individual
power descriptors should not repeat it unless the power explicitly changes that
rule. Prefer "When [recipient] subsequently [timing], and [Power] [Primary
condition] this round..." over "then when...".


Descriptor Chassis

The practical chassis matrix is:

- Immediate
- Field
- Attached
- Trigger
- Reserve

Immediate has no persistent structural presence by default.

Field creates a location or area presence that can deliver packets according to
authored timing and legal targets.

Attached creates a hosted structural presence attached to a target, object,
weapon, mark, curse, or state.

Trigger arms or watches for a condition and delivers packets when the trigger
condition is satisfied.

Reserve prepares a power for later release according to authored release rules.


Timing, Lifespan, Duration, And Commitment

Lifespan, Effect Timing, and Effect Duration are separate concepts.

Lifespan describes how long the power's structural presence or readiness exists.

Effect Timing describes when a packet resolves, such as on cast, start of turn,
end of turn, on trigger, on attach, or on payload where legal.

Effect Duration describes how long the packet's result persists.

Commitment Modifier is a Power-Level Field, not a chassis. The current choices
are:

- Standard
- Channel
- Charge

Standard is the default.

Channel means the user may or must continue spending later Power Actions to
maintain, reapply, or sustain the power after initial use. First-pass Channel
support is limited to Immediate, Field, and Attached.

Charge means the user spends or may spend later Power Actions building power
before release/cast. Current Charge types include Delayed Cast and Build Power.

Commitment Modifier does not replace Lifespan, Effect Timing, or Effect
Duration.


Counter And Response Cost Principles

Counter: Yes is added use permission, not exclusivity.

Do not discount a power because Counter-enabled use replaces normal active
defence; the power remains normally usable as a Power Action.

Attack Counters carry a premium because they create off-turn damage or effects
and the triggering attacker does not receive an active defence roll against
them.

Defence Counters are priced as specialist active-response alternatives, not as
free stacks on top of Dodge, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or Resist.

Mixed defensive Counters are Modal Defence Counters by default. If one Counter
contains multiple active defence paths, the actor chooses one eligible path on
declaration before rolls are made. Only that path resolves. Do not price a mixed
Dodge + Block Counter as Dodge upside plus fallback Block insurance unless the
power explicitly buys Adaptive Counter or Layered Defence Counter wording.
Adaptive Counter and Layered Defence Counter are premium / explicit exceptions,
not default pricing assumptions.

Dual-Lane Defence Responses are also explicit paid exceptions. A Counter or
Assist that contains complementary Physical Defence / Block and Mental Defence
/ Block packets for the same trigger rolls once and uses that same success
result for both authored wound lanes. Price the power as buying dual-lane
coverage, not as a free secondary defence roll. This exception is limited to
complementary physical/mental wound lanes and does not apply to Dodge + Block,
same-lane Block + Block, or Resist packets.

Assist Cost And Payload Principles

Authoritative Assist rule law lives in 01. For power building and costing, the
important consequence is that generic Assist is not free off-turn payload.

A generic Assist must assist the triggering ally's intended action or defence.
If an offensive or hostile power is used as an Assist, its hostile output is
converted into Assist pressure. Assist pressure may occupy or reduce the
target's defence, prevention, Dodge opposition, Resist cancellation, or other
relevant opposition against the triggering ally's action, but it does not deal
independent damage, apply independent hostile effects, or overflow into damage.

Offensive Assist pressure is not free extra damage. It cannot increase the
triggering ally's final payload beyond the triggering ally's own generated
payload. The assisting power's normal output profile still determines the size
and lane of the Assist pressure, but that output is used only as pressure
against opposition.

Assist pressure is output-stage opposition suppression, not a roll modifier or
payload boost. It does not add attack successes, alter dice, modify Attributes,
increase generated payload, create wound bands, apply damage types, trigger
damage/wound effects, or overflow into damage. After rolls are converted into
outputs, matching Assist pressure reduces only the legal opposing output:
Dodge successes before match-or-exceed, Block or wound prevention before
payload comparison, or relevant Resist/cancellation where the assisting effect
plausibly matches that lane or effect. It cannot reduce any defensive output
below 0; excess Assist pressure is wasted.

Direct payload-boosting support must be explicitly authored and costed. If a
power is meant to add wounds, add successes directly to an ally's payload,
multiply an ally's output, create independent off-turn damage, or apply an
independent hostile rider during another actor's turn, that permission must be
written as a Counter, Trigger, interrupt, retaliation, trap, group-assist,
combo, formation, ritual, squad tactic, or similarly explicit mechanic.

Multiple Assists may support the same trigger by default only when each generic
Assist uses a distinct Power Intention. Current Power Intentions include
Attack, Defence, Augmentation, Debuff, Healing, Cleanse, Movement, and Control.
Duplicate generic Assists using the same Power Intention do not stack on one
trigger unless explicit special authoring says otherwise.

Multi-intention Assist use must not bypass the duplicate-intention limit. If a
multi-intention power resolves as written and applies several intentions to the
same trigger, it occupies each Power Intention it actually applies for duplicate
Assist purposes.

Movement and Control Primary interrupt Counters need high premium because a
strict timing win can avoid, cancel, or deny an incoming action. First-pass
timing requires strictly more successes than the trigger; ties fail.

Do not price Secondary Control as an interrupt by default. Attack Primary +
Control Secondary is priced as an Attack Counter with an after-effect Control
rider: the Attack may resolve as counter damage, but the Secondary Control does
not use strict-exceed interruption and does not halt the incoming action.
Control Primary + Attack Secondary may buy strict-exceed interruption if the
Control effect is logically capable of stopping the incoming action; the Attack
Secondary resolves only if powered by the successful Primary Control result
unless explicitly authored otherwise. Failed interrupting Control gives no
partial mitigation, no damage/potency/success reduction, and no separate
attacker Resist roll.

Preparation stacks. Panic buttons replace.


Power / Combat / Outcome Tuning Split

Keep the three tuning layers separate:

- Power Tuning: canonical power truth and BasePowerValue.
- Combat Tuning / Monster Baseline Tuning: non-power monster body, natural
  output, equipped baseline contribution, and survivability contribution.
- Outcome Normalization: radar interpretation and display curves.

Outcome Normalization must not hide bad canonical power value or missing combat
baseline law.


Cost Integration Architecture

BasePowerValue remains the canonical Phase 6 power-cost truth.

Character Builder may convert BasePowerValue into player-facing Power Point
spend through a player economy scalar, but that scalar must not change canonical
power truth.

Derived cooldown should continue to derive from BasePowerValue unless a future
explicit rule changes that pipeline.

Costing should account for:

- packet family
- packet magnitude
- packet count
- primary/secondary relationship
- chassis burden
- timing burden
- duration burden
- range and target access
- Counter access
- Commitment Modifier
- repeats, refreshes, and delayed payloads
- defensive pool depth and burst where relevant


Formula Architecture Status

The post-c89338d formula-shape audit is complete. It found that current data
paths are hydratable, but serious balance tuning is blocked until the formula
architecture is corrected or explicitly accepted as a conscious interim model.

Current Character Builder pools remain:

- normal powers: Character Level x 50; Level 3 = 150
- Signature Move: Character Level x 20; Level 3 = 60

Signature Move uses its own separate budget and must not be mixed into ordinary
power spend.

Current Character Builder spend uses the active playerPowerSpendScalar from the
same Character Builder tuning source used by the live app. That source is the
active/default Character Builder tuning row, currently
characterBuilderTuning/default. The recent scalar provenance audit found that
row at scalar 2. Earlier reports that showed scalar 3 were using a formula
harness fallback after querying a non-current "global" row, and must not be
treated as live app truth.

- spend = CEILING(BasePowerValue x playerPowerSpendScalar)

Balance Environment calibration characters are hydratable and have been repriced
against the active/default scalar 2 path. They still require scenario evidence
before any win-rate conclusion is promoted to balance truth. Treat older
overspend tables as historical audit snapshots, not current source law.

Official balance evidence for powers, Signature Moves, Forge items, and monster
actions must be runtime-resolved. The Balance Environment campaign supplies
authored test assets; Combat Lab/runtime supplies rules resolution. Formula
harnesses, calculators, spreadsheets, simplified probes, and synthetic fixtures
are diagnostics unless they explicitly route Balance Environment-authored assets
through the relevant live adapter/runtime path and report unsupported,
approximated, ignored, or stripped mechanics.

Scalar 2 is provisional calibration state, not locked economics. It may still
be rebased after expected-output constants and Balance Environment assets are
calibrated.

Current resolver shape is mostly additive:

- shared context
- structural cost
- access cost
- packet totals
- packet count
- ongoing runtime estimate
- cross-packet synergy

The audit red flag is that variables that multiply table impact are often
represented as small flat increments. Examples from the active tuning at audit
time:

- baseline melee 1 target: BasePowerValue 12
- same melee 2 targets: BasePowerValue 13
- ranged 60 ft, 1 target: BasePowerValue 13.5
- ranged 60 ft, 2 targets: BasePowerValue 14.5
- AoE count 3 sphere: BasePowerValue 19

Those numbers are useful diagnostics, not locked final constants. The locked
architecture principle is that cost and outcome should be based on expected
table impact, not tag accumulation. A power or item that delivers the same
payload to more targets must not be priced as a tiny flat addition. Delivery
and repeat mechanics multiply payload impact.


Shared Expected-Output Vocabulary

Use these terms consistently across Character Builder, Signature Move,
Summoning Circle, Forge, Monster Outcome Calculator, and Combat Lab:

- payload: wounds, healing, block units, resist cancellation, control units,
  augment/debuff magnitude, movement-equivalent effect, or other mechanical
  output.
- expected successes: expected output successes from dice count and the locked
  two-tier success model: 1-3 = 0, 4-9 = 1, 10+ = 2, maximum 2 per die.
- table-facing output: the value players see at the table, such as wounds per
  success.
- expected target count: expected number of meaningful affected targets, not
  necessarily maximum possible targets.
- uptime: how often the effect can matter over expected encounter length.
- delivery risk: melee exposure, range safety, area placement, trigger
  condition, setup requirement, or similar delivery friction.
- action economy value: whether the effect uses normal action, response,
  Counter, trigger, passive, field, attached, or another timing lane.
- reliability: accuracy, dice, Attribute route, reroll, advantage-like
  improvement, or consistency.
- flexibility: target choice, ally/enemy applicability, multiple packets,
  multiple damage types, broad application, or broad condition coverage.


Character Power Builder Formula Shape

Conceptual architecture, not final constants:

baseExpectedImpact =
  expectedSuccesses(diceCount)
  x payloadPerSuccess
  x expectedTargetMultiplier
  x durationOrRepeatMultiplier
  x uptimeMultiplier

powerValue =
  baseExpectedImpact
  x deliveryMultiplier
  x actionEconomyMultiplier
  x flexibilityMultiplier
  x reliabilityMultiplier
  x packetInteractionMultiplier
  + flatStructuralCosts
  - explicitLimitationsDiscounts

Potency must feed payloadPerSuccess. It must not live only as a small isolated
flat add-on.

Target count must multiply payload value. AoE must multiply or curve-scale
expected target value.

Duration and repeat effects must be valued by expected meaningful applications
over time, not merely by a small duration tag.

Cooldown should affect frequency, but it must not erase burst value. A high
burst effect with a cooldown still has encounter-shaping peak impact.

Range should not be a tiny flat add-on when it changes safety, uptime, and
target access.

Secondary Packets must be priced according to dependency:

- independent same-bundle packets
- linked-to-primary packets
- dependent sequential packets
- triggered / conditional packets

Unsupported runtime mechanics should not receive full calibration credit until
they are supported and measured through runtime-resolved evidence.


Potency Treatment

Potency should not be priced only as a flat additive.

Potency increases payload per success and therefore scales with:

- dice count / expected successes
- target count
- AoE
- duration / repeat
- secondary packet dependency
- action economy modifiers

Higher potency may need nonlinear scaling. At minimum, potency must be inside
the expected-output multiplication before delivery and target multipliers are
applied.


Target / Range / AoE Treatment

Target count and AoE are force multipliers.

Required doctrine:

- single target is baseline
- additional targets multiply expected payload
- ranged targets multiply expected payload by expected meaningful targets
- AoE count and AoE geometry estimate expected affected targets and placement
  flexibility
- AoE radius and shape are not cosmetic geometry
- a 60 ft sphere cannot be priced as a tiny flat increase over a single target
- target and AoE modifiers may be capped or use expected-target curves to avoid
  pricing only by theoretical maximum, but they must not be flat trivial
  additions


Duration / Ongoing / Repeating Effects

Duration should be valued by expected number of meaningful applications.

The formula must account for:

- first tick timing
- cleanup, Resist, or removal chance
- target survival
- action economy
- repeat / trigger cadence
- front-loaded versus delayed output


Cooldown / Availability

Cooldown should affect frequency but should not fully discount peak impact.

Use a two-component concept:

- peak impact value
- expected encounter contribution value

Cooldown can reduce repeated contribution, but it should not make high burst
cheap enough to decide encounters for free.


Defensive Powers Formula Policy

Defensive powers should normalize against offensive powers without erasing the
game by default.

Doctrine:

- damage must generally outweigh defence to prevent stalemate
- equal-cost defence should meaningfully mitigate equal-cost offence, not erase
  it by default
- Block Pool, Dodge Pool, and Resist Pool need different value models
- pool depth and per-trigger cap must be split
- passive and persistent defences need uptime and removal assumptions
- defensive pools are finite committed resources, not static armour

Block Pool value is comparatively linear wound prevention. Dodge Pool value is
threshold-based because match-or-exceed can avoid an entire legal packet.
Resist Pool value is lane-limited cancellation against hostile successes,
stacks, ongoing units, or equivalent effect units.


Control / Debuff / Resist Pricing

Control and debuff pricing should account for:

- expected cancelled enemy output
- severity of denied action
- duration
- resistance or cleanup chance
- target count / AoE
- action economy

Resist powers should be priced against expected prevented hostile impact, not
merely raw cancellation points.


Healing / Cleanse Pricing

Healing value should account for:

- timing
- expected wounds restored
- overheal waste
- target survival value
- action economy
- duration / repeat
- cleanse severity

Cleanse should price the severity and expected value of what it can remove, not
just the word Cleanse.


Assist / Counter / Response Timing

Off-turn and response effects have high action-economy value.

Counter damage or punishment must be priced distinctly from normal main-action
attacks.

Assist pressure is not payload and should not be priced as independent damage.

Response-lane powers may require extra cost or strict limitations because they
interact with another actor's turn and can change action outcome timing.


Signature Move Formula Policy

Signature Moves use the same base cost model as ordinary powers unless an
explicit future rule says otherwise.

The difference is budget pool, not a cheaper formula.

Signature Move budget is Character Level x 20.

Signature Move must remain separate from normal powers.

Signature Move should still be included in Combat Lab action hydration and
campaign balance evidence.

Signature Move uniqueness is open design backlog, not final costing law.

Future Signature-specific costing or permission rules may be needed if the final
design requires Signature Moves to feel more iconic than concentrated normal
powers. Possible future levers include Signature-only permissions,
Signature-only scaling, special cooldown/friction rules, main-power
concentration limits, or tying Limit Break / over-limit mechanics to Signature
Moves.

Signature Move Priming is open design backlog, not final costing law. It may be
needed to control opening burst and preserve dramatic pacing. Cooldown and
Priming should be treated as separate design axes unless a later explicit
decision deliberately merges them. Possible future levers include Signature
start state, Priming X, conditional priming, Limit Break / over-limit gating, or
budget/share/threat-based priming.

Do not lock numeric formula values from this backlog note.


Monster Outcome Calculator Formula Shape

Monster Outcome Calculator should remain a shaping/radar tool, but its expected
output assumptions should become compatible with the power and Combat Lab model.

Intended shape:

physicalThreat =
  expectedPhysicalOutputFromNaturalAttacks
  + expectedPhysicalOutputFromWeapons
  + expectedPhysicalOutputFromPowers
  + physicalTraitModifiers
  adjusted by Attributes, accuracy, target count, cooldown, uptime, and
  reliability.

mentalThreat =
  expectedMentalOutputFromNaturalAttacks
  + expectedMentalOutputFromPowers
  + mentalTraitModifiers
  adjusted by Attributes, accuracy, target count, cooldown, uptime, and
  reliability.

controlThreat =
  expectedDeniedActionsOrDebuffValue
  adjusted by resistance, duration, targets, and reliability.

durability =
  effectiveHealth
  adjusted by protection, defence dice, block per success, resistances,
  defensive powers, healing, and C14 / legendary model where applicable.

A D12 Attack monster with the same weapon should show much higher threat than a
D6 Attack monster. Rerolls and traits must matter when mechanically supported.
Natural attacks, weapons, powers, protections, and traits must combine into the
outcome model. The outcome calculator is not Combat Lab, but it should use
compatible expected-output assumptions.

Current Phase 1 implementation:

- supported natural and equipped attack outputs use expected two-tier successes,
  not chance of any success
- natural and equipped attack dice count follows current Weapon Skill-style
  semantics rather than one implicit die
- raw attack Strength is converted through the current table-facing
  Strength x 2 wounds-per-success convention
- physical versus mental threat lane is separate from roll Attribute; a mental
  natural or weapon-style attack still rolls Attack unless a future explicit
  authoring field says otherwise
- authored ATTACK power packets can feed expected-output threat when callers
  pass packet data into the calculator
- protection survivability scoring is monotonic for supported protection lanes
- Legendary/C14 monsters receive a preliminary effective durability adjustment
  where the current monster model exposes the legendary flag

Remaining Monster Outcome gaps:

- callers that pass only resolver-axis summaries still fall back to resolver
  axes for ATTACK power threat; richer packet-data plumbing is still needed
  across every reporting surface
- CONTROL, DEBUFF, SUPPORT, healing, and defensive power expected-denied-output
  remain resolver-axis or diagnostic based
- structured trait reroll/reliability semantics are not yet available for full
  reroll math
- the Legendary/C14 adjustment is conservative Phase 1 shaping, not the full
  Blaze / Major Injury decision model


Forge Formula Shape

Forge item value should estimate contribution to:

- attack output
- defence / protection
- Attribute modification
- special mechanics
- range / targets / AoE
- level / rarity scaling

Forge values should be compatible with:

- Character Builder equipment-derived output
- Summoning Circle equipment use
- Monster Outcome Calculator threat / durability
- Combat Lab hydration

Current Forge output profile uses Strength x 2 as table-facing wounds per
success. Current Forge output bands are useful diagnostics, but report-only
bands are not sufficient balance enforcement if overpowered items can save
freely.


Unsupported Mechanics Policy

If a mechanic is not currently simulated or represented in outcome calculators,
it should not receive full balance evidence credit.

Pure Movement powers are excluded from current automated balance evidence until
Movement Abstraction V1 exists. Movement-flavoured powers may count only when
they resolve through currently supported mechanics.

Unsupported traits, rerolls, effects, and special mechanics must be clearly
marked. Unsupported mechanics should receive no or reduced balance credit until
the relevant runtime and calculator support exists.


Current Formula Red Flags

Critical / High:

- target count and AoE delivery are additive instead of payload-multiplicative
- older Balance Environment overspend tables are historical after the scalar
  provenance and repricing passes; current assets still need scenario evidence
  before promotion to balance truth
- potency appears insufficiently tied to multiplied payload delivery
- Monster Outcome Calculator Phase 1 now uses compatible expected-output math
  for supported natural/equipped attacks, but remains a shaping/radar tool
  rather than full Combat Lab parity
- Forge output bands are report-only

Medium:

- cooldown warning/noise and derived-vs-stored mismatch
- secondary packet interaction needs careful multiplier treatment
- unsupported runtime mechanics can distort evidence if counted fully
- Movement currently lacks Movement Abstraction V1 balance support


Defensive Pool Pricing Matrix Principles

Duration Dodge, Physical Block, Mental Block, and Resist powers create finite
pools. They must not be valued as if they rerolled or refreshed their full
result on every incoming attack, and they must not be valued as a single instant
defence when their duration allows selective spending across several triggers.

Pool size = expected successful power-roll successes x potency.

Standard per-trigger cap = potency.

Defensive pool pricing must distinguish:

- pool type: Dodge / Physical Block / Mental Block / Resist
- expected pool size
- per-trigger cap
- duration window
- target count and recipient flexibility
- delivery chassis
- effect timing cadence
- refresh/replace cadence
- field-bound versus target-following behaviour
- channel maintenance cost
- charge dice versus charge potency
- pool-only option value
- additive/recharging special mechanics

Potency is especially important because it increases both total pool depth and
per-trigger burst capacity.

Dodge Pool value is threshold-based and can avoid an entire physical or
explicitly dodgeable packet on match-or-exceed.

Resist Pool value is lane-limited cancellation against hostile successes,
stacks, ongoing units, or equivalent units.

Physical Block Pool and Mental Block Pool are more linear because they add
direct wound prevention in the matching lane.

Pool-only use adds option value because the protected actor can spend finite
points without consuming a normal active defence degradation step.

Refresh/replace higher-of-current-or-new is the default repeated-application
model. Additive or recharging defensive pools require explicit authoring,
limits, and heavy surcharge.


Primitive Family Benchmark Approach

Power families should be priced against primitive benchmark shapes before being
trusted in complex combinations. Useful primitive anchors include:

- direct physical attack
- direct mental attack
- physical Block Pool
- mental Block Pool
- Dodge Pool
- Resist Pool
- healing
- cleanse
- movement
- control
- debuff
- augment

Primitive benchmarks are not final balance proof. They are calibration anchors
for the cost resolver and tuning seams.


Roleplay Ability And Scene Impact Framework

Roleplay Abilities use a parallel `RoleplayAbility` model and share the single
character build budget through Potential (working pool name).

Locked identity:

- Powers manipulate quantified combat state: wounds, healing, Block, Dodge,
  Resist, stacks, units, numeric modifiers, measured movement, areas,
  durations, and repeating effects.
- Roleplay Abilities establish explicit themed scene outcomes.
- On success, the selected Outcome Contract's derived Success Outcome becomes
  true in the scene and the Game Director narrates how it manifests in the
  current context.
- No additional roll or affected-target action is required to establish that
  outcome.
- No distance measurement or conversion into wounds, Block, Dodge, Resist,
  stacks, units, movement points, or other quantified Power mechanics occurs.
- A Roleplay Ability may materially affect combat by establishing a binding
  scene truth rather than by using a Power mechanic.

Core distinction: "Powers calculate effects. Roleplay Abilities establish
outcomes."

Roleplay Ability authoring has distinct authority layers:

A. Intention and Method

- Intention defines the broad roleplay lane;
- Method is structured and defines the legal approach;
- standard Methods have stable IDs, owning Intentions, definitions, legal
  approach examples, and exclusions;
- contract compatibility uses Method IDs.

B. Narrative Theme

- required player-authored free text describing how the character performs the
  Method through style, invocation, tools, attitude, or source;
- helps the Game Director judge fictional suitability, Attribute, Difficulty,
  and Scene Manifestation;
- is not mechanically costed or parsed by the descriptor engine;
- cannot define the result, change Method, enlarge Impact or Scope, add
  duration, targeting, secondary outcomes, or quantified modifiers, change
  cost, or bypass exclusions.

C. Outcome Contract

- structured and mechanically authoritative;
- defines the exact reusable scene privilege purchased;
- defines derived Outcome Lane and Success Outcome;
- defines compatible Intention, Method, Scene Impact, and Scope;
- defines Counter eligibility and the eventual privilege-cost / cooldown key.

D. Scene Impact and Scope

- Scene Impact selects an explicitly authored magnitude or persistence variant;
- Scope defines affected breadth.

E. Scene Manifestation

- chosen and narrated by the Game Director from current context;
- explains how the contract becomes true;
- requires no additional roll, action, measurement, or conversion into a Power
  mechanic.

"Method defines the approach. Theme personalises the approach. The Outcome
Contract defines the result."

"The Narrative Theme explains the action, not the result." Plain writing is
enough; writing quality, vocabulary, spelling, grammar, drama, or eloquence do
not alter cost, Dice Count, contract strength, thresholds, or entitlement.

Required authored fields:

- Name
- Narrative Theme
- Roleplay Intention
- Method
- Scene Impact
- Scope
- Outcome Contract
- Dice Count

Optional / conditional authored fields:

- Additional Restriction
- Counter permission, only when the selected standard contract permits it
- Proposed Method Name and Custom Method Request, only for Custom Method review
- Custom Outcome Request and proposed Outcome Lane, only for Custom Outcome
  review

Derived from a standard Outcome Contract:

- Outcome Lane
- Success Outcome
- Descriptor
- contract compatibility and Counter eligibility
- eventual privilege-cost adjustment and cooldown pressure

Free-text Success Outcome is not part of ordinary standard-contract authoring.

Method Registry

The standard Method registry contains APPEAL and RALLY (Persuasion), MISDIRECT
(Deception), RESCUE and INTERRUPT (Intervention), CHALLENGE (Intimidation), and
DISCERN_TRUTH (Perception). All five Intentions have an approved standard
Method. Each definition owns a concise definition, illustrative legal
approaches, and exclusions. Legal approaches are examples rather than a phrase
whitelist. A declaration may vary in expression while remaining the same
approach and may not claim another Method or enlarge its contract.

RALLY means: "Unite a bounded group around one clear shared course by invoking
common purpose, courage, duty, identity, hope, urgency, or mutual reliance."
It may invoke shared purpose/duty/identity, one immediate plan, courageous
example, dependent people, focus amid panic/confusion/disagreement, solidarity,
mutual reliance, or one honest collective priority. Appeal persuades one target;
Rally unites a bounded group. Threat/fear-led Intimidation and false or
misleading Deception remain distinct. Rally does not require identical tactics.
It cannot dominate, lie, invent a common basis, create command/general obedience,
erase agency, dictate tactics/actions, grant actions/movement/modifiers/
immunities, remove quantified effects, affect outsiders, resolve unrelated
disputes, guarantee success, or let Difficulty/Impact legalise incoherence.

MISDIRECT means: "Lead a target toward a false or materially misleading
conclusion through direct falsehood, omission, implication, distraction,
selective truth, staged cues, or deceptive framing." It may work through words,
behaviour, presentation, existing evidence, or omission. Classification follows
the deliberately created conclusion rather than the literal truth of each
sentence, so technically true selective framing may be Misdirect. Appeal is
honest persuasion, Intimidation primarily uses threat/fear, and a distraction
is Misdirect only when it establishes a false interpretation. It cannot dominate,
rewrite memory, invent perception/capability/evidence/credentials/authority,
overcome conclusive knowledge, compel action/emotion, deceive unselected targets,
bundle unlimited claims, guarantee beneficial inference, or make an ineligible
premise legal through Difficulty or Impact.

Custom Method - GD Review Required stores a Proposed Method Name and Custom
Method Request describing a reusable approach, legal applications, and
boundaries. It cannot automatically match standard contracts or establish
trustworthy automatic costing/final Cooldown and remains unapproved until the
GD or Architect maps or approves it. Custom Method text describes the approach,
not the successful result.

Declared Aim is runtime scene context, never stored or costed Builder
authoring. The player states Ability, target, specific Aim, current Method
expression, and Narrative Theme application before the GD judges eligibility,
selects Attribute, or sets Difficulty. Difficulty measures challenge only after
eligibility and cannot legalise an invalid request. Once accepted and
successful, the purchased Outcome Contract is binding. Ongoing narrative
outcomes resolve through fulfilment, genuine impossibility, or an identifiable
meaningful change that makes continuation incoherent—not mere inconvenience to
plot or encounter. This adds no numeric duration or cost field.

Current standard compatibility is Rescue with HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER,
Interrupt with DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT, and Challenge with
DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION. Appeal uses SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION, Rally uses
ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE, Misdirect uses ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF, and Discern
Truth uses UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH. Rally is Small Group only; the others are One
Target. Each family has all four approved Impact tiers where specified. Custom
Outcome review remains
available for results outside the registry and existing Custom Outcome records
are not automatically converted to a newly available standard contract.

UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH is Help and Counter-ineligible at every tier. Its exact
variant outcomes and future privilege-cost keys are:

- Minor / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_MINOR: "you learn whether the target is
  concealing something relevant to the immediate situation and, if so, its
  general nature; if no qualifying concealed truth exists, you learn that
  nothing relevant is being concealed"
- Standard / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_STANDARD: "you learn one useful concealed
  truth about the target relevant to the immediate situation; if no qualifying
  concealed truth exists, you learn that nothing relevant is being concealed"
- Major / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_MAJOR: "you learn a central concealed truth
  about the target that is shaping the current situation; if no qualifying
  concealed truth exists, you learn that nothing relevant is being concealed"
- Legendary / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_LEGENDARY: "you learn a defining
  concealed truth about the target whose significance extends beyond the
  current scene; if no qualifying concealed truth exists, you learn that
  nothing relevant is being concealed"

The selected variant must return a genuinely qualifying truth or the reliable
no-qualifying-truth result. The GD selects among qualifying truths but cannot
substitute vague, speculative, false, useless, or misleading information. The
contract does not compel revelation, read thoughts, create proof, expose truth
publicly, or bypass Method, Theme, access, or plausibility. The four keys reserve
variant-aware privilege adjustment without locking any numeric value.
When the GD accepts a specific investigative subject before Difficulty, the
successful truth/no-truth answer must address that subject directly; an
unrelated secret cannot satisfy the purchased outcome.

SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION is the Help / Persuasion / Appeal / One Target family
and is Counter-ineligible at every tier. Its working v0 name remains eligible
for the later naming pass. Exact outcomes and reserved privilege-cost keys:

- Minor / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_MINOR: "the target willingly complies with
  one small immediate request requiring negligible sacrifice, risk, or
  commitment"
- Standard / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_STANDARD: "the target willingly agrees
  to and sincerely carries out one meaningful request involving inconvenience,
  social cost, or modest personal risk"
- Major / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_MAJOR: "the target willingly commits to
  and sincerely pursues one difficult request involving substantial effort,
  personal cost, reputational danger, or physical risk"
- Legendary / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_LEGENDARY: "the target willingly makes
  one defining commitment, alliance, or promise whose consequences extend
  beyond the current scene and sincerely upholds it until it is fulfilled or
  narratively resolved"

The exact request is accepted before Difficulty. It must be bounded, possible,
within actual agency, authority, and capability, appropriate to Impact, and
reachable through Appeal and Theme. Impact caps burden but never grants
universal permission. Willing cooperation is an accepted fictional choice;
sincere cooperation prevents sabotage, pretence, and technical evasion without
adding competence or guaranteeing success. Third parties remain independent.
Ongoing commitments use global Narrative Resolution. No numeric cost, cooldown,
privilege value, or restriction discount is locked.

Small Group is one clearly bounded collection of scene-local individuals who
share the same immediate situation and can all directly perceive and understand
one Method expression. Parties, squads, small councils, households, rescue
teams, and identified civilian clusters may qualify; diffuse crowds, separated
clusters, vague bystanders, organisations, settlements, factions, armies, and
intermediary-only audiences do not. No numeric headcount is locked.

Before Difficulty the player identifies exact members or an unambiguous set,
states one bounded Declared Shared Course, and explains how Rally/Theme reach
everyone. The GD confirms boundary, access, comprehension, shared situation,
each member's eligibility, Impact, and Scope before Attribute/Difficulty. The GD
may narrow or reject the group; Difficulty may account for disagreement, fear,
confusion, priorities, pressure, or the least receptive relevant member. Success
affects every accepted member without per-member rolls, majority success,
quiet exemptions, or hidden partial failure. Membership fixes when Difficulty
is set and never propagates to arrivals, outsiders, replacements, followers, or
people hearing testimony. Members retain separate turns, minds, actions,
resources, tactics, values, priorities, and judgement.

ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE is the Help / Persuasion / Rally / Small Group family
and is Counter-ineligible at every tier. Establish Shared Resolve is the working
v0 name and remains eligible for the naming pass. It is the first approved Small
Group contract; Large Group and Faction / Army remain unproven. Exact outcomes
and reserved privilege-cost keys:

- Minor / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_MINOR: "the selected group steadies around
  one simple immediate course and sincerely pursues it through the current
  meaningful exchange despite ordinary hesitation, confusion, or pressure"
- Standard / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_STANDARD: "the selected group adopts one
  clear shared course as its immediate priority for the rest of the current
  scene and sincerely pursues it despite meaningful fear, confusion,
  disagreement, or pressure"
- Major / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_MAJOR: "the selected group commits to one
  difficult shared course for the rest of the current scene and sincerely
  pursues it despite serious fear, division, personal cost, or danger unless
  decisive circumstances or narrative resolution make that course no longer
  coherent"
- Legendary / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_LEGENDARY: "the selected group forms one
  defining shared resolve, pledge, or cause whose consequences extend beyond
  the current scene and sincerely upholds it until it is fulfilled or
  narratively resolved"

The course must be coherent, understood by all, within each member's agency and
group capability, possible, Impact-appropriate, and reachable through Rally. It
cannot bundle independent commands. Sincere pursuit means genuine priority and
good-faith effort without pretence, deliberate sabotage, or technical evasion;
members retain tactics, positioning, timing, resources, reactions, and unrelated
beliefs/relationships. The contract grants no competence, success, resources,
actions, synchronised turns, identical behaviour, immunity from setbacks, or
settlement of unrelated disagreement.

Narrative Resolution can apply to the whole group or one member through genuine
fulfilment, impossibility, or identifiable meaningful fictional change. One
member's resolution or inability does not cancel everyone else's outcome. The
contract grants no command/general obedience/future commands; multiple courses;
identical tactics/actions/movement/resources; removed identity/judgement;
actions/movement/modifiers/immunities; removal of quantified effects; binding of
outsiders, arrivals, replacements, larger Scopes, or institutional followers;
guaranteed success; false participation; evasion; or arbitrary plot-driven
cancellation. Declared Shared Course and membership remain runtime context; no
saved fields are added. No numeric cost, cooldown, Scope multiplier, headcount,
privilege value, or restriction discount is locked.

ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF is the Hinder / Deception / Misdirect / One Target family
and is Counter-ineligible at every tier. Establish False Belief is the accepted
working v0 name and remains eligible for the later naming pass. Exact outcomes
and reserved privilege-cost keys:

- Minor / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_MINOR: "the target accepts one small and
  immediately plausible false premise as true for the current meaningful
  exchange and treats it as true when making relevant decisions"
- Standard / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_STANDARD: "the target genuinely accepts one
  plausible false premise relevant to the current situation as true for the
  rest of the current scene and treats it as true when making relevant
  decisions unless meaningful contradictory evidence resolves the belief"
- Major / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_MAJOR: "the target genuinely accepts one
  central false premise shaping the current situation as true for the rest of
  the current scene and continues to treat it as true when making relevant
  decisions unless decisive contradictory evidence, direct experience, or
  narrative resolution ends the belief"
- Legendary / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_LEGENDARY: "the target genuinely accepts
  one defining false premise whose consequences extend beyond the current scene
  as true and treats it as true when making relevant decisions until it is
  decisively disproved or narratively resolved"

The exact Declared Premise is stated and accepted before Difficulty. It must be
one coherent, plausible, bounded false or materially misleading conclusion that
does not contradict conclusive target knowledge and fits access, Method, Scope,
Impact, and fiction. It may include minimal context but not independently
consequential bundled beliefs. On success the target treats it as true for
relevant decisions while retaining independent judgement, priorities, values,
and agency; the contract does not pre-author their reaction or compel action.

Minor lasts for the meaningful exchange and is fragile. Standard lasts for the
scene unless meaningful contradictory evidence resolves it. Major requires
decisive contrary evidence, direct experience, or narrative resolution during
the scene. Legendary can extend beyond the scene until decisive disproof or
narrative resolution. Unsupported suspicion, ordinary doubt, inconvenience, or
plot pressure is insufficient. Resolution must follow an identifiable meaningful
fictional change and the accepted belief cannot be secretly withheld, weakened,
replaced, casually ignored, or arbitrarily cancelled.

The family does not compel action; control response; remove judgement, values,
or priorities; rewrite memories; establish multiple premises or unspecified
future lies; make incoherent premises believable; override conclusive knowledge;
survive sufficient contrary evidence automatically; create proof, evidence,
credentials, disguises, authority, or documents; spread belief to third parties
or institutions automatically; guarantee a beneficial reaction; manufacture
love, loyalty, devotion, consent, or obedience; permit arbitrary cancellation;
or let Difficulty/Legendary Impact legalise an ineligible premise. Examples
range from a small harmless explanation at Minor, through current-scene false
authority or innocence at Standard/Major, to a defining false identity,
allegiance, origin, or relationship at Legendary; none is automatically legal.
Declared Premise is runtime terminology only and no premise field is stored or
costed. No numeric cost, cooldown, privilege value, or restriction discount is
locked.

Outcome Contract Registry

The registry catalogues materially distinct rule privileges, not every
narrative expression. Each standard definition has a stable ID, player-facing
name, lane, exact Success Outcome, compatible authoring combinations, Counter
eligibility, eventual privilege-cost key, examples, and exclusions. Registry
growth must come from actual play and approved concepts rather than predicting
every possible outcome. No numeric costs are locked here.

Initial standard contracts:

HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER

- Name: Hide from Immediate Danger
- Lane: Help
- Success Outcome: "the target becomes hidden from the immediate danger"
- Compatible: Intervention / Rescue / Minor / One Target
- Counter eligible: No
- Eventual privilege-cost key: HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER; numeric adjustment
  remains open.
- Excludes permanent invisibility, concealment from every creature, ending the
  encounter, quantified-effect removal, measured movement, another action, and
  any additional Hide, movement, or defence roll.

Legal Theme examples include diving between ruins, disappearing into a crowd,
concealing mist, slipping beneath a wagon, or a companion's distraction. Theme
cannot turn this contract into permanent invisibility, universal perception
immunity, encounter removal, battle-long concealment, or erasure from memory.

DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT

- Name: Deny Imminent Hostile Act
- Lane: Hinder
- Success Outcome: "the target's current or next hostile action fails"
- Compatible: Intervention / Interrupt / Major / One Target
- Counter eligible: Yes
- Eventual privilege-cost key: DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT; numeric adjustment
  remains open.
- Excludes permanent incapacitation, passive-effect cancellation, removal of
  stacks, units, fields, attachments, or active powers, scene-long action
  prevention, conversion into Block / Dodge / Resist / Cleanse, and automatic
  group effect.

Broader concealment or group denial requires a separate future standard
contract. Theme or higher Impact cannot silently enlarge these contracts.

Custom Outcome - GD Review Required is not a standard automatically costed
contract. It stores a proposed Outcome Lane and Custom Outcome Request, remains
Pending GD Approval, has unavailable automatic privilege costing and untrusted
final Cooldown, and is not approved/final. The Game Director or Architect must
map it to an existing contract, approve a new standard contract, or manually
approve and price a campaign-local custom contract. Recurring requests should
inform registry expansion; unrestricted prose cannot be automatically balanced.

Standard contract availability is filtered by Intention, Method, Scene
Impact, and Scope. Custom Outcome remains available for every combination.
Changing any authoring field can invalidate a selected standard contract and
requires a new compatible selection; it cannot mutate the contract definition.

Locked ladders:

- Intention: Persuasion, Intimidation, Deception, Perception, Intervention
- Scene Impact: Minor, Standard, Major, Legendary
- Scope: Self, One Target, Small Group, Large Group, Faction / Army

Attribute is not a fixed builder field or authored cost. Basic Trials and
Clashes remain available outside abilities; Roleplay Abilities are privileged
access pathways, not permission gates.

- Scene Impact: Major

- Outcome Contract: Deny Imminent Hostile Act
- Derived Outcome Lane: Hinder
- Restriction: only Agents of Morgoth
- Counter: Yes

- Descriptor: "Choose one Agent of Morgoth and roll 4 dice. On success, the

- Method: Rescue
- Scene Impact: Minor

- Outcome Contract: Hide from Immediate Danger
- Derived Outcome Lane: Help
- Restriction: Frodo only
- Counter: No

- Descriptor: "Choose Frodo and roll 3 dice. On success, the target becomes

Roleplay Ability Potential Cost Principles

LOCKED NON-NUMERIC ARCHITECTURE; NUMERIC IMPLEMENTATION PARKED. Use this exact
future order:

1. Resolve exact legal standard authoring.
2. Resolve the Scene Impact / Scope base.
3. Resolve the exact Outcome Contract privilege adjustment.
4. Resolve Dice reliability.
5. Resolve selected Counter surcharge where eligible.
6. Establish Gross Potential and the cooldown basis.
7. Resolve any separately approved cooldown tradeoff.
8. Apply an approved Additional Restriction discount last.
9. Produce Net Potential.
10. Validate against the separate character budget authority.

Symbolically:

ImpactScopeBase + ContractPrivilegeAdjustment + DiceReliabilityAdjustment +
SelectedCounterAdjustment = GrossPotential

CooldownBasisPotential = GrossPotential

GrossPotential + ApprovedCooldownTradeoffAdjustment =
PreRestrictionPotential

PreRestrictionPotential - ApprovedAdditionalRestrictionDiscount = NetPotential

No term has a numeric value. Cost resolution and character-budget validation
are separate authorities.

Roleplay Window access is universal and has no purchased-permission surcharge.
Roleplay Abilities cannot use normal Assist, so no Roleplay Ability Assist
surcharge exists. Counter permission is paid flexibility, not Counter-only use.
Help / Hinder alone is not the primary privilege price; the selected Outcome
Contract is the main privilege-cost key. Custom Outcome cannot be automatically
costed.

Baseline cooldown derives from Gross Potential before restrictions. The older
"Cooldown adjustment" stage is not a complete mechanic: any future authored
cooldown tradeoff must operate one-directionally from an already resolved
baseline. Net Potential cannot feed back into baseline cooldown; restrictions
cannot lengthen cooldown automatically, shorten derived cooldown, or cause cost
and cooldown to recalculate each other recursively. Player-selectable Roleplay
cooldown remains unavailable until this circularity gate is separately approved.

Restriction discounts reduce Net Potential only after approval. Requested band
and free-text prose are not automatic discount authority. Intrinsic contract
limits and cosmetic wording earn no discount. Without approved classification,
a future resolver may expose Gross Potential but cannot claim authoritative Net
Potential.

Scene Impact and Scope remain the base dimensions, but global Scope options do
not create support: exact standard variants do. Dice Count prices reliability,
not permission ceiling. Custom, unsupported, incomplete, or missing-tuning
authoring receives no authoritative numeric result. Large Group, Faction / Army,
all numeric values, cooldown rules, privilege adjustments, Scope calibration,
and restriction discounts remain open.

The complete resolver contract, live twenty-two-variant inventory, authority
rules, invariants, evidence matrix, and numeric unlock gates are in
[Roleplay Ability Costing Readiness](roleplay-ability-costing-readiness.md).

Power Action remains the current working action name. Do not rename it as part
of this framework alignment; capture any wider action terminology change as
future work.

Restriction discount bands v0:

- None / Cosmetic
- Light
- Moderate
- Harsh
- Severe / Oath-Level

A restriction earns a discount only if it is clear, enforceable, meaningfully
limiting, relevant in actual play, not decorative, not a loophole, and likely
to sometimes make the player wish they did not have it.

Bad / irrelevant restriction:

- "Only works on people I have met."
- Reason: too easy to satisfy or rules-lawyer.

Strong restriction:

- "Only works on Agents of Morgoth."
- Reason: narrow, campaign-relevant, often unavailable, table-visible.

Severe / Oath-Level restrictions are allowed on normal Powers and Roleplay
Abilities in v0 testing. Watch playtests carefully. If every player adds
Oath-Level restrictions to everything, either discounts are too generous,
restrictions are too easy to write around, enforcement is too soft, or the
table is genuinely enjoying oath-driven thematic greatness. Do not assume
failure; test it.

For v0, restrictions can be manually authored, reviewed, and priced by the
Architect / Game Director. No structured tag engine or automatic restriction
calculator is required immediately.

Restrictions do not add upside. If text says "only works on allies, but doubles
effect," the "doubles effect" part is not a restriction. Upside belongs to
Archetypes, racial traits, Characteristics, special features, or explicit
ability mechanics. Do not mix discount and bonus in one field.


Natural And Forge Parity Principles

Final table-facing output should compare equivalently regardless of whether it
comes from natural biology, equipped item, shield, modifier, or power.

Source origin is debug metadata, not separate balance law.

Natural-only output bands establish baseline monster expectations before Forge
item balancing.

Forge should price and validate items by final table-facing output profile, not
raw backend values and not source origin.

Rarity and item level should preserve item-level throughput truth. Broad features
may create feature pressure or effective profile level pressure, but a higher
rarity item should not simply become "same item, more damage" by default.

Shield is a hybrid item, not a free second full item.

PPV and MPV item bands should be calibrated against monster defence
expectations, with physical and mental protection allowed to remain asymmetric
if Combat Tuning supports that asymmetry.


Outcome Contract Variant Costing

A standard Outcome Contract may use one stable family ID with multiple
deliberately authored variants selected by Intention, Method, Scene Impact,
and Scope. The selected variant supplies the exact Success Outcome, Counter
eligibility, examples, exclusions, and relevant future privilege-cost key.
Higher Impact never scales contract prose generically.

DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION is one Hinder family for Intimidation / Challenge / One
Target. Its Minor, Standard, Major, and Legendary variants use the exact locked
Success Outcomes in the combat bible and these future privilege-cost keys:

- DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION_MINOR
- DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION_STANDARD
- DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION_MAJOR
- DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION_LEGENDARY

Counter is unavailable for every current variant. Preserve the current cost
skeleton: Scene Impact x Scope base changes normally by tier, then the Outcome
Contract privilege adjustment uses the selected variant where relevant. The
Legendary rivalry may eventually require a different adjustment from the
shorter hostility-redirection variants. No numeric values are locked in this
pass.

Restriction discounts apply only to limits beyond a contract's intrinsic
applicability and exclusions. Restating "only works when the target is hostile"
for Draw Hostile Attention, "only works on hostile actions" for Deny Imminent
Hostile Act, or "only works when the target is in danger" for Hide from
Immediate Danger earns no discount. Meaningful additions may include only
Agents of Morgoth, only a sworn rival, only after the target harms an ally, or
only while defending a named place or person. No numeric discount follows from
these examples in this pass.
