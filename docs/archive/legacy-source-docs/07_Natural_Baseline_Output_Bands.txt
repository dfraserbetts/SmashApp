Natural Baseline Output Bands v1
================================

Status: Phase 6 source document.
Scope: Level 5 Soldier natural-only monster output expectations and first-pass Level 1-20 output-band proposal.
Not scope: Forge implementation, item balance, global tuning changes, or final canon.

1. Purpose
----------

This document establishes v1 natural-only monster output expectations before Forge item balancing.

The goal is to preserve the three-layer split:

- Power Tuning is canonical power truth.
- Combat Tuning / Monster Baseline Tuning is non-power monster baseline contribution.
- Outcome Normalization is downstream radar interpretation and display.

The calculator and later smoke adapters should compare final table-facing output against level and tier expectations, regardless of whether the output came from natural biology, equipped items, or another source. Source origin remains debug metadata, not separate balance law.

Strength rule for this document:

- Authored Strength is multiplied by 2 for table-facing wounds per success.
- If an attack has multiple damage types and each type receives the authored Strength output, total wounds per success is the sum across those damage types.
- Total pressure for multi-target or area attacks must be read separately from per-target wounds.

No existing repo table was found that directly defines table-facing Level 1-20 natural damage, PPV, MPV, Physical Defence String Value, or Mental Defence String Value bands. The Level 1-20 tables below are therefore v1 proposals, extrapolated transparently from the Level 5 Soldier anchors and should not be treated as final canon.

4. Damage Output Bands at Level 5
---------------------------------

These bands use table-facing total wounds per success for a single target. They are output bands, not radar bands.

Level 5 Soldier single-target wounds per success:

Band      Table-facing total wounds per success   Fixture anchors
Low       2-4                                      below current accepted natural attacks
Standard  6-8                                      Defender 6, Controller 8, Glass ranged per-target 8
High      10-12                                    Bruiser 12
Extreme   14+                                      Glass melee 16

Multi-target / area pressure:

- Read per-target wounds first.
- Then multiply by targets or affected bodies for total pressure.
- Glass Cannon Natural Psionic is Standard per target at 8, but 16 total pressure across 2 targets.
- Multi-type damage can behave like higher total pressure because each listed damage type receives the table-facing wound output where authored that way.

- Do not invent mental Dodge as a separate table-facing lane.

7. Role Modifiers
-----------------

Bruiser:

- May sit above Standard in physical damage and physical defence.
- Should not also carry strong mental profile unless that is the monster's explicit identity.
- Natural Bruiser anchor: High physical output, High PPV, High Physical Defence String Value, Low MPV/Mental Defence String Value.

Glass Cannon / Burst:

- May sit at High or Extreme damage.
- Should sit below Standard defence, especially PPV and physical pool resilience.
- May use Dodge or mobility to survive, but should not become broadly safe.
- Natural Glass Cannon anchor: Extreme melee physical output, Standard ranged mental per-target output with multi-target pressure, Low PPV, Low Physical Defence String Value, High Dodge.

Defender:

- May exceed defence bands, especially PPV/MPV and defence strings.
- Should sit below damage bands unless its role is hybrid defender/bruiser.
- Can carry support or debuff powers without those being read as direct table damage.
- Natural Defender anchor: Standard damage with rider, High PPV/MPV, High defence strings, Low Dodge.

Controller:

- May have lower direct damage if manipulation/control is meaningful.
- Can sit above Standard mental survivability.
- Should not be judged only by direct wound output.
- Natural Controller anchor: Standard mental damage, High MPV/Mental Defence String Value, Extreme Dodge, control-heavy power kit, low physical defence.

8. Forge Implications
---------------------

These natural bands imply the following for Forge later:

- Item level should drive core damage and defence output.
- Rarity should mostly drive breadth, geometry, alternate profiles, riders, and versatility rather than raw mandatory damage.
- Damage type count consumes output budget because total wounds per success matters more than raw Strength.
- Multi-target and area attacks need separate pressure accounting from single-target wounds.
- PPV and MPV item bands should be calibrated against these monster defence expectations.
- Equipped and natural origin should converge through shared output bottlenecks before radar interpretation.
- Forge should not use synthetic combat smoke fixtures as substitutes for accepted natural baseline monsters.

9. Known Limitations
--------------------

- v1 is based on four accepted Level 5 Soldier natural-only fixtures only.
- This does not validate every level or tier.
- The Level 1-20 tables are transparent extrapolations, not final canon.
- Combat smoke is pre-playtest evidence only.
- Radar display is improved, but radar is still not the source of table-facing output truth.
- Fixture calculator exports reflect the export state at fixture creation time; later calculator debug improvements may display the same table-facing truth differently.
- Forge implementation has not started.
