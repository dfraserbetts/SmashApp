Roleplay System Roadmap

The roleplay side of Incarnate still needs building. It is smaller than the
combat side, but still substantial.

Future roleplay-system work includes:

- out-of-combat Trials
- out-of-combat Clashes
- Characteristics
- Traits
- roleplay-facing reward loops
- character-sheet tracking for roleplay resources
- Game Director guidance for rewards, consequences, and limits


Closed Beta Vertical Slice

Status: CLOSED BETA FIRST SLICE / PLANNING SOURCE LOCK.

North Star:

"A dynamic crisis combat scenario using pregenerated characters, pregenerated
enemies, objective tracks, and a small controlled set of roleplay abilities
usable inside combat."

This is the first controlled Closed Beta slice. It is not the full beta
programme. The Roleplay Ability timing, Outcome Contract architecture, and
non-numeric cost order are locked; final numeric costing and future registry
expansion remain open.

Locked first-slice structure:

- Use a familiar fantasy scenario to reduce cognitive load.
- Working scenario shape: a fantasy town under orc-style assault.
- Use pregenerated characters.
- Use pregenerated enemies.
- Use objective tracks.
- Use a small roleplay ability testbed usable inside combat.
- Victory should be multi-path, not only "kill all monsters."
- Combat should exist because a crisis is unfolding, not merely because enemies
  appeared.

Locked test areas:

Combat Systems:

- Attacks
- Defences
- Powers
- Movement
- Damage
- Injury / defeat basics
- Enemy pressure

Roleplay Ability Systems:

- Encourage
- Command / Persuade
- Intimidate
- Distract
- Perceive / Reveal
- Rescue / Intervention

Dynamic Scene Systems:

- Objective tracks
- Enemy Momentum / Horde abstraction
- Fire / environmental pressure
- Morale
- Discovery / weakness
- Multi-path victory

Locked first-pass party makeup:

- Striker / Legolas-type:
  - Damage, mobility, perception, horde pressure.
- Defender / Boromir-type:
  - Protection, holding ground, heroic morale.
- Authority / Gandalf-type:
  - Command, Encourage, restricted major power.
  - Scenario should test restriction not met early, then met after escalation.
- Trickster / Hobbit-type:
  - Distract, Rescue, bravery, objective play.

Gandalf-style restriction test:

- Round 1: restriction not met.
- Round 2: restriction not met.
- Round 3 or comparable escalation: restriction met.
- Purpose: test whether the character is fun while restricted and whether
  release feels earned and powerful.

Normal roleplay versus Roleplay Abilities:

- Basic roleplay must remain available to every player through normal Trials
  and Clashes.
- A player does not need a special ability to attempt ordinary persuasion,
  deception, intimidation, rescue, investigation, or similar actions.
- Roleplay Abilities are privileged access pathways, not permission gates.
- Roleplay Abilities provide explicit authored scene outcomes within purchased
  Scene Impact, Scope, and restrictions. They may materially affect combat by
  establishing scene truth, not by converting into quantified Power mechanics.
- Scene Impact should define the authored ceiling of a roleplay ability, not
  rely only on Game Director generosity.
- Difficulty remains situational and can still be set by the Game Director.

Example principle:

- A normal character might attempt to persuade a guard or even make an
  impossible appeal at very high difficulty.
- A character with an authored high-impact ability such as "Gondor Calls for
  Aid" may have a privileged pathway to attempt larger-scale persuasion because
  they spent resources on that kind of scene impact.

Roleplay Ability versus Cleanse distinction:

- Roleplay Abilities should not invalidate existing Cleanse mechanics.
- Cleanse removes or reduces eligible effects/stacks/units through its own
  lane.
- A Roleplay Ability establishes its selected contract's derived Success
  Outcome as scene truth.
  It does not remove or reduce stacks/units, grant measured movement, create
  wounds/healing, or produce Block, Dodge, Resist, or another quantified Power
  output.
- A combat-relevant Success Outcome changes the fictional state from which play
  continues; it does not become a disguised Cleanse packet.

Roleplay Ability v0 Framework

A. Architecture

- Roleplay Abilities should use a parallel `RoleplayAbility` model rather than being
  authored as ordinary combat powers.
- Roleplay Abilities should look familiar to the existing Power Builder but should
  not inherit unnecessary combat-power fields.
- The builder entry points should stay explicit:
  - Add Power -> opens the Power Builder.
  - Add Ability -> opens the Roleplay Ability Builder.
- Powers and Roleplay Abilities share one build budget.
- Shared budget working name (v0): **Potential**.
- Potential rationale:
  - broader than "Power Points";
  - works for both combat abilities and roleplay investments;
  - supports player-facing wording: "You have reached your current Potential."

Roleplay Ability authoring separates these authorities:

1. Intention defines the broad roleplay lane.
2. Method is structured and defines mechanically legal approaches.
3. Narrative Theme is required free text explaining how this character performs
   the Method. It helps the GD judge fictional reach, Attribute, Difficulty,
   and manifestation but is not costed or parsed.
4. Outcome Contract is structured and authoritative. It defines the purchased
   scene privilege, derived Outcome Lane and Success Outcome, compatible
   authoring, Counter eligibility, and eventual privilege-cost / cooldown key.
5. Scene Impact selects approved magnitude/persistence and Scope defines
   affected breadth.
6. Scene Manifestation is chosen and narrated by the Game Director from the
   current context and requires no additional roll, action, measurement, or
   conversion into a Power mechanic.

"Method defines the approach. Theme personalises the approach. The Outcome
Contract defines the result."

"The Narrative Theme explains the action, not the result." Narrative Theme may
describe style, invocation, tools, attitude, or source and guide fictional
suitability, Attribute, Difficulty, and manifestation. It may not define the
result, change Method, contract, Impact, Scope, duration, targeting, cost, or
secondary outcomes, add quantified modifiers, or bypass exclusions. One or two
concrete sentences in plain language are normally enough. Writing quality and
eloquence never change mechanical entitlement.

B. Investment profile / scale

- Roleplay-heavy builds should not be framed as "suboptimal".
- Show investment profile as a visible Combat-vs-Roleplay balance scale.
- Scale and potential spend should make build intent clear and reduce judgmental
  interpretation.
- It should help players and Game Directors understand emphasis.

C. Core fields (v0)

Required:

- Name
- Narrative Theme
- Roleplay Intention
- Method
- Scene Impact
- Scope
- Outcome Contract
- Dice Count

Optional / conditional:

- Additional Restriction
- Counter permission where the selected standard contract permits it
- Proposed Method Name and Custom Method Request for Custom Method review
- proposed Outcome Lane and Custom Outcome Request for Custom Outcome review

Derived / display-only:

- Outcome Lane
- Success Outcome
- Descriptor
- contract compatibility and Counter eligibility
- eventual privilege-cost adjustment and cooldown pressure

Remove from the active field model:

- Output Category
- Output Subtype
- Crisis Assist
- Repeat Limit / Friction terminology
- Result Window as a separate display field
- Action Enable details

Use "Cooldown". Cooldown should eventually derive from pre-Restriction / gross
cost, not discounted net Potential spend.

Free-text Success Outcome is removed from ordinary standard-contract authoring.
The standard Outcome Contract derives the exact Success Outcome and lane.

Custom Outcome - GD Review Required stores a proposed Outcome Lane and Custom
Outcome Request. It remains Pending GD Approval, is not approved/final, cannot
be automatically costed, and has pending final Cooldown. The Game Director or
Architect must map it to an existing contract, approve a new standard contract,
or manually approve and price a campaign-local custom contract. Recurring
requests should inform registry growth; unrestricted prose cannot be
automatically balanced.

Resolution-rule fields are not fixed builder fields:

- Difficulty / Opposition
- Attribute

Attribute is never a fixed builder field.
The builder should not suggest typical Attributes.
The Game Director chooses Attribute from declared method and fiction; substitution by
Characteristic or other rule is possible.

D. Cut / Deferred fields

Roleplay Abilities v0 should exclude:

- Use Mode
- Ability Shape
- Duration
- Recipient Type
- Scene Track as player-facing scope
- Full combat Range / AoE geometry
- Damage Type
- Wound Channel
- Primary Defence Gate
- Multiple packets
- Secondary dependency logic
- Local targeting override
- Full combat Descriptor Chassis machinery

Rationale:

- visual familiarity does not require field parity.
- roleplay abilities are power-shaped but not power-identical.
- Scene Tracks remain Game Director / future Scenario Manager business, not player
  optimization targets.

E. Ability Shape

Ability Shape is removed from v0.

Rationale:

- After removing Sustained, Prepared, and Triggered, leaving only Immediate
  would be clutter.
- Prepared is removed because a delayed object such as a letter is resolved by
  the quality of the action when written, not by a separate Prepared ability
  shape.
- Triggered is removed because authored triggers tend to become either too
  narrow to use or too generic to be meaningful.
- Sustained is deferred until persistence, upkeep, and maintenance are
  deliberately designed.

Roleplay Abilities v0 also do not author Duration.

The selected Outcome Contract's Success Outcome becomes true on success and
remains a binding scene truth until later fiction changes it. Duration is not
replaced by a separate builder or display field.

F. Roleplay Intentions and Methods

Approved v0 top-level intentions:

- Persuasion
- Intimidation
- Deception
- Perception
- Intervention

The standard Method registry initially contains exactly:

- Appeal (APPEAL), owned by Persuasion
- Rally (RALLY), owned by Persuasion
- Misdirect (MISDIRECT), owned by Deception
- Rescue (RESCUE), owned by Intervention
- Interrupt (INTERRUPT), owned by Intervention
- Challenge (CHALLENGE), owned by Intimidation
- Discern Truth (DISCERN_TRUTH), owned by Perception

A Method definition has a stable ID, player-facing name, owning Intention,
concise definition, illustrative legal approaches, and exclusions. Approaches
are examples, not an exhaustive phrase whitelist. New expressions are legal
when they use the same approach, respect exclusions, do not claim another
Method, and do not enlarge the selected Outcome Contract.

Appeal connects one clear request to the target's values, interests, loyalties,
emotions, relationships, duties, or understanding to seek willing agreement.
Rally unites a bounded group around one clear shared course through common
purpose, courage, duty, identity, hope, urgency, or mutual reliance. It may
honestly describe danger without becoming Intimidation and does not require
identical tactics; Appeal remains one-target persuasion, Intimidation primarily
uses threat/fear, and Deception creates false or misleading conclusions.
Misdirect leads a target toward a false or materially misleading conclusion
through direct falsehood, omission, implication, distraction, selective truth,
staged cues, or deceptive framing. It may use words, behaviour, presentation,
existing evidence, or omission; classification follows the conclusion
deliberately created rather than whether every literal sentence is false.
Rescue protects or secures a target from immediate danger through warning,
guidance, shelter, distraction, interposition, or plausible routes to safety.
Interrupt intervenes at the moment of a meaningful impending act to stop,
spoil, or break it before resolution. Challenge openly contests courage,
authority, attention, reputation, or willingness to oppose. Discern Truth
learns concealed information through observation, deduction, evidence,
intuition, behavioural tells, contradictions, or perceptive insight. Their exact
approaches and exclusions are locked in the combat bible.

Custom Method - GD Review Required takes a Proposed Method Name and a request
describing the reusable approach, legal applications, and boundaries, not the
successful result. It remains Pending GD Approval with no standard contract
matching, automatic costing, or trusted final Cooldown.

G. Thematic viability

A Roleplay Ability is legal when declared action fits:

- Intention
- Method
- selected Outcome Contract and its derived Outcome Lane / Success Outcome
- Narrative Theme
- Scope
- Scene Impact
- Attribute approach
- Fictional positioning
- Additional Restriction, where included

Intention states the broad lane; Method defines the structured approach.
Difficulty and legality respect this pairing without judging prose quality.
Outcome Contract compatibility uses Method IDs.

Current mappings are Intervention / Rescue / Minor / One Target for
HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER; Intervention / Interrupt / Major / One Target for
DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT; and Intimidation / Challenge / One Target across the
approved DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION Impact variants. Perception / Discern Truth /
One Target offers UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH and REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS, in
that registry order, across all four approved Impact tiers.
Persuasion / Appeal / One Target uses SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION across all four
approved Impact tiers. Persuasion / Rally / Small Group uses
ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE across all four approved Impact tiers. Deception /
Misdirect / One Target uses
ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF across all four approved Impact tiers. All five
Intentions now have at least one approved standard Method.
Custom Outcome review remains available for other requested results.
The registry remains exactly seven Methods and now contains eight standard
Outcome Contract families and twenty-six exact variants. Spot Weakness,
Investigate, Search, Analyse, and synonymous desired results remain Custom
Method review rather than new Methods because Discern Truth already defines the
perceptive approach.

Global declaration doctrine: Incarnate retains bounded GD judgement but places
it before the roll and protects purchased outcomes after success. The player
states Ability, target, runtime Declared Aim, Method expression, and Narrative
Theme application before the GD judges eligibility, selects Attribute, or sets
Difficulty. Difficulty cannot legalise an invalid Aim. An accepted successful
Outcome Contract is binding. Declared Aim is not saved Builder state.

Ongoing narrative states resolve when fulfilled, genuinely impossible, or made
incoherent by an identifiable meaningful change in circumstances, knowledge,
relationships, loyalties, or target situation. They do not end merely because
they inconvenience the GD, planned plot, or encounter.

H. Scope

v0 Scope ladder:

- Self
- One Target
- Small Group
- Large Group
- Faction / Army

Mythic / Realm remains excluded from v0.

- Self is a hard restriction and the cheapest Scope tier.
- One Target is broader than Self and costed above it.
- Small Group is a coherent, directly addressable scene-local group and is not
  hard-numbered in v0. Game Directors adjudicate its contextual boundary.
- Large Group remains unproven in this pass.
- Faction / Army remains unproven in this pass and has no approved standard
  Outcome Contract.
- Scene Tracks are not player-facing Scope choices.

I. Scene Impact

v0 Scene Impact tiers:

- Minor
- Standard
- Major
- Legendary

Minor: small scene moment (brief opening, small clue, limited reaction change).
Standard: meaningful scene beat (local problem resolved, one meaningful rescue, tactical
opportunity, useful reveal).
Major: scene swing (shift encounter direction, rally group, major opening, morale
turnaround).
Legendary: battle-defining or faction-scale moment (rare through cost, scenario-gated in
first slice).

- Scene Impact is authored consequence ceiling.
- Dice Count sets reliability.
- Scope sets spread.
- Difficulty sets challenge.
- Scope and Scene Impact remain hard ceilings. On success, the selected
  contract's complete Success Outcome becomes true within those ceilings.

J. Resolution model

v0 order:

1. Player declares the Roleplay Ability and describes its Narrative Theme.
2. The Game Director confirms that the use fits the selected Outcome Contract,
   derived Outcome Lane and Success Outcome, Narrative Theme, Intention,
   Method, Scope, Scene Impact, fictional position, and Additional Restriction.
3. The Game Director declares Difficulty and the relevant Attribute.
4. The player may apply a valid Characteristic substitution.
5. The player rolls the authored Dice Count with the final Attribute die.
6. On success, the selected contract's Success Outcome becomes true and the
   Game Director narrates its Scene Manifestation in the current context.

Roleplay Abilities roll authored Dice Count, not the default 3-die Trial. No
additional roll or action from the affected target is required to establish a
successful outcome. No distance measurement is required. The result does not
convert into wounds, healing, Block, Dodge, Resist, stacks, units, movement
points, areas, durations, repeating effects, or other quantified Power
mechanics.

Core distinction: "Powers calculate effects. Roleplay Abilities establish
outcomes."

K. Roleplay Abilities vs Normal Trials / Clashes

- Normal trials/clashes stay available to every player for ordinary roleplay actions.
- Roleplay Abilities are privileged access pathways, not permission gates.
- They should help character specialization produce larger/specialized consequences
  when potential is invested.

L. Outcome Contract Registry

The registry catalogues materially distinct rule privileges, not every
narrative expression. Many themes may use one contract. Each standard contract
defines a stable ID, name, Help / Hinder lane, exact Success Outcome, compatible
Intention / Method / Scene Impact / Scope combinations, Counter eligibility,
an eventual privilege-cost key, examples, and explicit exclusions. Registry
growth follows actual play and approved concepts. Numeric costs are not locked
in this pass.

Initial standard registry only:

HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER

- Player name: Hide from Immediate Danger
- Derived lane: Help
- Exact Success Outcome: "the target becomes hidden from the immediate danger"
- Compatible: Intervention / Rescue / Minor / One Target
- Counter eligible: No
- Eventual privilege-cost key: HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER; numeric adjustment
  remains open.
- Excludes permanent invisibility; hiding from every creature; ending the
  encounter; quantified-effect removal; measured movement; another action; and
  any additional Hide, movement, or defence roll.
- Theme examples: diving between ruins, disappearing into a crowd, concealing
  mist, slipping under a wagon, or a companion's distraction.
- Theme cannot turn this into universal perception immunity, encounter removal,
  battle-long concealment, permanent invisibility, or erasure from memory.

DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT

- Player name: Deny Imminent Hostile Act
- Derived lane: Hinder
- Exact Success Outcome: "the target's current or next hostile action fails"
- Compatible: Intervention / Interrupt / Major / One Target
- Counter eligible: Yes
- Eventual privilege-cost key: DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT; numeric adjustment
  remains open.
- Excludes permanent incapacitation; passive-effect cancellation; removal of
  existing stacks, units, fields, attachments, or active powers; prevention of
  every scene action; conversion into Block, Dodge, Resist, or Cleanse; and
  automatic group effect.

Broader concealment or group-scale denial requires a distinct future contract;
Theme or higher Impact cannot silently enlarge either standard contract.

Standard contract availability is filtered by Intention, Method, Scene
Impact, and Scope. Custom Outcome - GD Review Required remains available for
all combinations. If an authoring-field change invalidates a standard contract,
the builder requires a new compatible selection rather than mutating the
contract.

Example: You Shall Not Pass uses DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT, derives Hinder and
"the target's current or next hostile action fails", and renders: "Choose one
Agent of Morgoth and roll 4 dice. On success, the target's current or next
hostile action fails."

Example: Frodo Hide! uses HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER, derives Help and "the
target becomes hidden from the immediate danger", and renders: "Choose Frodo
and roll 3 dice. On success, the target becomes hidden from the immediate
danger."

M. Roleplay Abilities vs Cleanse

- Roleplay Abilities should not invalidate existing Cleanse.
- Cleanse stays the direct effect lane for removing / reducing eligible effects.
- Cleanse must remain mechanically distinct and should not be invalidated by
  Roleplay Abilities.
- A Roleplay Ability establishes its explicit Success Outcome as scene truth.
  It does not remove or reduce stacks/units or create another quantified
  Cleanse, Resist, movement, damage, healing, or defence output.
- A successful scene outcome may change what is fictionally possible next, but
  it is not converted into a Power packet.

N. Timing / Action Economy

Locked timing lanes:

A. Standard Use in combat

- consumes the actor's Power Action.

B. Standard Use out of combat

- is an ordinary scene declaration;
- has no combat action cost.

C. Roleplay Window

- costs 1 Response;
- is universally available to Roleplay Abilities and is not an authored or
  purchased permission;
- permits one Roleplay Ability maximum per Roleplay Window.

D. Counter

- costs 1 Response;
- requires authored paid Counter permission;
- is legal only when the Ability user is personally targeted by the hostile
  action or effect;
- adds flexibility and does not make the Ability Counter-only.

Roleplay Abilities cannot be used as normal Assists and cannot enter the normal
Assist declaration stack. Ordinary Power Assist law remains unchanged.

Roleplay Window order:

1. The Game Director describes a meaningful, perceivable impending intent,
   movement, preparation, or scene escalation before formal hostile-action
   declaration.
2. The Game Director explicitly opens a Roleplay Window.
3. One eligible actor may spend 1 Response and declare one Roleplay Ability.
4. The Game Director confirms that the use fits the selected Outcome Contract,
   its derived Outcome Lane and Success Outcome, Narrative Theme,
   Intention, Method, Scope, Scene Impact, fictional position, and Additional
   Restriction.
5. The Game Director declares Difficulty and the relevant Attribute.
6. The Ability roll resolves.
7. On success, the selected contract's Success Outcome becomes true and the
   Game Director narrates its Scene Manifestation.
8. The Game Director continues the activation from the changed scene state.
9. When a formal hostile action is declared, the normal Response Window /
   Incarnate Stack begins.

Guardrails:

- The Game Director opens the window; players cannot interrupt every
  descriptive sentence.
- A window is for a meaningful scene beat, not every movement or incidental
  act.
- Hidden actions do not need to be revealed merely because Roleplay Abilities
  exist.
- Only perceivable intent can normally create a window.
- Only one Roleplay Ability may be declared in each window.
- A failed Roleplay Ability does not weaken or consume the impending action.
- The selected Outcome Contract and derived Success Outcome remain binding on
  success; Theme cannot enlarge them.
- Scope and Scene Impact remain hard ceilings.
- The Game Director decides Difficulty and Attribute after confirming thematic
  legality.

Power Action remains the current working action name. Wider action terminology
changes remain future work.

O. Potential Cost Skeleton

COMPLETED ARCHITECTURE / NUMERIC IMPLEMENTATION PARKED. Roleplay semantics are
mature enough for structural costing readiness, while authoritative prices stay
behind the whole-system balance gates.

The locked order resolves exact legal authoring, Impact/Scope base, exact
contract privilege key, Dice reliability, and selected eligible Counter into
Gross Potential. Baseline cooldown uses Gross Potential. Any separately approved
cooldown tradeoff follows without recursion; an approved Additional Restriction
discount applies last to produce Net Potential. Budget validation is separate.

Roleplay Window access is universal and has no purchased permission surcharge.
Help / Hinder alone is not the primary privilege price. The selected Outcome
Contract is the main privilege-cost key. Custom Outcome cannot be automatically
costed.

Runtime Difficulty, Theme, writing, and runtime declarations do not alter build
cost. Restriction prose and a requested band are not approval. Numeric values,
Builder totals, cooldown controls/displays, validation, budget enforcement, and
saved cost/cooldown fields remain unavailable. The focused authority, live
variant inventory, circularity gate, evidence matrix, and unlock gates are in
[Roleplay Ability Costing Readiness](roleplay-ability-costing-readiness.md).

P. Restriction Discount Bands

Restriction discount bands for v0 testing:

- None / Cosmetic
- Light
- Moderate
- Harsh
- Severe / Oath-Level

These labels are requested authoring classifications only. They do not grant an
automatic authoritative discount; approval provenance and discount values remain
OPEN.

A restriction earns a discount only if it is clear, enforceable, meaningfully
limiting, relevant in actual play, not decorative, not a loophole, and likely
to sometimes make the player wish they did not have it.

Bad / irrelevant restriction:

- "Only works on people I have met."
- Reason: too easy to satisfy or rules-lawyer.

Strong restriction:

- "Only works on Agents of Morgoth."
- Reason: narrow, campaign-relevant, often unavailable, table-visible.

Severe / Oath-Level restrictions are allowed on normal Powers and Roleplay
Abilities in v0 testing. Watch playtests carefully. If every player adds
Oath-Level restrictions to everything, either discounts are too generous,
restrictions are too easy to write around, enforcement is too soft, or the
table is genuinely enjoying oath-driven thematic greatness. Do not assume
failure; test it.

For v0, restrictions can be manually authored, reviewed, and priced by the
Architect / Game Director. No structured tag engine or automatic restriction
calculator is required immediately.

Restrictions do not add upside. If text says "only works on allies, but doubles
effect," the "doubles effect" part is not a restriction. Upside belongs to
Archetypes, racial traits, Characteristics, special features, or explicit
ability mechanics. Do not mix discount and bonus in one field.

Two use modes:

1. Out of combat or on NPCs in combat:
   - establishes the selected contract's Success Outcome in the scene with no combat
     action cost.
2. In combat on actors:
   - establishes the selected contract's Success Outcome as scene truth from
     which combat continues, without converting it into quantified Power
     mechanics.

Generic scene actions:

- Generic scene actions are not powers.
- Anyone can attempt them if fictionally positioned.
- Examples:
  - bar the gate,
  - drag a civilian from the fire,
  - throw water onto the stables,
  - climb the watchtower,
  - search for the commander,
  - repair a barricade,
  - shout orders to guards.
- The first slice should use both generic scene actions and authored roleplay
  abilities.

Signature Move Uniqueness

OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION.

Purpose:

- Ensure the Signature Move feels like the character's iconic move, not merely
  a normal power with a larger point budget.

Risk:

- If Signature Moves are differentiated only by raw points, players may treat
  them as optimization slots rather than defining character expressions.

Current early design directions:

1. Signature-only permission.
   Signature Moves may be allowed to combine effects or reach dramatic shapes
   normal powers cannot.
2. Signature-only scaling.
   Signature Moves may get special upgrade hooks at certain character levels.
3. Signature-only narrative weight.
   Signature Moves should be named, previewed, printed, and treated as the
   character's iconic move.
4. Signature-only cooldown/friction rules.
   Signature Moves may hit harder because the system knows they are major
   moments with special usage friction.
5. Main-power concentration limits.
   Normal powers may need limits so they cannot easily become more
   signature-like than the Signature Move.
6. Limit Break / over-limit restriction.
   The Limit Break / over-limit / cinematic push mechanic could potentially be
   restricted to Signature Moves only, which may be a simple way to make
   Signature Moves feel unique.

Do not stack all uniqueness protections by default. Future design should find
the smallest uniqueness package that makes Signature Moves feel iconic.

This item connects to Character Creator, Power Builder, Limit Break /

Signature Move Priming

OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION.

Purpose:

- Prevent powerful Signature Moves from becoming automatic Turn 1 nukes.

Risk:

- If every character can open combat with a high-output Signature Move,
  optimal play may become "everyone blows Signature immediately," especially at
  higher levels.

Design principle:

- Cooldown and Priming should be separate concepts.
- Cooldown limits repeat use after the move has been used.
- Priming limits whether the move is available at the start of combat.
- Do not solve this by automatically saying "starts on cooldown" unless the
  Architect later chooses that for simplicity.
- A cleaner model may be a Signature Start State or Priming Requirement.

Possible start states:

- Ready: available from Turn 1.
- Priming X: starts unavailable and becomes available after X rounds or turns.
- Conditional: becomes available when a defined trigger is met.
- Limit Break / Overdrive gated: requires a dramatic trigger, resource, or
  special condition.

Possible prime conditions:

- Damage Signature: primes after dealing or taking damage.
- Control Signature: primes after applying a status or winning a Clash.
- Defence Signature: primes when an ally is injured or protected.
- Support Signature: primes after assisting or healing.
- Finisher Signature: primes when a target is below a health threshold or has
  an injury.
- Transformation Signature: primes after Round 2, after taking damage, or
  after spending a resource.
- Summon Signature: primes after channeling, preparing a field, or spending a
  turn.

Player-facing wording examples only:

- "Priming 2: This Signature cannot be used until the start of your second
  turn."
- "Prime Condition - Marked Target: This Signature becomes available after you
  mark, wound, or study a target."

This belongs near Signature Move Uniqueness, but should remain tracked
separately. Signature Move Priming connects to Signature Move Uniqueness, Limit
Break / over-limit design, cooldown/friction rules, combat pacing, Character
Builder display, Power Builder validation, and handbook explanation.

Future design should decide whether priming is manually authored,
automatically inferred from Signature budget/share/threat rating, or assigned by
Game Director / Architect approval.

No final thresholds, formulas, or implementation behaviour are locked by this
roadmap note.


Approved Roleplay Outcome Contract Family: Establish Shared Resolve

ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE / Establish Shared Resolve is the accepted working v0
name, still eligible for the later naming pass. It is Help, Persuasion / Rally,
Small Group, and never Counter eligible. It is the first approved Small Group
contract; this pass creates no Large Group or Faction / Army variant.

- Minor / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_MINOR: "the selected group steadies around
  one simple immediate course and sincerely pursues it through the current
  meaningful exchange despite ordinary hesitation, confusion, or pressure"
- Standard / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_STANDARD: "the selected group adopts one
  clear shared course as its immediate priority for the rest of the current
  scene and sincerely pursues it despite meaningful fear, confusion,
  disagreement, or pressure"
- Major / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_MAJOR: "the selected group commits to one
  difficult shared course for the rest of the current scene and sincerely
  pursues it despite serious fear, division, personal cost, or danger unless
  decisive circumstances or narrative resolution make that course no longer
  coherent"
- Legendary / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_LEGENDARY: "the selected group forms one
  defining shared resolve, pledge, or cause whose consequences extend beyond
  the current scene and sincerely upholds it until it is fulfilled or
  narratively resolved"

Small Group means one clearly bounded collection of scene-local individuals who
share the same immediate situation and can all directly perceive and understand
one Method expression. Parties, squads, small councils, households, rescue
teams, and identified civilian clusters may qualify. Diffuse crowds, separated
clusters, vague bystanders, organisations, settlements, factions, armies, and
people reached only through intermediaries do not. No numeric headcount is
locked.

Before Difficulty the player identifies exact members or an unambiguous set,
states one bounded shared course, and explains how Rally and Narrative Theme
reach everyone. The GD confirms boundary, access, comprehension, shared
situation, each member's eligibility, Impact, and Scope before selecting
Attribute/Difficulty. Difficulty may reflect disagreement, fear, confusion,
priorities, pressure, or the least receptive relevant member. An illegal group
is narrowed or rejected before Difficulty.

Every accepted member receives a successful outcome. There is no per-member
roll, majority interpretation, quietly exempted dissenter, or hidden partial
failure. Membership fixes when Difficulty is set and does not propagate to
arrivals, outsiders, replacements, followers, testimony recipients, or
institutions. Small Group creates no shared turn, mind, action, or resource pool.

The Declared Shared Course must be one coherent priority, understood by all,
within each member's agency and group capability, possible, Impact-appropriate,
and reachable through Rally. Minimum context is legal; multiple independent
commands are not. Examples include staying together during an evacuation,
holding until civilians clear, protecting wounded while crossing, maintaining a
united refusal, or completing a rescue before pursuing thieves.

Sincere pursuit requires genuine priority and good-faith effort without
pretence, deliberate sabotage, or technical evasion. Members retain tactics,
positioning, normal timing, resources, unexpected-response judgement, values,
priorities, and unrelated relationships. The contract grants no competence,
success, resources, actions, synchronised turns, identical behaviour, immunity
from setbacks, or resolution of unrelated disagreement.

Global Narrative Resolution may apply to the whole group or an individual when
the course is genuinely fulfilled, impossible, or made incoherent by an
identifiable meaningful fictional change. One member's inability or resolution
does not cancel everyone else. Plot inconvenience is insufficient.

The family grants no open-ended command/general obedience, future unspecified
courses, multiple courses, dictated tactics/actions/movement/resources, removed
identity/judgement, actions/movement/modifiers/immunities, quantified-effect
removal, guaranteed success, binding of outsiders/arrivals/replacements/larger
Scopes, institutional propagation, false participation, sabotage, technical
evasion, or arbitrary cancellation. Declared Shared Course and membership are
runtime context only; no saved course/member fields or Builder controls are
added. No numeric costs, cooldowns, Scope multipliers, or headcounts are locked.


Approved Roleplay Outcome Contract Family: Establish False Belief

ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF / Establish False Belief is the accepted working v0
name, still eligible for the later naming pass. It is Hinder, Deception /
Misdirect, One Target, and never Counter eligible. It guarantees that the target
genuinely accepts one bounded false or materially misleading premise and treats
it as true for relevant decisions while retaining independent judgement,
priorities, values, and agency.

- Minor / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_MINOR: "the target accepts one small and
  immediately plausible false premise as true for the current meaningful
  exchange and treats it as true when making relevant decisions"
- Standard / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_STANDARD: "the target genuinely accepts one
  plausible false premise relevant to the current situation as true for the
  rest of the current scene and treats it as true when making relevant
  decisions unless meaningful contradictory evidence resolves the belief"
- Major / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_MAJOR: "the target genuinely accepts one
  central false premise shaping the current situation as true for the rest of
  the current scene and continues to treat it as true when making relevant
  decisions unless decisive contradictory evidence, direct experience, or
  narrative resolution ends the belief"
- Legendary / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_LEGENDARY: "the target genuinely accepts
  one defining false premise whose consequences extend beyond the current scene
  as true and treats it as true when making relevant decisions until it is
  decisively disproved or narratively resolved"

Before Attribute or Difficulty, the player states the exact false or materially
misleading premise and explains how Misdirect and Narrative Theme establish it.
The GD judges whether it is clear, one bounded coherent conclusion, plausible to
the target, not barred by conclusive knowledge, reachable through the Method,
appropriate to Impact and persistence, and legal for access and Scope. The GD
may reject or request revision before Difficulty. Once accepted and successful,
the GD cannot secretly withhold belief, replace it with something weaker or
unrelated, or cancel it because it obstructs the planned scene. Difficulty
measures an eligible deception and cannot legalise an impossible premise.

Misdirect may use a plausible lie, decisive omission, false explanation,
misleading framing of true evidence, false implication of authority/innocence/
danger/normality, an existing prop/disguise/document/reputation/cue, selective
information, or staged behaviour. Appeal instead uses honest persuasion;
Intimidation primarily uses threat or fear; a distraction is Misdirect only
when it establishes a false interpretation rather than merely dividing
attention. Misdirect cannot dominate, rewrite memory, invent perception,
capability, evidence, documents, disguises, credentials, or authority, overcome
conclusive knowledge, compel action/emotion, deceive unselected targets, bundle
unlimited claims, guarantee beneficial inferences, or make an ineligible premise
legal through Difficulty or Impact.

One bounded premise may contain only the minimum context needed for one coherent
conclusion, not multiple independently consequential beliefs. The target must
treat the accepted premise as true where relevant, but the contract does not
pre-author their decisions: competing duties, values, priorities, emergency
conditions, and self-preservation remain real. Secret disbelief, casual
relevant disregard, instant unsupported suspicion, or acting as if the premise
were false cannot nullify success.

Minor is fragile and ends with the meaningful exchange or an identifiable
resolution. Standard lasts for the scene unless meaningful contradictory
evidence materially challenges it. Major lasts for the scene and requires
decisive contrary evidence, direct experience, or narrative resolution.
Legendary may extend beyond the scene until decisive disproof or narrative
resolution. Ordinary doubt, unsupported suspicion, inconvenience, and plot
pressure are insufficient; global Narrative Resolution requires an identifiable
meaningful fictional change explainable to the table.

Illustrative premises include a harmless source for a noise or corrected minor
procedure (Minor); authorised visitor, moved meeting, harmless current danger,
or immediate innocence (Standard); superseded orders, a betraying lieutenant,
another faction causing the crisis, or central legitimate authority (Major);
and rightful heir, enemy allegiance, revised foundational history, false basis
for an oath, or defining identity/origin/relationship (Legendary). Every example
still needs target knowledge, plausibility, Method, access, Impact, and fiction.

The contract does not compel an action or response; remove judgement, values,
priorities, or agency; rewrite memories; establish multiple premises or future
unspecified lies; make incoherent premises believable; override conclusive
knowledge; survive sufficient contradictory evidence automatically; create
proof/evidence/credentials/disguises/authority/documents; spread automatically
to third parties or institutions; guarantee a beneficial reaction; manufacture
love, loyalty, devotion, consent, or obedience; permit arbitrary cancellation;
or let Difficulty/Legendary Impact legalise an ineligible premise. Declared
Premise is runtime terminology only; no premise field is stored or exposed in
the Builder. No numeric costs, cooldowns, privilege values, or restriction
discounts are locked.


Approved Roleplay Outcome Contract Family: Secure Willing Cooperation

SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION / Secure Willing Cooperation is the accepted working
v0 name, still eligible for the later naming pass. It is Help, Persuasion /
Appeal, One Target, and never Counter eligible. It guarantees the target's
willing choice to cooperate with one clear request accepted before Difficulty.

- Minor / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_MINOR: "the target willingly complies with
  one small immediate request requiring negligible sacrifice, risk, or
  commitment"
- Standard / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_STANDARD: "the target willingly agrees
  to and sincerely carries out one meaningful request involving inconvenience,
  social cost, or modest personal risk"
- Major / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_MAJOR: "the target willingly commits to
  and sincerely pursues one difficult request involving substantial effort,
  personal cost, reputational danger, or physical risk"
- Legendary / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_LEGENDARY: "the target willingly makes
  one defining commitment, alliance, or promise whose consequences extend
  beyond the current scene and sincerely upholds it until it is fulfilled or
  narratively resolved"

The request must be clear, singular, bounded, possible, inside actual agency,
authority, and capability, appropriate to Impact, and reachable through Appeal
and Narrative Theme. High Difficulty or Legendary Impact cannot legalise an
ineligible request. Extreme sacrifice, defining betrayal, identity abandonment,
or near-certain death needs a coherent basis in existing values/circumstances.
Authority secures sincere use of existing authority, not automatic control of
independent third parties.

Willingly means accepted fictional choice, not domination. Sincerely means
good-faith effort without sabotage, pretence, or technical evasion. It neither
adds competence nor guarantees success. The family grants no open-ended
authority, permanent obedience, future unspecified requests, manufactured
love/intimacy/consent, impossible capabilities, or arbitrary plot-driven
cancellation. Ongoing commitments follow global Narrative Resolution. No
numeric values are locked.


Approved Roleplay Outcome Contract Family: Uncover Concealed Truth

UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH / Uncover Concealed Truth is locked as Help,
Perception / Discern Truth, One Target, and never Counter eligible. It grants
the Ability user reliable concealed information about the target without
compelling speech, confession, cooperation, or deliberate revelation.

Exact variants and reserved privilege-cost keys:

- Minor / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_MINOR: "you learn whether the target is
  concealing something relevant to the immediate situation and, if so, its
  general nature; if no qualifying concealed truth exists, you learn that
  nothing relevant is being concealed"
- Standard / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_STANDARD: "you learn one useful concealed
  truth about the target relevant to the immediate situation; if no qualifying
  concealed truth exists, you learn that nothing relevant is being concealed"
- Major / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_MAJOR: "you learn a central concealed truth
  about the target that is shaping the current situation; if no qualifying
  concealed truth exists, you learn that nothing relevant is being concealed"
- Legendary / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_LEGENDARY: "you learn a defining
  concealed truth about the target whose significance extends beyond the
  current scene; if no qualifying concealed truth exists, you learn that
  nothing relevant is being concealed"

A qualifying truth is true, concerns the selected target, is not already known
or plainly apparent, meets the chosen variant's relevance/significance, and is
plausibly reachable through Method, Narrative Theme, access, and fiction. The
GD selects one when several qualify, and must give information that genuinely
satisfies the tier rather than vague, useless, speculative, false, or misleading
substitution. A successful accepted declaration cannot receive the no-truth
result when a qualifying truth exists. When none exists, learning that nothing
relevant is concealed is reliable information and not a wasted success.
When the GD accepts a specific investigative subject before Difficulty, the
successful truth or no-truth answer must directly address it; an unrelated
qualifying secret cannot be substituted. Broad accepted subjects retain the GD's
choice within their accepted breadth.

The family does not read thoughts or memories, create proof, publicly expose
truth, force belief, reveal every secret or implication, grant quantified
modifiers, mechanically alter the target, or bypass Discern Truth, Narrative
Theme, access, and plausibility. The player cannot choose among several
qualifying truths. No numeric cost or cooldown value is locked. Existing Custom
Outcome records remain Custom and are never automatically approved or rewritten
into this family.


Approved Roleplay Outcome Contract Family: Reveal Exploitable Weakness

REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS / Reveal Exploitable Weakness is locked as Help,
Perception / Discern Truth, One Target, and never Counter eligible. Its accepted
v0 working name remains eligible for the naming pass. It grants one true and
actionable weakness, opening, route, pattern, dependency, or leverage point
concerning the accepted target and Declared Aim, appropriate to Impact.

Exact variants and reserved privilege-cost keys:

- Minor / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_MINOR: "you identify one small
  immediately useful weakness, opening, or opportunity concerning the target
  for the current meaningful exchange; if no qualifying exploitable
  opportunity exists, you learn that none is presently accessible"
- Standard / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_STANDARD: "you identify one useful
  exploitable weakness, route, pattern, dependency, or leverage point
  concerning the target for the current situation; if no qualifying
  exploitable opportunity exists, you learn that none is presently accessible"
- Major / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_MAJOR: "you reveal one central exploitable
  vulnerability or opportunity concerning the target that is shaping the
  current scene and can materially change how it is approached; if no
  qualifying exploitable opportunity exists, you learn that none is presently
  accessible"
- Legendary / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_LEGENDARY: "you reveal one defining
  vulnerability, hidden route, dependency, or leverage point concerning the
  target whose significance extends beyond the current scene; if no qualifying
  exploitable opportunity exists, you learn that none is presently accessible"

Target means the bounded subject to which the contract applies. One Target may
be one character, creature, object, mechanism, bounded location or structure,
plan, ritual, environmental feature, or other coherent specifically identified
scene subject where contract and fiction support it. A nominal target cannot
bypass Scope by covering a diffuse crowd, unrelated subjects, battlefield,
city, faction, army, whole war, organisation membership, or abstract
collection. The GD judges boundary and access before Attribute or Difficulty.

A qualifying opportunity directly answers the accepted runtime Declared Aim,
concerns the target, is true or coherent, Impact-appropriate, reachable through
Discern Truth, Theme, access, and fiction, actionable rather than trivia, and
not already understood in that actionable form. It may be established, inferred,
an existing mechanical property, or a coherent previously unspecified Scene
Manifestation within locked fiction, Impact, Scope, and purchased result. The GD
chooses among qualifying answers and explains the opportunity, subject, why it
matters, and how it can inform a meaningful next choice.

No prepared opportunity is required. The GD cannot return "none" because none
was written, the encounter may change, the players may succeed, or the plot is
inconvenienced. That fallback is legal only when fiction genuinely provides no
qualifying accessible opportunity. Accepted practical subjects must be answered
directly; unrelated secrets or trivia do not satisfy the contract.

Uncover Concealed Truth answers what hidden fact is true. Reveal Exploitable
Weakness answers what actionable leverage can be used. The selected family is
binding and existing Uncover or Custom selections are not silently changed.
Actionable information does not automatically exploit, defeat, damage, disable,
traverse, persuade, grant quantified output, supply missing resources, or make a
later action succeed. It may identify but never create or alter an existing
mechanical property. Target, subject, Aim, and result remain runtime context;
there is no new Recipient Type or saved subject, investigation, objective,
weakness, or opportunity field. No numeric costing or cooldown value is added.

Illustrative Impact examples: Minor may expose a loose fastening or momentary
attention shift; Standard an exposed ritual focus, service route, attack
pattern, jam point, or messenger dependency; Major a central support, signalling
system, magical anchor, logistical dependency, or scene-changing route; and
Legendary an oath-bound flaw, fortress-defining route, foundational legendary
dependency, or leverage point significant beyond the scene. These examples do
not establish automatic legality.


Approved Roleplay Outcome Contract Family: Draw Hostile Attention

The launch Roleplay Ability registry supports a stable standard contract family
with multiple explicitly authored variants selected by Intention, Method,
Scene Impact, and Scope. Each variant owns its exact Success Outcome, Counter
eligibility, examples, exclusions, and future privilege-cost adjustment. A
higher Impact does not generically enlarge prose; every supported tier is
deliberately authored.

DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION / Draw Hostile Attention is locked as Hinder,
Intimidation / Challenge, One Target, and never Counter eligible. It redirects
existing hostility toward the Ability user without creating hostility or
compelling hostile action. Its exact outcomes are:

- Minor: "the next time the target acts with hostility, it must direct that
  hostility at you, if you are a valid target"
- Standard: "until the end of the target's next turn in combat, or the end of
  the current meaningful exchange outside combat, whenever the target acts
  with hostility, it must direct that hostility at you, if you are a valid
  target"
- Major: "for the rest of the current scene, whenever the target acts with
  hostility, it must direct that hostility at you, if you are a valid target"
- Legendary: "the target recognises you as its personal rival until the rivalry
  is narratively resolved"

Legendary rivalry is a persistent narrative relationship, not permanent forced
targeting or mechanical domination. The locked combat bible contains hostility
guidance, narrative-resolution examples, and intrinsic exclusions. These
boundaries do not earn Restriction discounts; only an Additional Restriction
that genuinely narrows the contract may do so.

This family remains subject to the parked non-numeric readiness architecture.
Scene Impact / Scope base and the exact variant privilege key are distinct
future components; Legendary rivalry may later require a distinct adjustment.
No numeric costing or discount value is locked.
