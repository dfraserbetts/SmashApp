01_Locked_Rules_And_Combat_Bible.txt

Purpose

This is the active rule-law document for Incarnate combat and runtime
resolution. It consolidates locked rules from the previous locked decision log,
power system spec, and implementation handoff notes.

If implementation disagrees with this document, treat the mismatch as a runtime
alignment issue unless the Architect explicitly changes the rule.


Roll Grammar And Success Quality

Use the existing documented success thresholds, dice sizes, maximum modifier,
and success-tier definitions. This consolidation pass does not change the
numeric threshold table.

Locked modifier/failure ruling:

- Natural 1s are hard failures.
- A natural 1 cannot be modified into a success by positive modifiers.
- A natural 1 cannot become a greater success, critical success, or any higher
  success tier.
- Natural 2s and 3s are recoverable failures.
- Positive modifiers may raise a natural 2 or 3 to the normal success threshold.
- A rescued natural 2 or 3 can produce at most 1 normal success.
- A rescued natural 2 or 3 cannot become a greater success, critical success, or
  higher-tier success, even if the final modified value would otherwise reach
  that tier.
- Only natural successes can spike into greater, critical, or higher-tier
  success results.
- Negative modifiers may reduce natural successes, including reducing them below
  the success threshold.


Action Economy: Legal Main Action Choices

During a Main Action, an actor may choose exactly one legal option:

- Attack with one currently equipped weapon.
- Interact with an object or use an item, such as interacting with an artifact,
  pulling a lever, reloading a weapon, drinking a medicinal tonic, or accessing
  a computer.
- Rush, moving an additional distance up to half the actor's maximum move
  distance.
- Brace, allowing each failed die in a Physical Defence or Mental Defence roll
  to be rerolled once while the Brace benefit is available. A die rerolled by
  Brace cannot be rerolled again.
- Resist, making a cleanup Resist roll against either persistent stacks or one
  ongoing damage effect.

Power use is not a Main Action by default. Powers are declared using a Power
Action or legal Response unless a more specific future rule explicitly creates
an exception.

Main Action Resist has two cleanup lanes:

- Against persistent stacks, roll the authored cleanup Resist Attribute and
  remove stacks equal to rolled successes.
- Against one chosen ongoing damage effect, roll the cleanup Resist lane for
  that effect. Physical ongoing damage defaults to Fortitude Resist. Mental
  ongoing damage defaults to Bravery Resist. Each success removes 1 ongoing
  damage unit from that chosen effect.

Normal active defence Resist, Defence -> Resist powers, Cleanse effects, and
Main Action cleanup Resist remain separate.


Response Window / Incarnate Stack

When a hostile action is declared, resolve in this order:

1. Apply already-active effects.
2. Declare the hostile action and targets.
3. Declare legal Assists.
4. Declare legal Counters.
5. Roll and apply Assists.
6. Roll and apply Counters.
7. Roll the triggering hostile action.
8. If no Counter was declared by a defender and the hostile action produced one
   or more actionable hostile successes, that defender rolls the chosen normal
   active defence.
9. Calculate prevention and effects.
10. Apply the incoming result and the Counter result.
11. Process defeats and cleanup.

All declared actions, Assists, Counters, and Responses resolve before defeat and
cleanup unless a power explicitly interrupts or cancels the triggering action.

Assist is universal by system rule and is not authored per power. Assists are
declared before Counters and roll/apply before Counters. The assisted actor
still chooses one active response path: normal defence or Counter.


Zero-Success Hostile Action Ruling

Actionable hostile successes are incoming successes or hostile units that create
something the defender must actually oppose, prevent, avoid, cancel, or suffer.
Examples include attack successes, wound-bearing successes, hostile effect
successes, control/debuff units, ongoing-effect application successes, or
resistible hostile units.

Zero-success hostile actions trigger declaration-based effects but do not cause
normal defence roll/degradation without actionable hostile successes.

A zero-success hostile action still counts as an attack/targeting event for
"when attacked" or "when targeted by a hostile action" effects.

A zero-success hostile action is not a hit and does not apply successful hostile
effects. Do not trigger "when hit", "when damaged", "when wounded", "when
affected", or "when successfully struck" effects from zero actionable hostile
successes.

The defender does not roll normal Dodge, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or
active Resist against zero actionable hostile successes. The defender does not
commit defensive-pool points and does not suffer resolution-based defence
degradation from normal defence against that action.

Counters, Assists, and declaration-triggered responses that were declared still
count as used and resolve according to their own rules unless they specifically
require the incoming action to hit, wound, damage, affect, or succeed.


Counter Rules

Counter: Yes adds a legal Counter mode. It does not make the power Counter-only.
The same power may still be used normally with a Power Action.

Counter replacement rule:

- A Counter replaces normal active defence unless explicit authored defensive
  packets say otherwise.
- If a Counter is declared, the defender does not also roll Dodge, Physical
  Defence, Mental Defence, or Resist unless the Counter explicitly grants or
  modifies that defence.
- Already-active passive effects, static protection, buffs, debuffs, and
  prepared states still apply because they were established before the response
  window.

Attack Counters are legal. They roll during the Counter step before the
triggering hostile action rolls. The triggering attacker does not get an active
defence roll against the Attack Counter. Attack Counter damage does not cancel
the triggering action unless the Counter explicitly has interrupt or cancel text.

Defence Counters are specialist alternative defensive responses. They replace
normal Dodge, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or Resist rather than stacking
with those normal gates by default.

Movement Counters can avoid or cancel a trigger only if both timing and geometry
are sufficient. First-pass Movement Counter timing is a Clash against triggering
successes and must strictly exceed the trigger successes. Ties fail.

Control interrupt Counters, including Force No Main Action and Force No Power
Action, can cancel the trigger only if they strictly exceed the triggering
action successes. Ties fail.

Counter Dodge remains one-shot unless it also explicitly creates a duration
Dodge Pool.


Legal Defence Modes

The legal Defence modes are:

- Block
- Dodge
- Resist

Block may operate through Physical Defence or Mental Defence according to the
incoming damage lane.

Dodge uses match-or-exceed. If the defender's Dodge successes match or exceed
the attacker's successes on a dodgeable hostile Primary Packet, the hostile
packet is avoided.

Dodge is not normal defence against mental attacks. Mental attacks require
Mental Defence unless a specific action explicitly allows Dodge.

Resist cancels hostile successes or hostile units in the relevant authored
Attribute lane. Resist powers are authored against one core Attribute lane per
packet: Attack, Guard, Fortitude, Intellect, Synergy, or Bravery.

Movement/Reposition is not a Defence mode.


Defence Degradation

Defence degradation is tracked independently by lane.

The independent lanes are:

- Physical Defence
- Mental Defence
- Dodge
- relevant active Resist lane

A defence lane degrades only when that specific lane actually resolves against
one or more actionable hostile successes.

Physical Defence degradation does not affect Mental Defence. Mental Defence
degradation does not affect Physical Defence. Dodge uses its own degradation
track and does not degrade Physical Defence or Mental Defence.

Active Resist degradation applies only to the relevant resisted lane or
attribute unless an explicit rule says otherwise.

Defensive pool-only commitment does not degrade normal defence.


Defensive Pools

Duration Dodge, Physical Block, Mental Block, and Resist powers create finite
defensive pools.

Pool size = successful power-roll successes x potency.

The standard per-trigger commitment cap is potency unless the power explicitly
modifies that cap.

Defensive pools may be committed without normal active defence. A protected
actor may choose one legal path for each matching incoming hostile action:

- take the hit/effect
- commit one matching defensive pool only
- roll the matching normal Dodge / Physical Defence / Mental Defence / active
  Resist only
- commit one matching defensive pool and roll the matching normal defence

If a pool is used with a normal defence or active Resist roll, commit pool points
before the normal roll. The defender cannot wait to see the normal defence or
Resist roll before deciding whether to spend pool points.

Committed points are spent whether they fully stop the incoming result or prove
ineffective.

Pool-only commitment spends finite pool points but does not roll or resolve
Dodge, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or active Resist, so it does not cause
resolution-based degradation.

Dodge Pool:

- Adds Dodge successes.
- Uses match-or-exceed.
- Can be used only against physical attacks or actions that explicitly allow
  Dodge.
- Does not make mental attacks dodgeable.

Physical Block Pool and Mental Block Pool:

- Add direct wound prevention in the matching lane.
- Physical Block Pool does not block mental wounds.
- Mental Block Pool does not block physical wounds.

Resist Pool:

- Cancels hostile successes, stacks, ongoing units, or equivalent units in the
  authored resisted Attribute lane.
- One packet maps to one resisted Attribute unless explicitly authored otherwise.
- Wrong lane does nothing.

Repeated defensive pool applications from the same source power, packet, and
protected target use refresh/replace higher-of-current-or-new by default.
Additive or recharging pools are not default behaviour and require explicit
special authoring, limiting rules, and added cost.

A defender may commit from only one matching defensive pool per incoming action
unless a power explicitly allows combined pool spending.


Passive Defensive Pool Lifecycle

Passive defensive pools do not naturally tick down.

They end when voluntarily ended, removed, cleansed, their carrier ends, source
or target lifecycle ends them, or defeat/cleanup applies.

Passive defensive pools do not become timed pools merely because turns pass.


Ongoing Damage And Persistent Stack Timing

Persistent-state stacks naturally degrade by 1 at the end of each affected
target's turn unless a more specific rule says otherwise.

Ongoing damage does not naturally degrade by 1 each turn. Ongoing damage has an
authored value and duration/tick model.

Pure start-of-turn damage over time does not deal immediate HP damage on
declaration. Stored tick value is the net ongoing value after declaration
defence, counter mitigation, and applicable passive/static mitigation.

Start-of-turn ongoing damage ticks at the affected target's start of turn. Stack
or duration decay follows the relevant end-of-turn or authored lifecycle rule.


Cooldown Lifecycle

Cooldown begins only after a power has completely resolved.

Immediate powers enter cooldown after their immediate resolution sequence
completes and any continuing duration tracking has been noted.

Non-Immediate Lifespan Turns powers treat cast / execution / release as Turn 0
and enter cooldown only after the final resolution window of the last lifespan
turn closes.

Passive Lifespan powers enter cooldown only when voluntarily ended by the caster
or when Cleanse / Resist reduces currently active successes to 0.

Counters and Assists use the same cooldown lifecycle as the power being
executed, treating the executing Response as Turn 0.

Charge is front-loaded and lifespan tracking begins only when the charged power
is released, cast, or otherwise executed.


Target Legality And Self Targeting

Range defines reach or area. Target legality defines who may be selected. Target
count limits selected targets.

Self targeting is selected-target legality, not range exclusivity. Self consumes
one target slot when self is legal.

Packet-local recipient classes may distinguish Primary Targets, Allies, and
Self where a Secondary Packet needs a different target class from Packet 1.
Allies includes self by default unless an explicit authored restriction says
otherwise.


AoE Overlap

Overlapping areas from the same packet at the same timing use one power roll,
pre-defence multiplication by overlap count, and one defence roll.


Defeat Cleanup And Source-Owned Effects

When an actor is defeated and leaves active combat, runtime systems must clean
up source-owned persistent effects, target-owned active statuses, active
defensive pools, cooldown/runtime state as appropriate, and refreshed responses.

Effects whose source or carrier has ended must not silently persist unless an
explicit rule says they linger.
