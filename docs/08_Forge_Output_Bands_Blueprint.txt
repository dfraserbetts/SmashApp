Forge Output Bands Blueprint v1
===============================

Status: Phase 6 source document.
Scope: first-pass Forge output targets after Natural Baseline v1, before Forge implementation starts.
Not scope: Forge code, item schemas, Forge UI, tuning changes, full armour slot weighting, or final canon.

1. Purpose
----------

This blueprint translates the accepted natural-only monster baseline into first-pass Forge item output expectations.

The document exists so Forge implementation can begin from table-facing output bands rather than from backend field names, item origin, rarity assumptions, or ad hoc item stats.

This is a design/output-band document. It does not implement Forge and does not change Power Tuning, Combat Tuning / Monster Baseline Tuning, or Outcome Normalization.

3. Core Law: Output First, Origin Second
---------------------------------------

Forge must price and validate items by final table-facing output profile, not raw backend values and not source origin.

Equivalent final output should compare equivalently whether it comes from:

- natural weapon
- equipped weapon
- natural armour
- equipped armour
- shield
- item modifier
- power

Origin remains visible for debug and authoring clarity. Origin must not create separate hidden balance math when final output is the same.

Important consequences:

- Authored Strength is not the table output. Strength x 2 is table-facing wounds per success.
- Total wounds per success is Strength x 2 x damage type count when each listed damage type receives the output.
- Damage type count consumes output budget.
- Target count, area geometry, and range consume output budget.
- Rarity must not become a raw damage loophole.
- Item level drives core throughput.
- Rarity mostly drives breadth, geometry, versatility, profile complexity, and premium features.

prices damage differently by size:

- Small weapons/shields treat raw wounds as more expensive than One-Handed.
- One-Handed weapons/shields are the middle reference band.
- Two-Handed weapons can carry higher core damage output.

Item level still drives core throughput. Rarity does not increase raw damage

Melee vs ranged expectation:

- Melee may sit at the band center more easily because it pays positional risk.
- Ranged should usually pay for reach, target safety, or flexible engagement by reducing some other budget component.
- A ranged profile at the same wounds per success as a melee profile should be checked for range, target count, rider, reload, and profile breadth.
- Forge Output-Band v1 applies a first-pass ranged core multiplier of 0.8 to ranged weapon profile wound expectations. Strength x 2 does not change, but the expected ranged damage allowance is lower than melee at the same item level and size.

Single-target vs multi-target pressure:

- Single-target band reads total wounds per success against one target.
- Multi-target pressure reads per-target wounds first, then total pressure across targets.
- A two-target weapon at Standard per-target output may create High or Extreme total pressure and must pay for that breadth.
- Area and multi-target output should not be judged only by one target's wounds.

AoE / target-count budget:

- Additional targets consume output budget even if per-target damage is Standard.
- Small AoE can be appropriate as an Uncommon or Rare breadth feature if per-target output remains controlled.
- Large AoE, flexible placement, or repeated area pressure should be Rare+ or Legendary/Mythic-style feature budget, not Common core throughput.

Damage type count budget:

- Each damage type that receives full Strength x 2 output increases total wounds per success.
- Multiple damage types may be allowed, but they consume the same core budget as raw damage.
- Damage-type flexibility can also live in Features & Versatility when it changes options rather than stacking simultaneous wounds.

5. Weapon Profile Rules
-----------------------

One profile vs multiple profiles:

- One main profile is the Common baseline.
- Multiple profiles are breadth, not free extra core throughput.
- A mixed melee/ranged weapon should usually lower one or both profiles, or spend rarity/feature budget.

Melee profile:

- Reads against the single-target wounds per success table.
- Can sit closer to Standard or High for combat-focused items.
- Riders, reach, or extra damage types still consume budget.

Ranged profile:

- Reads against the same table, then pays for distance and safety.
- Longer range, multiple targets, or strong riders should lower raw wounds or increase rarity/read as over-band.

Mixed melee/ranged item:

- May be acceptable when each profile is modest.
- Should not be a full-strength melee weapon plus a full-strength ranged weapon at the same item level without a clear rarity/feature cost.

Alternate damage type profiles:

- Alternate damage type profiles are versatility if they are options.
- Simultaneous damage types are core output if they all deal wounds on the same attack.

Greater Success / rider effects:

- Riders consume feature budget.
- Riders that add wounds, control, action denial, forced movement, protection bypass, or repeat damage may also consume core pressure budget.
- Minor descriptive or narrow riders can remain lower-cost features.

Target count:

- More targets means more total pressure.
- A multi-target weapon should be checked on both per-target output and total-pressure output.

AoE geometry:

- Geometry matters. Cone, line, sphere, burst, and placement flexibility are not interchangeable.
- Simple/small geometry can be a modest feature.
- Large/flexible geometry is a premium feature and may push effective profile level above item level.

Range distance:

- Range is a safety and coverage feature.
- Long range plus high damage plus multi-target pressure is a likely over-budget combination.

Examples at Level 5:

Acceptable Common one-profile weapon:

- Melee, 1 target, one damage type, Strength 3.
- Table output: 6 wounds per success.
- Read: Standard Level 5 core output, narrow profile.

Acceptable Uncommon multi-target weapon:

- Ranged, 2 targets, one damage type, Strength 3 or 4 depending on other features.
- Table output: 6-8 wounds per target, 12-16 total pressure.
- Read: acceptable if this spends breadth budget and does not also carry premium riders/large AoE.

Bad over-budget Level 5 weapon:

- Ranged, 2 targets, two simultaneous damage types, Strength 4, strong rider.
- Table output: 16 wounds per target, 32 total pressure before rider.
- Read: over-band for Level 5 unless deliberately flagged as higher effective profile level.

Acceptable lower-damage flexible weapon:

- One melee profile and one ranged profile, each Strength 2, one damage type.
- Table output: 4 wounds per success per profile.
- Read: Low damage, but acceptable breadth/versatility.

6. Rarity as Breadth / Feature Budget
-------------------------------------

First-pass rarity meaning:

Common:

- Narrow.
- One main profile.
- Minimal rider or no rider.
- Core output should sit around Low to Standard for item level unless the item is deliberately narrow and combat-focused.

Uncommon:

- Modest breadth.
- Extra target, reach, alternate damage type option, minor rider, or slightly more flexible profile.
- Should not simply jump raw damage without tradeoff.

Rare:

- Meaningful breadth.
- Alternate profile, small AoE, useful rider package, or meaningful tactical hook.
- Core throughput should remain item-level appropriate unless effective profile level is flagged.

Legendary:

- Broad tactical profile.
- Larger AoE, stronger rider budget, multiple profiles, or premium features.
- May feel exceptional through options and reliability rather than simply bigger single-target damage.

Mythic:

- Exceptional breadth, geometry, and features.
- May bend normal profile rules, but should still surface effective profile level when raw output behaves above item level.
- Should not be treated as "same item, more damage" by default.

Core principle:

- Item level drives core throughput.
- Rarity mostly drives breadth, geometry, versatility, profile complexity, and premium features.

Guard / Armour Skill interaction:

- PPV is not the whole physical defence story.
- Physical Defence String Value is the table-facing result after PPV and Guard / Armour Skill interaction.
- Forge should eventually show both raw protection and resulting defence-string output.
- High PPV with weak Guard / Armour Skill may be less efficient than moderate PPV with strong defence dice.

Dodge interaction:

- Physical defence includes Dodge.
- Dodge is not PPV and should not be converted directly into PPV.
- Heavy physical protection can interact with Dodge expectations; this belongs to Combat Tuning / Monster Baseline Tuning and later Forge diagnostics, not to a hidden item rarity rule.

Mental asymmetry:

- Do not invent mental Dodge.
- MPV may be narrower or more efficient than PPV because the mental lane has fewer baseline defence components.
- Mental Defence String Value should be read through MPV plus the relevant mental defence dice.

8. Armour Piece Assumptions
---------------------------

First-pass simple model:

- Expected full equipment package assumes around 4 meaningful equipped protection contributors.
- A rough per-piece PPV/MPV estimate can be made from total expected package output divided by 4.
- A 5-piece package can be above expectation and should be visible in diagnostics.
- Slot weighting is deferred.

Level 5 example:

- Standard PPV package: 6-10 total PPV.
- Rough 4-piece estimate: about 2 PPV per meaningful piece, with rounding and slot identity deferred.
- High PPV package: 12-16 total PPV.
- Rough 4-piece estimate: about 3-4 PPV per meaningful piece.
- Standard MPV package: 6-8 total MPV.
- Rough 4-piece estimate: about 1-2 MPV per meaningful piece.
- High MPV package: 9-12 total MPV.
- Rough 4-piece estimate: about 2-3 MPV per meaningful piece.

Deferred slot weighting:

- Head, torso, legs, feet, shoulders, shield, accessories, and special items are not weighted here.
- This document only establishes package-output expectations and a temporary divide-by-4 mental model.
- Slot weighting should be a separate Forge implementation/design pass.

9. Shield Split-Function Model
------------------------------

Shield is a hybrid item, not a free second full item.

A shield may be:

- defence-heavy / low attack
- balanced defence / minor attack
- attack-biased / low defence
- pure defence

Hard rule:

- A shield should not get strong defence and strong attack for free.

Shield attack expectations:

- Shield attack output should usually sit below true weapon output unless defence is sacrificed.
- If a shield has Standard weapon output, its defence should be modest or the item should read as higher effective profile level.
- If a shield has High or Extreme defence, its attack should usually be minor, conditional, or rider-focused rather than raw damage-focused.

10. Core Functionality vs Features & Versatility
------------------------------------------------

Forge should reason with two budget lanes.

Core Functionality:

- weapon wounds per success
- target count
- range category
- AoE geometry
- PPV / MPV
- defence string output
- shield core defence/attack split

Features & Versatility:

- extra profiles
- damage type flexibility
- Greater Success effects
- attribute modifiers
- resist/protection special cases
- returning / reach / reload / tags
- conditional effects
- special triggers
- premium traits

Design rule:

- Core Functionality establishes the item's table-facing throughput.
- Features & Versatility explain why the item is tactically interesting.
- Rarity should buy Features & Versatility more often than raw throughput.

11. Effective Profile Level / Over-Budget Readout
-------------------------------------------------

Forge should eventually show when an item's final output behaves like a higher-level item.

Concept:

- Item level is the authored item level.
- Effective profile level is the level implied by final table-facing output.
- The UI should make over-band output visible without automatically forbidding it.

Examples:

- A Level 5 item with Level 8 damage output should read as over-band or effective Level 8 damage.
- A Rare Level 5 item with broad features but correct Level 5 core throughput should not automatically read as over-damage.
- A Legendary Level 5 weapon with Standard damage, alternate profiles, and a rider package may be healthy.
- A Common Level 5 weapon with Extreme damage and no tradeoff should read as suspect.

12. Equipped Monster Validation Plan
------------------------------------

Stage 3 after Forge bands:

1. Build equipped monsters using expected item packages.
2. Compare against natural-only monsters with similar final output.
3. Test item-carried monsters that lean on weapons/armour rather than natural output.
4. Test over-budget stress monsters.
5. Verify output-origin parity remains true in practice.
6. Confirm natural/equipped source labels remain visible in debug.
7. Confirm final calculator/radar contribution converges when final table-facing output converges.

Validation examples to create later:

- Natural Bruiser-style monster versus equipped weapon/armour bruiser with equivalent table output.
- Glass Cannon-style monster versus equipped burst weapon user with equivalent table output.
- Natural Defender-style monster versus equipped armour/shield defender with equivalent PPV/MPV/defence strings.
- Natural Controller-style monster versus item-carried control/mental survivability profile.
- Over-budget Level 5 weapon that reads as Level 8+ effective profile.

13. Non-Goals

- implement Forge

14. Open Questions
------------------

- Exact per-level numeric bands may need iteration after equipped-monster validation.
- Exact armour slot weighting remains open.
- MPV may need a discount/efficiency rule relative to PPV, but the amount is not settled.
- Shield attack/defence exchange rate remains open.
- Item features need a cost model relative to core output.
- Effective profile level needs a UI representation.
- Damage type flexibility versus simultaneous multi-type damage needs explicit Forge authoring language.
- Reload, reach, returning, slow weapons, hand occupancy, and other tags need budget treatment.
- Rarity and item level need a clear interaction model that preserves item-level throughput truth.
