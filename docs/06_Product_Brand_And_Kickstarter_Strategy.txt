06_Product_Brand_And_Kickstarter_Strategy.txt

Purpose

This is the active product, brand, and Kickstarter strategy document. It keeps
commercial and messaging material separate from rules, costing, and
implementation handoffs.


Product Identity

Working product identity:

- Incarnate

Studio line:

- A Sanctuary Gaming Studios Manifestation.

Optional clarity usage:

- Incarnate TTRPG


Core Brand Thesis

Incarnate is imagination-first.

The player provides the conscious layer:

- fantasy
- intention
- character identity
- table choices
- creativity

The app/system provides the mechanical subconscious:

- legality
- balance
- output
- sheets
- consistency

The core promise is that player intent becomes playable form.


Tagline Direction

Current tagline direction:

- Be anyone.
- Do anything.
- Try everything.

Each line maps to a real system promise:

- Be anyone: character-expression freedom.
- Do anything: broad build and power grammar.
- Try everything: lateral table culture and experimentation.


Brand Posture

Incarnate should feel:

- vivid
- flexible
- empowering
- mechanically trustworthy
- imaginative without becoming vague
- accessible without being shallow

The game should not market itself as a spreadsheet, a balance engine, or a
replacement for imagination. The app is the quiet machinery that makes the
fantasy reliable.


Product Promise

Incarnate lets players imagine a character first and then make that character
mechanically legal, balanced, playable, and printable.

The app turns fantasy into mechanics without forcing the player into narrow
class or archetype lanes.

This is not "pick from these classes and we tell you how your character plays."
It is "how do you imagine your character, and how do we make that work at the
table?"


Kickstarter Demonstration Strategy

Recommended sequence:

1. Show the logo and tagline.
2. Show a short, impossible-seeming character fantasy.
3. Show the app turning that fantasy into legal mechanics.
4. Show the live preview updating.
5. Show the printable character sheet.
6. Show table application.
7. Show the over-limit or cinematic moment.
8. Then explain the system for viewers who want detail.

The strongest proof is a live transformation from concept to table-ready output.


Cover And Category Strategy

The cover should signal:

- product name
- TTRPG category
- imagination-first promise
- a concrete hint that the system supports broad character realization

Open cover decisions remain:

- final logo form
- whether "Core Rulebook" appears on the cover
- single hero image versus triptych or another structure
- how strongly front cover must signal TTRPG category versus relying on back
  cover, Kickstarter, and metadata


Mechanic Naming

The over-limit / cinematic push mechanic has candidate naming:

- Breakthrough
- Smash
- another later name

This remains open. Do not lock final mechanic naming from this document alone.


Studio-Level Language

Sanctuary Gaming Studios can use "Manifestation" as a philosophical studio line
where appropriate.

This positions the studio around turning imagination into playable form, not
only around one product.


What Not To Do

Avoid:

- leading with math before fantasy
- calling the app the point of the game
- implying the game removes human judgement
- overpromising that every conceivable build is effortless
- hiding the tabletop category
- making brand language so abstract that the product promise disappears


Open Brand Decisions

OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION:

- Final over-limit mechanic name.
- Whether "Core Rulebook" appears on the front cover.
- Final Incarnate logo form.
- Final cover art structure.
- Whether "Manifestation" is used on all Sanctuary products or only major

- Whether "SMASH" remains a mechanic name, internal legacy term, or disappears
