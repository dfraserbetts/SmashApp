06_Product_Brand_And_Kickstarter_Strategy.txt

Purpose

This is the active product, brand, and Kickstarter strategy document. It keeps
commercial and messaging material separate from rules, costing, and
implementation handoffs.

This document is product, brand, and commercial strategy authority only. It is
not combat rule, costing, validation, or implementation authority.


Product Identity

Working product identity:

- Incarnate

Studio line:

- A Sanctuary Gaming Studios Manifestation.

Optional clarity usage:

- Incarnate TTRPG


Core Brand Thesis

Incarnate is imagination-first.

The player provides the conscious layer:

- fantasy
- intention
- character identity
- table choices
- creativity

The app/system provides the mechanical subconscious:

- legality
- balance
- output
- sheets
- consistency

The core promise is that player intent becomes playable form.


Tagline Direction

Current tagline direction:

- Be anyone.
- Do anything.
- Try everything.

Each line maps to a real system promise:

- Be anyone: character-expression freedom.
- Do anything: broad build and power grammar.
- Try everything: lateral table culture and experimentation.


Brand Posture

Incarnate should feel:

- vivid
- flexible
- empowering
- mechanically trustworthy
- imaginative without becoming vague
- accessible without being shallow

The game should not market itself as a spreadsheet, a balance engine, or a
replacement for imagination. The app is the quiet machinery that makes the
fantasy reliable.


Product Promise

Incarnate lets players imagine a character first and then make that character
mechanically legal, balanced, playable, and printable.

The app turns fantasy into mechanics without forcing the player into narrow
class or archetype lanes.

This is not "pick from these classes and we tell you how your character plays."
It is "how do you imagine your character, and how do we make that work at the
table?"


Kickstarter Demonstration Strategy

Recommended sequence:

1. Show the logo and tagline.
2. Show a short, impossible-seeming character fantasy.
3. Show the app turning that fantasy into legal mechanics.
4. Show the live preview updating.
5. Show the printable character sheet.
6. Show table application.
7. Show the over-limit or cinematic moment.
8. Then explain the system for viewers who want detail.

The strongest proof is a live transformation from concept to table-ready output.


Closed Beta Playtest Framing

Incarnate's playtest direction should avoid a hard canyon between roleplay and
combat.

Combat scenes should be framed as dynamic story crises with objectives,
pressure tracks, and multiple ways to contribute. The first Closed Beta
vertical slice should demonstrate that characters can matter through attacks,
defence, movement, scene objectives, and roleplay-facing abilities inside the
same crisis.

This is product/playtest framing, not a marketing-copy lock.

Shared build pacing note:

- The shared build pool is now working-titled **Potential** so combat and roleplay
  investment can both be represented under one spend language.
- "You have reached your current Potential" is the preferred player-facing phrase for
  full spend.
- Roleplay-heavy builds should be recognised in the UI as intentional character
  emphasis, not called suboptimal.
- The visual investment scale should communicate:
  - combat build emphasis,
  - roleplay build emphasis,
  - remaining Potential.
- Final naming is still open and should be revisited in the naming pass.

- Smash

This remains open. Do not lock final mechanic naming from this document alone.


Launch Naming Backlog

A full naming pass is required across the system and digital toolkit before
player-facing release.

Development-facing names such as "Power Chassis" are useful internally, but
they are not automatically suitable for public rules text, builder labels, or
marketing copy.

The naming pass should cover:

- rules text
- builder labels
- dropdowns
- tooltips
- previews
- print output
- handbook terminology
- marketing copy

Limit Breaks / over-limit / cinematic push mechanics still need final
player-facing naming. Preserve the current product thesis: player intent becomes
playable form. Do not lock final names in this roadmap capture.

Signature Moves need player-facing iconic treatment. The naming and
presentation pass should make Signature Moves feel like character-defining
moments in builder labels, previews, print output, handbook language, and
marketing language where appropriate. If Limit Break / over-limit mechanics
become Signature-only, that may affect final naming and positioning.

Signature Move Priming may help Signature Moves feel like dramatic character
moments rather than automatic opening attacks. If adopted, naming and
presentation should distinguish priming from ordinary cooldown. If over-limit
or Limit Break mechanics become tied to primed Signature Moves, that may affect
final naming and marketing language. Keep this open, not locked.

This remains open product/naming backlog, not locked rule law.


Studio-Level Language

Sanctuary Gaming Studios can use "Manifestation" as a philosophical studio line
where appropriate.

This positions the studio around turning imagination into playable form, not
only around one product.


What Not To Do

Avoid:

- leading with math before fantasy
- calling the app the point of the game
- implying the game removes human judgement
- overpromising that every conceivable build is effortless
- hiding the tabletop category
- making brand language so abstract that the product promise disappears
