01_Locked_Rules_And_Combat_Bible.txt

Purpose

This is the active rule-law document for Incarnate combat and runtime
resolution. It consolidates locked rules from the previous locked decision log,
power system spec, and implementation handoff notes.

If implementation disagrees with this document, treat the mismatch as a runtime
alignment issue unless the Architect explicitly changes the rule.


Roll Grammar And Success Quality

Use the two-tier mechanical success model:

- Natural or modified 1-3 = failure / 0 successes.
- Natural or modified 4-9 = 1 success.
- Natural or modified 10+ = greater success / 2 successes.
- Maximum mechanical successes per die = 2.
- There is no mechanical 11+ / 3-success tier. Any historical "epic success"
  language is not active mechanical law unless a future explicit rule redefines
  it as non-numeric flavour.

Locked modifier/failure ruling:

- Natural 1s are hard failures.
- A natural 1 cannot be modified into a success by positive modifiers.
- A natural 1 cannot become a greater success or any higher result.
- Natural 2s and 3s are recoverable failures.
- Positive modifiers may raise a natural 2 or 3 to the normal success threshold.
- A rescued natural 2 or 3 can produce at most 1 normal success.
- A rescued natural 2 or 3 cannot become a greater success, even if the final
  modified value would otherwise reach 10+.
- Only natural successes can spike into greater success results.
- Negative modifiers may reduce natural successes, including reducing them below
  the success threshold.


Action Economy: Legal Main Action Choices

During a Main Action, an actor may choose exactly one legal option:

- Attack with one currently equipped weapon.
- Interact with an object or use an item, such as interacting with an artifact,
  pulling a lever, reloading a weapon, drinking a medicinal tonic, or accessing
  a computer.
- Rush, moving an additional distance up to half the actor's maximum move
  distance.
- Brace, allowing each failed die in a Physical Defence or Mental Defence roll
  to be rerolled once while the Brace benefit is available. A die rerolled by
  Brace cannot be rerolled again.
- Resist, making a cleanup Resist roll against either persistent stacks or one
  ongoing damage effect.

Power use is not a Main Action by default. Powers are declared using a Power
Action or legal Response unless a more specific future rule explicitly creates
an exception.

Main Action Resist has two cleanup lanes:

- Against persistent stacks, roll the authored cleanup Resist Attribute and
  remove stacks equal to rolled successes.
- Against one chosen ongoing damage effect, roll the cleanup Resist lane for
  that effect. Physical ongoing damage defaults to Fortitude Resist. Mental
  ongoing damage defaults to Bravery Resist. Each success removes 1 ongoing
  damage unit from that chosen effect.

Normal active defence Resist, Defence -> Resist powers, Cleanse effects, and
Main Action cleanup Resist remain separate.


Cleanse Eligibility And Declared Source Taxonomy

Cleanse and Main Action Resist must both declare the specific target effect,
source, or source category before rolling.

Successes cannot be rolled first and then distributed across several unrelated
effects. Each Cleanse or Main Action Resist roll applies only to the declared
effect, source, or source category.

Exception: a multi-packet or multi-intention power with multiple Cleanse packets
may declare a separate target effect, source, or source category for each
Cleanse packet.

Cleanse eligibility is determined by authored cleanse taxonomy:

- Cleanse Targeted Effect: remove a selected active effect from the target if
  the cleanse result is sufficient.
- Cleanse Source Category: remove or reduce effects from a declared source
  category, such as poison, disease, fire, fear, curse, active power, field,
  attachment, or another explicitly authored category.
- Cleanse All Eligible: affect all currently eligible effects in the authored
  category on the target, subject to potency, scaling, and any per-effect
  limits.
- Cleanse Self Source: remove or reduce effects sourced from the cleansing
  actor or power when explicitly authored.

Cleanse does not automatically remove every hostile state on a target. If a
cleanse does not name the relevant effect, source, source category, or "all
eligible" category, it is not eligible.

Cleanse can remove, if authored and eligible:

- effect stacks from powers.
- effect stacks from items, weapons, armour, shields, or other item-sourced
  effects.
- damage-over-time units.
- control stacks.
- debuffs.
- persistent forced-movement states, movement-control stacks, forced relocation
  states, or pending movement effects.
- active powers, if the Cleanse specifically targets Active Power.
- attached effects, fields, passive effects, channels, and charge states, if
  the Cleanse specifically targets Active Power.

Cleanse cannot:

- remove cooldowns.
- undo movement that has already resolved unless explicitly authored.
- remove multiple unrelated effects with one roll unless multi-packet or
  multi-cleanse authoring allows separate declarations.

Cleanse removal = rolled Cleanse successes x Cleanse potency.

Example: Potency 3 Cleanse with 2 successes removes 6 stacks or units from the
declared target effect, source, or category, assuming the Cleanse is not
resisted.

A cleanse that removes one source category does not remove unrelated categories
merely because they share the same target.

Natural expiry removes all stacks or units belonging to the expiring effect. It
does not clean every condition from the target.

Cleansing a specific affected target removes the effect only from that target.
Cleansing the source, carrier, or active power can remove that source effect
from all affected targets. Cleansing the source is stronger; the source may
resist.

Cleansing an ally of a hostile effect is normally not resisted by that ally.
Cleansing a hostile target of a beneficial effect may be resisted by that
hostile target.

Cooldown immunity, passive carrier ending, field ending, attachment breaking,
and source lifecycle removal are separate from Cleanse. They may remove effects
without using Cleanse, but they do not broaden what a Cleanse packet can target.


Response Window / Incarnate Stack

When a hostile action is declared, resolve in this order:

1. Apply already-active effects.
2. Acting actor declares the hostile action, intended areas, and intended
   targets.
3. Response Window opens.
4. Acting-side allies declare and resolve legal Assists that build or modify the
   incoming threat.
5. Target-side allies declare and resolve legal Assists that build or modify the
   defence or answer.
6. Targeted defenders declare their defence choice or Counter, including any
   legal pool commitments.
7. Response Window closes.
8. Acting actor rolls the triggering hostile action.
9. Defenders roll declared Counter or Defence String if required.
10. Convert rolls into outputs.
11. Apply prevention, Assist pressure, comparisons, results, and cleanup in the
   legal lanes.
12. Process defeats and cleanup after the full triggering attack, power, effect
   tick, or simultaneous result bundle resolves.

All declared actions, Assists, Counters, and Responses resolve before defeat and
cleanup unless a power explicitly interrupts or cancels the triggering action.


Roleplay Window / Roleplay Ability Timing

Powers calculate quantified effects. Roleplay Abilities establish explicit
authored scene outcomes.

Roleplay Ability authoring separates these authorities:

1. Intention defines the broad roleplay lane.
2. Method defines which structured approaches are mechanically legal.
3. Narrative Theme is required free text explaining how this character performs
   the Method. It helps the Game Director judge fictional reach, Attribute, and
   Difficulty, but is not costed or parsed.
4. Outcome Contract is the structured, authoritative mechanical source. It
   defines the reusable purchased scene privilege, derived Outcome Lane and
   Success Outcome, compatible authoring, Counter eligibility, and eventual
   privilege-cost and cooldown pressure.
5. Scene Impact selects an approved magnitude or persistence variant, while
   Scope defines affected breadth.
6. Scene Manifestation is chosen and narrated by the Game Director from the
   current context. It explains how the contract becomes true without another
   roll, action, measurement, or Power-mechanic conversion.

"Method defines the approach. Theme personalises the approach. The Outcome
Contract defines the result."

"Powers calculate effects. Roleplay Abilities establish outcomes."

Narrative Theme describes style, invocation, tools, attitude, or source and
guides Scene Manifestation. It may help the GD judge whether the Method can
plausibly reach the target, choose Attribute, and set situational Difficulty.
It cannot define the guaranteed result; rewrite the Outcome Contract; increase
Impact or Scope; add duration, targeting, secondary outcomes, quantified
bonuses, or penalties; change cost; convert the Ability into another Method; or
bypass Method or contract exclusions. "The Narrative Theme explains the
action, not the result." One or two concrete sentences in plain language are
normally enough. Writing quality, vocabulary, spelling, grammar, dramatic
flair, and eloquence never alter cost, Dice Count, Outcome Contract strength,
success thresholds, or mechanical entitlement. This is not a creative-writing
quality test.

Global Roleplay Declaration Rule

"Incarnate does not eliminate Game Director judgement. It defines where
judgement belongs and protects the mechanical outcomes players have purchased."

The system deliberately leaves bounded fictional judgement to players and the
GD rather than encoding every scene ruling, Method expression, request,
investigative subject, or narrative development. Before the GD chooses
Attribute or Difficulty:

1. The player identifies the Ability.
2. The player identifies the target.
3. The player states the specific Declared Aim: the request they want accepted,
   subject they want investigated, danger they want prevented, or other
   contract-relevant result pursued.
4. The player explains how the selected Method and Narrative Theme operate in
   the current fiction.
5. The GD decides whether the Method fits, the target is accessible, the Aim is
   legal, the Outcome Contract applies, the request/subject fits Impact and
   Scope, and every Additional Restriction is satisfied.
6. Only after hearing the complete declaration does the GD select Attribute and
   set Difficulty.
7. The player confirms the use and rolls under the existing timing and resource
   rules.
8. On success, the accepted Outcome Contract is binding.

The GD may reject or request revision before Difficulty is set. The GD may not
accept a declaration, set Difficulty, allow success, and then weaken, replace,
evade, or withhold the accepted contract. Difficulty measures the challenge of
an eligible declaration; high Difficulty never makes an ineligible declaration
legal. "Declared Aim" is runtime terminology only and is not saved on a
Roleplay Ability or added to the Character Builder.

Roleplay Roll Resolution And Optional Narrative Degrees

Resolve a Roleplay Ability roll in this order:

1. The player declares the Ability and intended use.
2. The player explains what the character does.
3. The Game Director judges legality.
4. The Game Director chooses the relevant Attribute and sets Difficulty.
5. The player rolls the Ability's authored Dice Count using the selected
   Attribute die size.
6. Meeting or exceeding Difficulty establishes the full purchased Outcome
   Contract.
7. Falling below Difficulty means the purchased Outcome Contract was not
   secured.

The GD may use ordinary narrative judgement when a roll falls just short or
succeeds exceptionally well. After a failed roll, the GD may optionally grant a
lesser success, partial opportunity, mixed result, smaller related development,
or useful progress that falls short of the purchased Outcome Contract. This is
not a player entitlement, and the failed roll still does not establish the
contract.

After an exceptionally strong successful roll, the GD may optionally grant a
particularly favourable manifestation, additional narrative benefit, stronger
cooperation or clarity, reduced complications, or another fitting expression of
exceptional success. The full purchased Outcome Contract has already been
secured and remains the guaranteed minimum result.

No fixed margin defines a near success or exceptional success. There is no
lesser-success table, greater-success table, partial-success threshold,
critical-success threshold, extra roll, or mandatory added effect. This
narrative judgement does not alter the existing per-die greater-success rules in
Roll Grammar And Success Quality.

Once a roll meets or exceeds Difficulty, the GD cannot use lesser-success
judgement to reduce, replace, or withhold the full purchased Outcome Contract.
A failed roll does not gain that contract merely because the GD grants a related
lesser success.

Example: a player uses a Small Group Persuasion Ability to convince a mercenary
company to defend a town gate. The mercenaries expect payment and have already
refused, so the GD sets Difficulty 4. The player rolls 3 successes. The company
still leaves because the Small Group contract was not secured, but the GD
decides the appeal reaches one old dwarf, who remains to defend the gate. This
is a valid optional lesser success: it rewards the fiction without retroactively
making the contract successful or weakening the meaning of Difficulty. The GD
was not required to grant it.

Roleplay Ability Costing Doctrine

Roleplay Methods and Narrative Theme define and personalise legal approaches;
Outcome Contracts define purchased results; Scene Impact selects consequence;
Scope selects breadth; Dice Count selects reliability; selected eligible Counter
adds timing flexibility. These semantic authorities remain separate from future
numeric economic tuning.

Ability Name, Narrative Theme, writing quality, Declared Aim/Premise/Shared
Course, runtime target, GD-selected Attribute, runtime Difficulty, Outcome Lane
alone, Intention alone, and Method alone are not direct cost components. Help
and Hinder are classifications, not price bands.

Gross Potential is resolved before any cooldown tradeoff or Additional
Restriction discount. Baseline cooldown derives from Gross Potential, never
discounted Net Potential. An approved Additional Restriction discount applies
last; it cannot lower Gross Potential or the cooldown basis, and intrinsic
contract exclusions or cosmetic wording earn no discount. Net Potential cannot
be negative or erase the economic identity of a non-empty standard Ability.

Numeric Roleplay costing remains unavailable until whole-system balance,
cooldown, tuning, privilege-key, Scope, restriction-approval, and evidence gates
are approved. The focused non-numeric authority is
[Roleplay Ability Costing Readiness](roleplay-ability-costing-readiness.md).

Roleplay Abilities cannot be used as normal Assists and cannot enter the normal
Assist declaration stack. Their timing lanes are:

A. Standard Use in combat

- consumes the actor's Power Action.

B. Standard Use out of combat

- is an ordinary scene declaration;
- has no combat action cost.

C. Roleplay Window

- costs 1 Response;
- is universally available to Roleplay Abilities and is not an authored or
  purchased permission;
- permits only one Roleplay Ability declaration in each Roleplay Window.

D. Counter

- costs 1 Response;
- requires authored Counter permission;
- is legal only when the Ability user is personally targeted by the hostile
  action or effect;
- adds flexibility and does not make the Ability Counter-only.

Resolve a Roleplay Window in this order:

1. The Game Director describes a meaningful, perceivable impending intent,
   movement, preparation, or scene escalation before formal hostile-action
   declaration.
2. The Game Director explicitly opens a Roleplay Window.
3. One eligible actor may spend 1 Response and declare one Roleplay Ability.
4. The Game Director confirms that the declared use fits the selected Outcome
   Contract, its derived Outcome Lane and Success Outcome, Narrative Theme,
   Intention, Method, Scope, Scene Impact, fictional position, and Additional
   Restriction.
5. The Game Director declares Difficulty and the relevant Attribute.
6. The Ability roll resolves.
7. On success, the selected contract's Success Outcome becomes true and the
   Game Director narrates its Scene Manifestation.
8. The Game Director continues the activation from the changed scene state.
9. When a formal hostile action is declared, the normal Response Window /
   Incarnate Stack begins.

Roleplay Window guardrails:

- The Game Director opens the window; players cannot interrupt every
  descriptive sentence.
- A window is for a meaningful scene beat, not every movement or incidental
  act.
- Hidden actions do not need to be revealed merely because Roleplay Abilities
  exist.
- Only perceivable intent can normally create a window.
- Only one Roleplay Ability may be declared in each window.
- A failed Roleplay Ability does not weaken or consume the impending action.
- The selected Outcome Contract and its derived Success Outcome remain binding
  on success; Narrative Theme cannot enlarge them.
- Scope and Scene Impact remain hard ceilings.
- The Game Director decides Difficulty and Attribute after confirming thematic
  legality.

On a successful Roleplay Ability roll, the selected Outcome Contract's derived
Success Outcome becomes true in the scene. No additional roll or target action
establishes that outcome, no distance measurement is required, and the outcome
is not converted into wounds, Block, Dodge, Resist, stacks, units, movement
points, or other quantified Power mechanics. The Ability may materially affect
combat by changing the scene truth from which play continues.

Global Narrative Resolution Rule

"An ongoing narrative outcome is resolved when it is fulfilled, becomes
genuinely impossible, or an identifiable and meaningful change in
circumstances, knowledge, relationships, loyalties, or the target's situation
makes its continuation no longer coherent."

Resolution follows an identifiable fictional change and must be explainable to
the table. Narrative states need not last forever and never permit permanent
puppetry or unconditional control, but they cannot be cancelled merely because
they inconvenience the GD, planned plot, or encounter. Examples include an
agreed task being completed; an alliance losing its purpose; a protected person
fundamentally betraying an oath-holder; new information changing the basis of a
commitment; the target genuinely losing capability or authority; mutual
release; or continuation becoming impossible. A target cannot simply abandon a
successful ongoing contract to restore the original plot direction. This rule
governs every ongoing Outcome Contract, including Legendary Draw Hostile
Attention rivalry.


Full Damage-Prevention Order

Every roll at the table should be fast to mentally compile before the roll and
fast to parse after the roll. Damage prevention uses intuitive layers:

- roll modifiers affect dice/results before success conversion.
- Protection adds damage-type-triggered defence dice.
- Resistance adds damage-type-triggered positive defence-roll modifiers.
- Vulnerability adds damage-type-triggered negative defence-roll modifiers.
- Block affects wounds after success conversion.
- Dodge avoids the packet if it succeeds.
- Assist Pressure suppresses defensive/opposition output and never becomes
  extra damage.

Hostile action resolution order:

1. Declare the action, packets, targets, damage lanes, and damage types.
2. Apply legal already-active effects and response declarations.
3. Build the relevant defence or opposition roll.
4. Apply Protection, Resistance, Vulnerability, and other roll-affecting effects
   before success conversion.
5. Roll and convert successes using the locked roll grammar.
6. Apply Dodge, Block, Resist, defensive pools, Assist pressure, and other
   output/prevention mechanics in their legal lanes.
7. Calculate net wounds or net hostile units.
8. Apply remaining-health cap for actual wounds suffered.
9. Apply wound-band, "when wounded", defeat, Major Injury, and cleanup triggers
   from the resulting actual game-state change unless a rule explicitly uses a
   different source.

Protection, Resistance, and Vulnerability do not directly add, subtract,
multiply, divide, or block wounds. They modify the defence/opposition roll
before output-stage prevention.

Block and block pools prevent wounds after success conversion and before actual
wounds are suffered. Dodge prevents the packet by matching or exceeding the
incoming successes when Dodge is legal. Resist cancels hostile successes or
units in its authored lane.

Assist pressure is output-stage opposition suppression. It can reduce matching
defence, prevention, cancellation, Dodge opposition, Resist cancellation, or
other legal opposition, but it never becomes damage, never creates wound bands,
and never increases the triggering ally's payload beyond that ally's generated
payload.

For Powers, Assist is universal by system rule and is not authored per power.
Power Assists are declared inside the response window. The assisted actor still
chooses one active response path: normal defence or Counter. This ordinary
Power Assist law does not make Roleplay Abilities eligible for normal Assist.

Assist Output, Assist Pressure, And Intention Stacking

An Assist must assist the triggering ally's intended action or defence.

If the ally is defending, the Assist must help that ally defend. If the ally is
performing a hostile action, the Assist must help that hostile action land,
overcome opposition, or resolve more effectively.

A generic Assist cannot create an independent hostile action. A generic Assist
cannot independently apply wounds, damage, control, debuffs, hostile statuses,
forced movement, or other hostile payload to an enemy.

When an offensive or hostile power is used as an Assist, its normal hostile
output is converted into Assist pressure rather than applied as independent
payload. Assist pressure may reduce, occupy, or consume the target's defence,
prevention, Dodge opposition, Resist cancellation, or other relevant opposition
against the triggering ally's action.

Assist pressure never applies as wounds or damage itself. Assist pressure does
not overflow into damage if it exceeds the target's defence, prevention, or
cancellation. Assist pressure cannot increase the triggering ally's final
payload beyond the triggering ally's own generated payload.

The assisting actor's power still uses its own normal output profile to
determine the size and type of Assist pressure, but that output is used only to
interfere with the target's defence or opposition against the ally's action.

Assist Pressure Is Output-Stage Opposition Suppression, Not Payload

Assist pressure is output-stage pressure, not a roll modifier. It does not add
or subtract dice, alter an Attribute, modify die results, add attack successes,
or change the triggering actor's generated payload.

After the triggering action and the relevant defence/opposition are rolled and
converted into outputs, matching Assist pressure reduces the relevant
defensive/opposition output before final comparison.

In the damage-prevention sequence, Assist pressure is applied after rolls are
converted into outputs and after matching defensive prevention is summed, but
before final hostile payload is compared against remaining defence.

Against Dodge, Assist pressure reduces the defender's final Dodge successes
before the Dodge match-or-exceed comparison. It does not add attack successes
and does not become wounds.

Against Block, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or wound prevention, Assist
pressure reduces wounds blocked or prevented before the triggering ally's
payload is applied.

Against Resist or cancellation, Assist pressure reduces relevant cancellation
only when the assisting effect plausibly applies to that lane or effect. It
does not itself apply the hostile effect.

Assist pressure cannot reduce defensive output, Dodge successes, Resist
cancellation, or prevention below 0. Excess Assist pressure is wasted.

Assist pressure does not generate wound bands and cannot be used as a Primary
wound result.

Damage types on the assisting power may describe what opposition the pressure
can plausibly affect, but Assist pressure does not apply those damage types to
the target and does not trigger "when damaged", "when wounded", "when hit by
[damage type]", or similar damage-triggered effects.

Assist pressure only reduces opposition it can legally pressure. Physical Assist
pressure reduces physical prevention or physical defence opposition. Mental
Assist pressure reduces mental prevention or mental defence opposition.
Resist/cancellation pressure applies only when the assisting effect plausibly
matches the resisted lane or effect. A power may explicitly author broader
pressure, but generic Assist does not assume universal pressure.

Example:

- Actor 1 declares a high-damage attack and generates 40 wounds of payload.
- Actor 2 Assists with an attack power and generates 8 wounds worth of Assist
  pressure.
- Target rolls Physical Defence and generates 28 wounds of prevention.
- Actor 2's Assist pressure occupies 8 of the 28 prevention.
- 20 prevention remains against Actor 1's attack.
- Actor 1 deals 20 wounds.
- Actor 2 deals 0 wounds.
- If the target had only generated 5 prevention, Actor 2's Assist pressure
  would occupy 5 prevention, Actor 1 would deal their full 40 wounds, and the
  remaining 3 Assist pressure would be wasted. It would not become damage.

Against Dodge or success-based opposition, Assist pressure may help the
triggering ally overcome the opposed success result. It still cannot create
independent payload and still cannot increase final payload beyond the
triggering ally's own generated payload.

Against Block, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or wound prevention, Assist
pressure may occupy or reduce wound prevention before the triggering ally's
payload is applied. Assist pressure does not itself wound the target.

Against Resist or cancellation, Assist pressure may occupy or reduce relevant
cancellation only if the Game Director agrees the assisting power plausibly
applies to that lane or effect. Assist pressure does not itself apply the
hostile effect.

Against an ally's defence, an Assist may add defensive help according to the
approved Assist procedure. If using an improvised Assist, successes may add to
the ally's defensive result where the Game Director agrees the power can
plausibly help. If using a written defensive or supportive power, it may resolve
as written where legal. This defensive use does not create hostile payload.

Assist modes:

1. Supportive Assist. If the power's written effect is beneficial, defensive,
   restorative, protective, positional, or otherwise directly helpful to the
   ally's triggering action or defence, it may resolve as written where legal.
2. Improvised Assist. If the power is not written as a support power, but the
   Game Director agrees it could plausibly help the ally's triggering action or
   defence, the power is rolled using its normal roll descriptor and an
   appropriate attribute chosen by the Game Director. Its normal hostile or
   non-applicable payload is converted into defensive/supportive Assist
   successes where the ally is defending, or Assist pressure where the ally is
   making a hostile action, instead of resolving as written.
3. Explicit off-turn hostile exception. A power may deal independent off-turn
   damage or hostile effects only if explicitly authored as a Counter, Trigger,
   interrupt, retaliation, trap, or other special off-turn hostile mechanic.
   Generic Assist does not grant this permission.

Multiple Assists may apply to the same triggering action or defence, but each
generic Assist must use a distinct Power Intention. Current Power Intentions
include Attack, Defence, Augmentation, Debuff, Healing, Cleanse, Movement, and
Control.

The declared Assist Power Intention is the mechanical route by which the Assist
helps. Duplicate generic Assists using the same Power Intention do not stack on
the same trigger unless an explicit group-assist, combo, formation, ritual,
squad tactic, or similar authored mechanic allows it.

An offensive power used creatively as an Assist keeps its assisting Power
Intention. A Fireball used to burn incoming arrows is an Attack-intention Assist
that creates Assist pressure. It does not become a Defence-intention Assist
merely because the ally is defending.

Allowed multi-Assist example:

- Assist 1: Attack intention - Fireball creates Assist pressure against the
  target's defence.
- Assist 2: Debuff intention - Curse weakens the target's Defence Attribute.
- Assist 3: Augmentation intention - Bless empowers the ally's Attack
  Attribute.

These may all apply to the same trigger because each uses a distinct Power
Intention.

Blocked duplicate example:

- Assist 1: Attack intention - Fireball creates Assist pressure against Block.
- Assist 2: Attack intention - Arrow barrage creates Assist pressure against
  Block.
- Assist 3: Attack intention - Multi-strike creates Assist pressure against
  Block.

By default, these are duplicate Attack-intention Assists on the same trigger and
do not stack.

If a multi-intention power is used as an Assist, the assisting player must
declare which Power Intention is being used for the Assist if only one intention
is relevant. If the power resolves as written and applies multiple intentions
to the same trigger, it occupies each Power Intention it actually applies.
Multi-intention Assist use must not bypass the duplicate-intention limit unless
explicitly authored.

Design intent: Assist exists to make characters reactive and able to help
allies outside their own turn. Assist should feel cinematic, tactical, and
generous without becoming hidden extra off-turn attacks. Offensive powers can
be used creatively to help, but their hostile payload becomes Assist pressure,
not independent damage. The privilege of Assist is action-economy potency:
helping an ally's action land or helping an ally survive. The cost is that
using a power as an Assist may give up its normal direct payload compared with
using it as intended on the actor's own turn. Multiple Assists should be allowed
when they are tactically distinct, not when they all duplicate the same
mechanical route.

Multiple responders assemble support in acting-side-first, target-side-second
order:

When both sides have Responses to the same triggering action, the acting side
declares and resolves supporting Assists first to establish the final incoming
threat. The target side then declares and resolves supporting Assists that build
or modify the defence / answer. After support is known, targeted defenders
declare their defence choice or Counter, including any legal pool commitments.
The response window then closes; neither side may add more Responses unless an
explicit rule allows it. The triggering action, defensive rolls, Counter rolls,
prevention, comparisons, results, and cleanup then resolve under the normal
resolution order.

Use "acting side" and "target side" rather than only "attacker" and "defender",
because not every triggering action is a simple attack.

This preserves the table rhythm: threat is declared -> threat is supported ->
answer is supported -> defenders choose defence or Counter -> resolve.

The acting side already chooses the action, target, timing, and pressure point,
so it declares its support first. The target side is reacting to a known threat
package, so it builds support second. Defenders then choose normal defence or
Counter with the supported threat and supported answer visible. This prevents
gotcha play where the target side spends defence commitments before the acting
side support is known, while also preventing a response bidding war after
defence choices are declared.

There is no response bidding war by default. Once acting-side and target-side
support and targeted defenders' choices are declared, the response window
closes. A character cannot respond to a Response unless a specific rule
explicitly creates that permission.

Acting-side Assists resolve first because they build or modify the incoming
threat. Target-side Assists resolve second because they build or modify the
answer to that threat. This does not mean damage is applied before defence. It
means support effects are assembled in threat-first, answer-second order before
final application.

The multiple-responder support rule sits inside the declaration / response
window before rolls are resolved. It does not change interrupt law or make a
Counter resolve before the triggering action unless that Counter is separately
authored as an interrupt. Ordinary timing remains: support is assembled,
defence/Counter choices are declared, the incoming action is rolled, declared
Counter or Defence String rolls are made if required, outputs and prevention
resolve, results are applied, and defeats are processed after the full
triggering bundle.

Example 1 - monsters attacking, characters assisting:

- Monster 1 declares a hostile action against Character 1.
- Monster side declares and resolves supporting Assists to Monster 1 first.
- Character side declares and resolves answering Assists second.
- Targeted Character 1 declares normal defence or Counter, including legal pool
  commitments.
- Window closes.
- The action resolves under normal timing.

Example 2 - characters attacking, monsters responding:

- Character 1 declares a hostile action against Monster 1.
- Character side declares and resolves support first.
- Monster side declares and resolves answering support second.
- Targeted Monster 1 declares normal defence or Counter if it has legal access.
- Window closes.
- Resolve normally.

Example 3 - no infinite chain:

If Character 2 Assists Character 1, Monster 2 cannot then add a new Response to
Character 2's Assist unless an explicit future rule permits Responses to
Responses. The ordinary response window is one closed support-and-choice pass,
not a bidding chain.


Zero-Success Hostile Action Ruling

Actionable hostile successes are incoming successes or hostile units that create
something the defender must actually oppose, prevent, avoid, cancel, or suffer.
Examples include attack successes, wound-bearing successes, hostile effect
successes, control/debuff units, ongoing-effect application successes, or
resistible hostile units.

Zero-success hostile actions trigger declaration-based effects but do not cause
normal defence roll/degradation without actionable hostile successes.

A zero-success hostile action still counts as an attack/targeting event for
"when attacked" or "when targeted by a hostile action" effects.

A zero-success hostile action is not a hit and does not apply successful hostile
effects. Do not trigger "when hit", "when damaged", "when wounded", "when
affected", or "when successfully struck" effects from zero actionable hostile
successes.

The defender does not roll normal Dodge, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or
active Resist against zero actionable hostile successes. The defender does not
commit defensive-pool points and does not suffer resolution-based defence
degradation from normal defence against that action.

Counters, Assists, and declaration-triggered responses that were declared still
count as used and resolve according to their own rules unless they specifically
require the incoming action to hit, wound, damage, affect, or succeed.


Counter Rules

Counter: Yes adds a legal Counter mode. It does not make the power Counter-only.
The same power may still be used normally with a Power Action.

Counter replacement rule:

- A Counter replaces normal active defence unless explicit authored defensive
  packets say otherwise.
- If a Counter is declared, the defender does not also roll Dodge, Physical
  Defence, Mental Defence, or Resist unless the Counter explicitly grants or
  modifies that defence.
- Already-active passive effects, static protection, buffs, debuffs, and
  prepared states still apply because they were established before the response
  window.

Attack Counters are legal. They roll during the Counter step before the
triggering hostile action rolls. The triggering attacker does not get an active
defence roll against the Attack Counter. Attack Counter damage does not cancel
the triggering action unless the Counter explicitly has interrupt or cancel text.

Defence Counters are specialist alternative defensive responses. They replace
normal Dodge, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or Resist rather than stacking
with those normal gates by default.

Mixed defensive Counters are Modal Defence Counters by default. If a Counter
contains multiple active defence packets, the actor chooses one eligible active
defence path when declaring the Counter, after the incoming hostile action is
declared but before incoming and Counter rolls are made. The actor may use known
declared information such as attack type, target, damage type, range, and
visible threat context. The actor may not wait to see roll results before
choosing unless the power explicitly has Adaptive Counter wording.

Only the chosen active defence path resolves. Unchosen active defence packets do
not apply, do not create pools, do not provide successes, do not mitigate, and
do not act as fallback protection against that incoming packet. A mixed Dodge +
Block Counter therefore chooses Dodge mode or Block mode on declaration. If
Dodge mode fails, Block does not then apply unless the power is explicitly
authored as a Layered Defence Counter.

Dual-Lane Defence Responses are a paid exception to the normal one-active-
defence-path rule. A Dual-Lane Defence Response is a Counter or Assist that
contains complementary Physical Defence / Block and Mental Defence / Block
packets for the same incoming trigger.

When a Dual-Lane Defence Response is used, roll once using the primary packet's
dice. Those successes also power the complementary defensive packet. The
secondary defensive packet is not rolled separately and does not create an
independent defended entry point.

Each defensive packet applies only to its authored wound lane. Against a
dual-lane incoming action, Physical Defence applies to physical wounds and
Mental Defence applies to mental wounds from the same triggering action.
Against a single-lane incoming action, only the matching lane applies. Packet
order does not prevent the matching lane from applying.

Dual-Lane Defence is about wound lanes, not damage type tags. Physical and
Mental are wound lanes. Fire, Ice, Slashing, Holy, and similar tags are damage
types. Vulnerability, Resistance, and Protection still key from damage types on
the incoming packet unless a more specific rule says otherwise.

Dual-Lane Defence does not include Dodge + Physical Defence, Dodge + Mental
Defence, Physical Defence + Physical Defence, Mental Defence + Mental Defence,
or Resist packets. Those remain modal, adaptive, layered, or otherwise governed
by their own explicit authoring.

Counter terminology:

- Single-lane Counter: a Counter with one active defence path, such as Dodge,
  Physical Defence / Block, Mental Defence / Block, or Resist.
- Modal Defence Counter: the default for a Counter with multiple active defence
  paths; choose one eligible active defence path on declaration.
- Dual-Lane Defence Response: a paid Counter or Assist exception that rolls
  once and applies complementary Physical Defence and Mental Defence packets to
  their matching wound lanes from the same triggering action.
- Adaptive Counter: explicit premium wording that allows the actor to choose
  the active defence path after seeing the relevant roll/result.
- Layered Defence Counter: explicit premium wording that allows more than one
  active defence path against the same incoming packet.

Adaptive Counter and Layered Defence Counter are not default behaviour and are
future / explicit authoring only. Passive/static mitigation remains governed by
existing mitigation rules.

Movement Counters can avoid or cancel a trigger only if both timing and geometry
are sufficient. First-pass Movement Counter timing is a Clash against triggering
successes and must strictly exceed the trigger successes. Ties fail.

Control Counters and Control Assists can halt an incoming action only when
Control is the Primary packet / Primary intention of that Counter or Assist,
and only when the chosen Control effect could logically stop the incoming
action.

An interrupting Control Counter or Assist succeeds only if its Control result
strictly exceeds the incoming action's successes. Ties fail. If the
interrupting Control result does not strictly exceed the incoming action, the
interrupt fails completely: the incoming action resolves normally, and its
successes, wounds, potency, stacks, duration, movement, target count, and other
outputs are not reduced. Failed interrupting Control is not partial mitigation
and does not become weaker Block, Resist, Dodge, tax, or damage reduction.

The attacker does not make a separate Resist roll against an interrupting
Control Counter or Assist by default. The incoming action's successes are the
interruption contest value.

Force No Main Action can interrupt an incoming Main Action if it strictly
exceeds. Force No Power Action can interrupt an incoming Power Action if it
strictly exceeds. A movement-stopping Control may interrupt a charge or
movement-dependent action if it logically prevents the action. A Control that
only affects later movement cannot cancel a melee attack already in range
unless explicitly authored.

Control as a Secondary packet is after-effect Control by default. It cannot
interrupt, cancel, halt, weaken, reduce, or partially mitigate the triggering
incoming action. It applies only after the triggering action resolves, if
powered by the Primary result and if its own timing / legality conditions are
met.

If a Counter has Attack Primary + Control Secondary, the Control does not use
strict-exceed interruption. The Attack may resolve as counter damage, and the
Control is an after-effect only; the incoming action is not halted.

If a Counter has Control Primary + Attack Secondary, the Control may use
strict-exceed interruption. The Attack Secondary does not resolve regardless; it
resolves only if powered by the successful Primary Control result, unless the
power explicitly says otherwise.

If a successful interrupting Control also applies persistent Control stacks,
duration, or an ongoing effect, those follow normal Primary-result scaling.
Default scaling uses applied / net interrupt success, not raw successes, unless
explicitly authored otherwise.

Counter Dodge remains one-shot unless it also explicitly creates a duration
Dodge Pool.


Legal Defence Modes

The legal Defence modes are:

- Block
- Dodge
- Resist

Block may operate through Physical Defence or Mental Defence according to the
incoming damage lane.

Dodge uses match-or-exceed. If the defender's Dodge successes match or exceed
the attacker's successes on a dodgeable hostile Primary Packet, the hostile
packet is avoided.

Dodge is not normal defence against mental attacks. Mental attacks require
Mental Defence unless a specific action explicitly allows Dodge.

Resist cancels hostile successes or hostile units in the relevant authored
Attribute lane. Resist powers are authored against one core Attribute lane per
packet: Attack, Guard, Fortitude, Intellect, Synergy, or Bravery.

Movement/Reposition is not a Defence mode.


Defence Degradation

Defence degradation is tracked independently by lane.

The independent lanes are:

- Physical Defence
- Mental Defence
- Dodge
- relevant active Resist lane

A defence lane degrades only when that specific lane actually resolves against
one or more actionable hostile successes.

Physical Defence degradation does not affect Mental Defence. Mental Defence
degradation does not affect Physical Defence. Dodge uses its own degradation
track and does not degrade Physical Defence or Mental Defence.

Active Resist degradation applies only to the relevant resisted lane or
attribute unless an explicit rule says otherwise.

Defensive pool-only commitment does not degrade normal defence.


Defensive Pools

Duration Dodge, Physical Block, Mental Block, and Resist powers create finite
defensive pools.

Pool size = successful power-roll successes x potency.

The standard per-trigger commitment cap is potency unless the power explicitly
modifies that cap.

Defensive pools may be committed without normal active defence. A protected
actor may choose one legal path for each matching incoming hostile action:

- take the hit/effect
- commit one matching defensive pool only
- roll the matching normal Dodge / Physical Defence / Mental Defence / active
  Resist only
- commit one matching defensive pool and roll the matching normal defence

If a pool is used with a normal defence or active Resist roll, commit pool points
before the normal roll. The defender cannot wait to see the normal defence or
Resist roll before deciding whether to spend pool points.

Committed points are spent whether they fully stop the incoming result or prove
ineffective.

Pool-only commitment spends finite pool points but does not roll or resolve
Dodge, Physical Defence, Mental Defence, or active Resist, so it does not cause
resolution-based degradation.

Dodge Pool:

- Adds Dodge successes.
- Uses match-or-exceed.
- Can be used only against physical attacks or actions that explicitly allow
  Dodge.
- Does not make mental attacks dodgeable.

Physical Block Pool and Mental Block Pool:

- Add direct wound prevention in the matching lane.
- Physical Block Pool does not block mental wounds.
- Mental Block Pool does not block physical wounds.

Resist Pool:

- Cancels hostile successes, stacks, ongoing units, or equivalent units in the
  authored resisted Attribute lane.
- One packet maps to one resisted Attribute unless explicitly authored otherwise.
- Wrong lane does nothing.

Repeated defensive pool applications from the same source power, packet, and
protected target use refresh/replace higher-of-current-or-new by default.
Additive or recharging pools are not default behaviour and require explicit
special authoring, limiting rules, and added cost.

A defender may commit from only one matching defensive pool per incoming action
unless a power explicitly allows combined pool spending.


Defensive Pools With Counters

Using a Counter replaces normal active defence. Already-active defensive pools
are not erased by declaring a Counter, but they do not create a free extra
defence path.

A defensive pool may contribute during a Counter only when at least one of these
is true:

- the Counter contains a matching defensive packet/path for that pool; or
- the power explicitly authors permission for that pool to apply during that
  Counter.

Matching examples:

- A Dodge Counter may use a matching Dodge Pool if the incoming action is
  dodgeable.
- A Physical Defence / Block Counter may use a matching Physical Block Pool.
- A Mental Defence / Block Counter may use a matching Mental Block Pool.
- A Resist Counter may use a matching Resist Pool in the same resisted
  Attribute lane.
- A Dual-Lane Defence Response may use matching pools only in the lanes it
  actually resolves.

Nonmatching examples:

- An Attack-only Counter does not get to use a Block Pool merely because the
  protected actor owns one.
- A Movement Counter does not get a Dodge Pool unless it is also authored as a
  Dodge defensive path or explicit pool-compatible exception.
- A Control Counter does not get a Block, Dodge, or Resist Pool merely because
  Control is defensive in purpose.
- A pool in the wrong wound lane or resisted Attribute lane does nothing.

If no matching Counter path or explicit permission exists, the pool remains
active and unspent.


Passive Defensive Pool Lifecycle

Passive defensive pools do not naturally tick down.

They end when voluntarily ended, removed, cleansed, their carrier ends, source
or target lifecycle ends them, or defeat/cleanup applies.

Passive defensive pools do not become timed pools merely because turns pass.


Duplicate, Same-Name, And Same-Condition Effect Reapplication

Runtime and table handling should track effect instances individually even when
multiple instances share a display name or create the same condition.

Same-source reapplication refreshes or replaces the existing effect by default.
Use the higher-of-current-or-new remaining strength when a finite pool or stack
effect is reapplied from the same source power, packet, and target, unless the
power explicitly says it stacks additively.

Different-source applications can coexist unless a rule or condition explicitly
says they are exclusive. Coexisting sources remain individually traceable for
expiry, cleanse, source cleanup, and contribution reporting.

When multiple source instances create the same binary restrictive condition on a
target, the target experiences one combined burden of that condition. Removing
one source instance does not remove the whole condition if another active source
still maintains it.

For stack or unit removal from multiple active same-condition sources, remove
from the lowest-stack eligible source first unless the cleanse/resist effect
declares a source. If stacks are tied, remove from the oldest eligible source
first. Explicit source-targeted removal overrides this default.

Source cleanup, carrier ending, field ending, attachment ending, and defeat
cleanup remove only the effects tied to that source, carrier, field, attachment,
or defeated actor unless the effect explicitly says it lingers or spreads.


Ongoing Damage And Persistent Stack Timing

Persistent-state stacks naturally degrade by 1 at the end of each affected
target's turn unless a more specific rule says otherwise.

Ongoing damage does not naturally degrade by 1 each turn. Ongoing damage has an
authored value and duration/tick model.

Pure start-of-turn damage over time does not deal immediate HP damage on
declaration. Stored tick value is the net ongoing value after declaration
defence, counter mitigation, and applicable passive/static mitigation.

Start-of-turn ongoing damage ticks at the affected target's start of turn. Stack
or duration decay follows the relevant end-of-turn or authored lifecycle rule.


Cooldown Lifecycle

Cooldown begins only after a power has completely resolved.

Immediate powers enter cooldown after their immediate resolution sequence
completes and any continuing duration tracking has been noted.

Non-Immediate Lifespan Turns powers treat cast / execution / release as Turn 0
and enter cooldown only after the final resolution window of the last lifespan
turn closes.

Passive Lifespan powers enter cooldown only when voluntarily ended by the caster
or when Cleanse / Resist reduces currently active successes to 0.

Counters and Assists use the same cooldown lifecycle as the power being
executed, treating the executing Response as Turn 0.

Charge is front-loaded and lifespan tracking begins only when the charged power
is released, cast, or otherwise executed.


Target Legality And Self Targeting

Range defines reach or area. Target legality defines who may be selected. Target
count limits selected targets.

Self targeting is selected-target legality, not range exclusivity. Self consumes
one target slot when self is legal.

Packet-local recipient classes may distinguish Primary Targets, Allies, and
Self where a Secondary Packet needs a different target class from Packet 1.
Allies includes self by default unless an explicit authored restriction says
otherwise.

Secondary Packet Dependency And Timed Delivery

Secondary Packets are always powered by the Primary Packet result. They never
make an independent roll or create an independent gate. If the Primary Packet
does not apply an operative result, the Secondary Packet does not fire even if
the Secondary Packet's authored timing later occurs.

Secondary Packet simultaneous bundle law:

After the Primary Packet resolves, all independent eligible Secondary Packets
resolve as a simultaneous result bundle. Independent Secondary Packets do not
resolve in authored order and do not modify one another unless explicit
sequential, dependent, or triggered language says otherwise. Numeric effects
that apply to the same target, same timing window, and same pool/lane are
netted before final state changes are applied. Defeat, Major Injury, defeat
cleanup, and other final-state checks occur after the full action result bundle
resolves. Authored packet order is presentation order only unless the power
explicitly defines dependency.

Primary Packet resolves first. Independent Secondary Packets that are eligible
to apply from the Primary result are then collected into one simultaneous
result bundle. Authored packet order is presentation / authoring order only; it
is not a hidden mechanical priority unless a packet is explicitly marked or
worded as sequential, dependent, or triggered.

Examples:

- Physical damage and Physical healing on the same target in the same bundle
  net into one final Physical result.
- Mental damage and Mental healing on the same target in the same bundle net
  into one final Mental result.
- Physical damage and Mental healing do not net because they affect different
  pools / lanes.
- Effects on different targets do not net.

A target is not defeated mid-bundle by one independent Secondary Packet if
another simultaneous Secondary Packet in the same bundle nets the relevant pool
/ lane above the defeat threshold before final state is applied.

Explicit dependent or sequential packets are allowed only when the power text /
authoring explicitly says the packet depends on a prior result. Examples of
dependent / sequential packet language include:

- "Heal the caster equal to net wounds dealt."
- "If this defeats the target, apply Fear to nearby enemies."
- "After the shield is broken, deal damage."
- "Cleanse the poison, then heal extra if poison was removed."

A dependent or sequential packet is not created merely by placing packets in a
particular authored order.

Design intent:

- Prevent hidden optimisation through packet ordering.
- Prevent "Packet 2 before Packet 3" becoming a secret balance lever.
- Preserve cinematic fairness for simultaneous mixed effects.
- Allow explicit special mechanics like lifesteal, execute riders, or
  conditional follow-ups, but require clear authoring and future pricing
  treatment.
- Keep ordinary multi-packet powers predictable for players, Game Directors,
  Codex, and Combat Lab simulation.

Where a wound-based Secondary Packet scales from Primary wound bands, use:

Applied Success Bands = CEILING(Actual Wounds Suffered / Wounds Per Success)

The default Wounds Per Success denominator is the Primary Packet's total
simultaneous wounds per success across all damage components. Multi-damage
Primary Packets increase the band size; they do not generate separate bands per
damage type unless explicitly authored and priced.

Actual Wounds Suffered means wounds that remain after defence, prevention,
pool/prevention spending, and the target's remaining-health cap. Overkill wounds
do not count for default wound-band scaling. A power may scale from overkill
only if it explicitly authors overkill-scaling as a premium exception.

Examples:

- Single damage type: Primary deals 6 physical Slashing wounds per success, so
  the Secondary gains one band per 6 applied Primary wounds, rounding up.
- Three simultaneous damage types: a Potency 3 Primary deals 6 Fire, 6 Ice, and
  6 Slashing wounds per success. Total simultaneous wounds per success is 18,
  so a default wound-band Secondary gains one band per 18 wounds in the Primary
  result, rounding up.
- Component-specific exception: "for every 6 Slashing wounds in the Primary
  result" uses only the Slashing component and must be explicitly authored and
  priced.
- Per-component additive exception: "each damage component generates its own
  wound bands" is a special multi-component band engine, not the default.

For an action-level Secondary that scales from multiple Primary targets, pool
actual wounds suffered across all valid Primary targets once, then calculate
bands from that pooled actual-wound total.

For a target-local Secondary, calculate bands from that target's own actual
Primary wounds suffered only. Target-local Secondaries do not borrow excess
wounds from other targets unless explicitly authored.

Vulnerability, Resistance, Protection, And Wound Bands

Vulnerability, Resistance, and Protection trigger from damage types on the
incoming packet. If the incoming packet has a triggering damage type, apply the
relevant Vulnerability / Resistance modifiers and Protection dice to the one
defence roll against that packet, then calculate the Primary result normally.

Vulnerability is a negative modifier to the relevant defence roll. Resistance
is a positive modifier to the relevant defence roll. Protection adds additional
dice to the relevant defence roll. None of these effects directly increases,
reduces, multiplies, divides, subtracts, or blocks wounds, and none alters the
Primary wound-band denominator.

For Secondary Packet wound-band scaling, resolve the Primary Packet normally,
including any Vulnerability / Resistance / Protection effects on the defence
roll, then calculate wound bands from the resulting Primary wound total using
the Primary Packet's authored total simultaneous wounds per success as the
default band size.

Example: if an Ice packet meets Vulnerability Ice 2 and Resistance Ice 1, the
defence roll receives net -1. If a Fire packet meets Protection Fire 2, add 2
dice to the relevant defence roll. If one Primary Packet deals 6 Fire, 6 Ice,
and 6 Slashing wounds per success, total authored simultaneous wounds per
success is 18; any triggered V/R/P modifies the one defence roll, and default
Secondary wound-band scaling still uses one band per 18 wounds in the resulting
Primary result.

There is no active general Immunity rule. Damage immunities are future Monster
Trait design debt and must define their interaction with defence rolls, wound
results, and Secondary wound-band scaling if implemented.

For Field, Attached, Triggered, and other recurring packet delivery, the first
matching Primary trigger in a round rolls once and stores that roll for that
round. Later matching Primary triggers in the same round reuse that stored roll.
Each affected Primary Target still defends separately, so target-local Primary
results may differ after defence and prevention.

If a Secondary Packet affects the Primary Target, use that target's own applied
Primary result. If a Secondary Packet affects Self, Allies, the Field, an
attached host's allies, or another non-primary recipient, the first successful
Primary application that round sets the Secondary payload for that round. A
fully defended first trigger sets no payload; the next successful trigger in
that round may set it. Once set, later same-round Primary triggers do not
replace, improve, reduce, or add to that payload unless explicitly authored.
When that non-primary Secondary payload is described, refer to the power's
Primary result this round rather than cumulative wounds or successes across all
same-round triggers.

For Field area triggers, per-recipient trigger wording is target-local by
default. "A target enters / starts / ends / leaves the area" affects that target
unless the packet is explicitly authored as area-wide. "An ally starts / ends
their turn in the area" affects that ally unless explicitly authored as
all-allies delivery.

Each packet owns its own Timing and Duration. Secondary Packets do not inherit
the parent power duration, Primary Packet duration, or chassis lifespan as their
own Effect Duration. An Instant Secondary Packet must not be described as
lasting for 1 turn.

The one-roll-per-round stored Primary result rule belongs in global rules
documentation. Individual descriptors should not repeat it unless an authored
power changes that default. Preferred split-timing descriptor shape is "When
[recipient] subsequently [timing], and [Power] [Primary condition] this
round..." rather than "then when...".


AoE Overlap

Overlapping areas from the same packet at the same timing use one power roll,
pre-defence multiplication by overlap count, and one defence roll.


Health-Channel Defeat, Major Injury, And Blaze Of Glory

Physical HP and Mental HP are separate health channels. A packet that deals
physical wounds checks physical HP. A packet that deals mental wounds checks
mental HP. A packet that deals both lanes checks each affected lane after the
full simultaneous result for that packet or Secondary bundle has been applied.

Actor defeat model:

Combat actors require an explicit defeat model / actor category for defeat
handling. Use current project naming conventions in code and data, but the
semantic categories are:

- NORMAL_MONSTER
- PLAYER_CHARACTER
- LEGENDARY_MONSTER

Normal monsters are defeated when either Physical HP or Mental HP reaches 0.
Player Characters and Legendary Monsters do not immediately leave combat at 0;
they enter the Major Injury / Blaze of Glory flow. Do not overload monster tier
alone as the defeat model. Tier may inform defaulting, but defeat model should
be explicit where possible.

Non-Legendary Monsters:

- Non-Legendary Monsters are defeated immediately when either Physical Health
  or Mental Health reaches 0.
- They do not use the Major Injury system.
- They do not use Blaze of Glory unless explicitly promoted to Legendary or
  specially authored.

Player Characters and Legendary Monsters:

- When Physical or Mental Health is reduced to 0, they do not immediately leave
  combat.
- After the full triggering action, effect tick, or simultaneous result bundle
  resolves, they make a Major Injury Trial for the affected side unless they
  declare Blaze of Glory.
- Physical side attributes are Attack, Guard, and Fortitude.
- Mental side attributes are Intellect, Synergy, and Bravery.
- The actor chooses one attribute from the affected side to place at risk.

Major Injury Trial:

- Roll 3 dice using the chosen at-risk Attribute.
- The actor may commit legal Limit Break dice before rolling.
- Limit Break dice are added to the dice pool and use the same chosen Attribute
  die size unless explicitly stated otherwise.
- Count successes using the normal success doctrine: 1-3 = 0 successes, 4-9 =
  1 success, 10+ = 2 successes, maximum 2 successes per die.
- Apply the overflow severity modifier to each die result before success
  counting.
- Count successes from the modified die results.
- A modified die result below 4 produces 0 successes; 4-9 produces 1 success;
  10+ produces 2 successes.
- 0-1 total successes = Major Injury.
- 2 total successes = Minor Injury.
- 3+ total successes = No Injury.

Overflow severity:

- Calculate overflow damage after the full action bundle resolves.
- Overflow damage means damage from the triggering attack, effect tick, or
  netted result bundle in the affected channel that passes the 0 HP point.
- Overflow is event-local. It is not an accumulated negative-HP pool.
- After the Major Injury Trial resolves, the affected Health pool is normalized
  to 0 unless the actor is defeated.
- Future attacks or effects calculate fresh overflow from their own damage
  event.
- At 0 HP, 100% of any further incoming damage in that channel from that future
  event is overflow for that future Trial.
- For every full amount of overflow equal to the actor's level, apply -1 to the
  Major Injury Trial.
- Round down.
- Apply the normal modifier cap.

Examples:

- Level 5 actor suffers 12 overflow damage. 12 / 5 = 2 full level chunks, so
  the Major Injury Trial suffers -2.
- Level 9 actor suffers 18 overflow damage. 18 / 9 = 2 full level chunks, so
  the Major Injury Trial suffers -2.
- Level 9 actor suffers 17 overflow damage. 17 / 9 = 1 full level chunk, so the
  Major Injury Trial suffers -1.
- Level 3 actor suffers 3 overflow damage. 3 / 3 = 1 full level chunk, so the
  Major Injury Trial applies a -1 per-die modifier. Raw results 2, 7, 8 become
  modified results 1, 6, 7, for per-die successes 0, 1, 1. Total successes are
  2, so the result is Minor Injury.
- Level 3 actor suffers 7 overflow damage. 7 / 3 = 2 full level chunks, so the
  Major Injury Trial applies a -2 per-die modifier. Raw results 10, 4, 3 become
  modified results 8, 2, 1, for per-die successes 1, 0, 0. Total successes are
  1, so the result is Major Injury. The modified 8 is only 1 success; greater
  success still requires 10+ after the per-die modifier.
- Level 3 actor suffers 0 overflow damage. Raw results 4, 9, 10 remain
  modified results 4, 9, 10, for per-die successes 1, 1, 2. Total successes are
  4, so the result is No Injury.
- Level 3 actor at 5 HP suffers 8 damage. The event-local overflow is 3 and the
  Trial suffers -1. The Health pool is then normalized to 0.
- Level 3 actor already at 0 HP suffers 7 damage. The event-local overflow is 7
  and the Trial suffers -2.
- If that actor previously suffered a separate 11-overflow injury event, the
  later 7-damage hit still uses overflow 7, not 18.

Major Injury severity does not use actual-wounds-after-cap. Actual wounds after
remaining-health cap is the default wound-band scaling source, not the Major
Injury overflow-severity source.

Trial outcomes:

- No Injury: the actor avoids injury, remains at 0 Health in that channel, and
  must repeat the Trial if struck again in that channel.
- Minor Injury: the actor suffers no current gameplay effect unless a future
  rule explicitly adds one; it may still be tracked and should be logged.
- Major Injury: the chosen attribute suffers a Major Injury and becomes
  unusable.
- Physical and Mental injury tracks are separate.
- A character can have 2 Physical Major Injuries and 2 Mental Major Injuries and
  still not be defeated.
- Once all three attributes on one side have suffered Major Injuries, the actor
  is defeated or killed.

Both channels hit 0 in the same bundle:

- If the same action bundle reduces both Physical and Mental Health to 0 for a
  Player Character or Legendary Monster, resolve both Major Injury events after
  the bundle resolves.
- Resolve Mental Major Injury events before Physical Major Injury events. This
  is a simulation/runtime ordering rule only; it does not change separate
  Physical and Mental injury tracking.
- Do not collapse this into one defeat event for PC / Legendary Major Injury
  handling.
- For ordinary non-Legendary Monsters, either channel at 0 defeats them.

Same-bundle healing:

- Injury checks happen only after the full action result bundle resolves.
- If same-bundle healing restores the relevant pool above 0 before injury
  processing, no Major Injury Trial occurs for that pool.

Removal timing:

- Actors are not removed mid-packet and not at end of round by default.
- A defeated target is removed after the full triggering attack, power, effect
  tick, or simultaneous result bundle that defeated them resolves.

Blaze of Glory:

- Only Player Characters and Legendary Monsters may declare Blaze of Glory.
- Blaze of Glory is declared after the triggering attack or effect bundle would
  prompt a Major Injury Trial and before the Major Injury Trial is rolled.
- Declaring Blaze of Glory cancels the pending Major Injury Trial.
- The actor immediately takes their Blaze of Glory turn.
- Once all Blaze actions resolve, including movement, Main Action, Power Action,
  and Limit Breaks if legal, the actor is defeated and removed from combat.
- If one triggering bundle would cause both Physical and Mental Major Injury
  Trials, one Blaze of Glory declaration cancels all pending Major Injury Trials
  from that bundle.

Combat Lab / simulation policy:

- Automated simulations do not automatically declare Blaze of Glory by default.
  The default auto-sim state is "Blaze available but not used."
- Manual or test mode may force Blaze of Glory later. Do not invent tactical
  Blaze AI until it is explicitly designed and priced.
- When automated simulation must choose an Attribute for a Major Injury Trial,
  choose from the affected side and prefer Attributes seldom used by that actor.
  A Supporter likely chooses Attack more readily than a Glass Cannon. A
  physically focused character should not always get to roll their best physical
  Attribute if that Attribute is already central to their normal build loop.
- If runtime does not yet track Attribute usage frequency well enough, use
  deterministic test hooks first and defer automatic Attribute-choice policy.
- Limit Break dice may be committed by the table rule above, but current Combat
  Lab implementation may ignore Limit Break dice until Limit Break runtime is
  modeled.


Defeat Cleanup And Source-Owned Effects

When an actor is defeated and leaves active combat, runtime systems must clean
up source-owned persistent effects, target-owned active statuses, active
defensive pools, cooldown/runtime state as appropriate, and refreshed responses.

Effects whose source or carrier has ended must not silently persist unless an
explicit rule says they linger.


Structured Roleplay Method Registry

A standard Method definition contains a stable ID, player-facing name, owning
Intention, concise definition, illustrative legal approaches, and explicit
exclusions. Legal approaches are examples, not an exhaustive phrase whitelist.
A declaration may use another fictional expression when it uses the same kind
of approach, respects exclusions, does not claim another Method, and does not
enlarge or rewrite the Outcome Contract. Builders filter Methods by Intention
and contracts by the complete Intention / Method / Scene Impact / Scope
combination.

APPEAL

- Intention: Persuasion
- Definition: "Persuade a target by connecting a clear request to their values,
  interests, loyalties, emotions, relationships, duties, or understanding of
  the situation."
- Legal approaches: present a reasoned argument; make an emotional appeal;
  establish shared interest or mutual benefit; appeal to compassion or mercy;
  invoke duty or responsibility; offer an honest compromise or negotiated
  concession; invoke a relationship, promise, value, or common cause; honestly
  explain the likely consequences of a choice.
- Boundaries: honestly described consequences do not automatically make the
  Method Intimidation. Intimidation applies when threat or fear is the primary
  lever; Deception when deliberate falsehood or concealed misinformation is
  primary. Appeal seeks willing agreement to a clear request.
- Exclusions: does not rely primarily on threats or fear; deliberate lies or
  concealed falsehoods; supernatural control or domination. Does not create
  comprehension, agency, or capability; manufacture love, devotion, intimacy,
  or consent; grant open-ended authority; create permanent general obedience;
  bind anyone other than the selected target; make every request eligible at
  high Impact; or guarantee that the requested task succeeds.
- Current standard contract: SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION.

RALLY

- Intention: Persuasion
- Definition: "Unite a bounded group around one clear shared course by invoking
  common purpose, courage, duty, identity, hope, urgency, or mutual reliance."
- Illustrative legal approaches:
  - Call attention to a common purpose
  - Remind the group of shared duty or identity
  - Present one clear immediate plan
  - Reinforce courage through example
  - Frame the stakes around people who depend on the group
  - Restore focus amid panic, confusion, or disagreement
  - Invoke solidarity or mutual reliance
  - Turn competing reactions toward one honest collective priority
- Distinctions: Appeal persuades one target to accept one bounded request. Rally
  unites a bounded group around one shared course or priority. Intimidation uses
  threat or fear as the primary lever. Deception deliberately creates a false
  or materially misleading conclusion. Rally may honestly describe danger or
  consequences without becoming Intimidation and does not require identical
  tactics from every member.
- Exact exclusions:
  - Does not rely primarily on threats or fear.
  - Does not rely on deliberate lies or concealed falsehoods.
  - Does not use supernatural control or domination.
  - Does not create a shared purpose where no coherent common basis exists.
  - Does not grant open-ended command authority or general obedience.
  - Does not erase individual identity, values, priorities, or judgement.
  - Does not dictate identical tactics, movement, or actions.
  - Does not grant extra actions, measured movement, quantified bonuses, or
    immunities.
  - Does not remove quantified fear, Control stacks, conditions, attachments,
    fields, or active powers.
  - Does not automatically affect anyone outside the selected group.
  - Does not resolve unrelated disputes or disagreements.
  - Does not guarantee that the shared course succeeds.
  - Does not make high Difficulty or Legendary Impact legalise an incoherent
    course.
- Current standard contract: ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE.

MISDIRECT

- Intention: Deception
- Definition: "Lead a target toward a false or materially misleading conclusion
  through direct falsehood, omission, implication, distraction, selective
  truth, staged cues, or deceptive framing."
- Coverage: a misleading conclusion may be created through words, behaviour,
  presentation, existing evidence, or omission. Classification follows the
  conclusion intentionally created, not merely whether every literal sentence
  spoken was false. A technically true statement uses Misdirect when it is
  deliberately framed to cause a materially false conclusion.
- Legal approaches: tell a plausible direct lie; omit a decisive fact to produce
  a false conclusion; redirect attention toward a false explanation; frame true
  evidence misleadingly; imply authority, innocence, danger, or normality that
  does not exist; use an existing disguise, prop, document, reputation, or
  environmental cue deceptively; present selective information to lead the
  target toward the wrong conclusion; stage behaviour or circumstances to
  support a false interpretation. These are illustrative, not exhaustive.
- Distinctions: Appeal uses honest persuasion to gain willing cooperation;
  Intimidation uses threat or fear as its primary lever; Misdirect deliberately
  creates a false or materially misleading conclusion. A distraction uses
  Misdirect only when it establishes a false interpretation rather than merely
  dividing attention.
- Exact exclusions:
  - Does not use supernatural domination or control.
  - Does not rewrite memories.
  - Does not create comprehension, perception, agency, or capability where none
    exists.
  - Does not make an impossible or internally incoherent premise believable.
  - Does not overcome conclusive knowledge already possessed by the target.
  - Does not create physical evidence, documents, disguises, credentials, or
    authority that do not exist.
  - Does not compel a particular action or emotional response.
  - Does not automatically deceive anyone other than the selected target.
  - Does not establish an unlimited collection of separate false claims.
  - Does not guarantee that every inference drawn from the premise benefits the
    Ability user.
  - Does not make high Difficulty or Legendary Impact legalise an ineligible
    premise.
- Current standard contract: ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF.

RESCUE

- Intention: Intervention
- Definition: "Protect or secure a target from immediate danger by warning,
  guiding, sheltering, distracting, interposing, or exploiting a plausible
  route to safety."
- Legal approaches: warn or signal imminent danger; reveal nearby cover,
  concealment, or escape opportunity; create a protective distraction or
  opening; interpose where fictionally possible; guide or coordinate an
  immediate rescue; exploit the environment or social situation to secure
  safety.
- Exclusions: does not directly restore Health or treat Injury; remove
  quantified stacks, units, fields, attachments, or active powers; grant
  another action; move a measured distance; teleport or force impossible
  relocation; or deny an impending hostile act unless the selected contract
  explicitly grants that result.
- Current standard contract: HIDE_FROM_IMMEDIATE_DANGER.

INTERRUPT

- Intention: Intervention
- Definition: "Intervene at the moment of a meaningful impending act to stop,
  spoil, or break that act before it resolves."
- Legal approaches: issue a decisive command or defiance; create a timely
  distraction; physically interpose where fictionally possible; disrupt
  concentration or preparation; invoke authority, an oath, a symbol, or a
  known weakness; reveal something at the decisive moment.
- Exclusions: does not suppress every future action; remove passive effects;
  remove existing stacks, units, fields, attachments, or active powers;
  permanently incapacitate; become Block, Dodge, Resist, or Cleanse; or
  automatically reveal or interrupt a hidden or unperceived act.
- Current standard contract: DENY_IMMINENT_HOSTILE_ACT.

CHALLENGE

- Intention: Intimidation
- Definition: "Confront a target openly to contest its courage, authority,
  attention, reputation, or willingness to oppose you."
- Legal approaches: issue a direct challenge; openly defy the target; appeal
  to honour, pride, duty, or reputation; provoke the target; make a public
  stand; declare a personal rivalry; take responsibility for an accusation or
  confrontation.
- Exclusions: does not create hostility where none exists; compel a hostile
  act; make the Ability user a valid target; force movement, range, or
  impossible actions; alter rolls or quantified output; create Control stacks;
  or automatically override unrelated duties, loyalties, or objectives.
- Current standard contract: DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION.

OVERAWE

- Intention: Intimidation
- Definition: "Intimidate through a credible threat, display of power,
  authority, reputation, certainty, omen, or consequence that makes continued
  opposition feel too dangerous or costly."
- Legal approaches: issue a credible threat; demonstrate power, capability, or
  readiness; invoke a feared reputation; invoke recognised authority or
  judgement; make the likely consequences of resistance unmistakable; exploit
  visible fear, doubt, or failing confidence; present an ominous symbol,
  warning, prophecy, or sign supported by the fiction; show absolute resolve or
  willingness to follow through. These are illustrative, not exhaustive.
- Distinctions: Challenge openly contests courage, authority, attention,
  reputation, or willingness and may establish hostility, attention, or
  rivalry. Overawe makes continued opposition feel untenable through credible
  fear, awe, danger, authority, or consequence. Appeal seeks willing agreement
  through values, interests, compassion, duty, or reason. Misdirect deliberately
  creates a false or materially misleading conclusion. Honest consequences are
  Appeal when shared interest or reason is the primary lever and Overawe when
  fear is primary. False threats or fabricated authority use Misdirect.
- Exact exclusions:
  - Does not rely on deliberate lies or concealed falsehoods.
  - Does not make an impossible, incoherent, or wholly baseless threat credible.
  - Does not use supernatural domination or control.
  - Does not create comprehension, fear, self-preservation, evaluative
    judgement, or agency where none exists.
  - Does not inflict wounds, damage, Injury, or another quantified output.
  - Does not create or remove Fear, Control stacks, conditions, penalties,
    bonuses, fields, attachments, or active powers.
  - Does not force surrender, confession, cooperation, loyalty, or obedience
    unless an exact Outcome Contract explicitly grants a narrower result.
  - Does not dictate the target's exact alternative action, movement, tactics,
    or use of resources.
  - Does not grant open-ended authority over future decisions.
  - Does not create permanent general obedience or permanent emotional rewriting.
  - Does not automatically affect anyone outside the selected Scope.
  - Does not bypass target access, comprehension, fictional plausibility, or
    contract exclusions.
  - Does not guarantee that a threatened consequence is later carried out.
  - Does not make high Difficulty or Legendary Impact legalise an ineligible use.
- Current standard contract: BREAK_SHARED_RESOLVE.

DISCERN_TRUTH

- Intention: Perception
- Definition: "Learn concealed information through observation, deduction,
  evidence, intuition, behavioural tells, contradictions, or another
  perceptive insight."
- Legal approaches: study body language, hesitation, or emotional reactions;
  compare statements for contradiction; inspect evidence or physical details;
  notice omissions, avoidance, or unusual emphasis; connect previously
  discovered clues; use intuitive, spiritual, magical, or supernatural
  perception where supported by Narrative Theme and current fiction.
- Exclusions: does not compel speech, confession, or cooperation; force the
  target deliberately to reveal information; read thoughts or memories without
  a separate contract or Power; publicly expose truth; create evidence; force
  belief; reveal unrelated facts merely because they are secret; or
  mechanically alter the target.
- Current standard contracts, in registry order: UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH and
  REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS. Custom Outcome review remains available for
  other requested results. Spot Weakness, Investigate, Search, Analyse, and
  synonymous desired results do not become new Methods.

Narrative Theme Coaching And GD Adjudication

A good Narrative Theme states what the character does, invokes, notices, says,
or uses; is easy to picture and compare with the Method; and does not write the
successful result into the Theme. Good Discern Truth example: "You study the
target's hesitation, contradictions, and the subjects they avoid." Bad example:
"You force the target to confess everything and expose every lie to the room."
The bad example attempts coercion rather than perception, writes the result,
adds public exposure, and exceeds Discern Truth.

A. Fits: the declaration is inside the Method and selected contract. The GD
chooses Attribute and Difficulty normally.

B. Questionable fit: the approach is within the Method, but current
circumstances make it difficult, indirect, or poorly positioned. The GD may
choose another appropriate Attribute or higher Difficulty, but never penalises
writing style or lack of eloquence.

C. Does not fit: the declaration uses another Method, violates an exclusion,
seeks another Outcome Contract, or enlarges Scope, Impact, duration, or
secondary results. The GD rejects it or asks for revision before Difficulty is
set.

Custom Method - GD Review Required is not an automatically approved Method. The
player supplies a Proposed Method Name and Custom Method Request describing the
reusable approach, legal applications, and boundaries, not its successful
result. Status remains Pending GD Approval; standard contract matching,
automatic costing, and final Cooldown are unavailable or untrusted; saving does
not approve the Ability. The GD or Architect must map it to an existing Method,
approve a new standard Method, or manually approve a campaign-local Method and
its contract compatibility.


Small Group Targeting Doctrine

LOCKED: "A single clearly bounded collection of scene-local individuals who
share the same immediate situation and can all directly perceive and understand
one Method expression."

An adventuring party, squad or patrol, small council, household, rescue team, or
clearly identified cluster of civilians may qualify. A diffuse crowd, multiple
separated clusters, everyone vaguely nearby, an abstract organisation,
settlement, faction, army, or people reached only through intermediaries do not.
No exact numeric headcount is locked: the boundary is a coherent, directly
addressable scene-local group rather than Large Group or Faction / Army scale.

Before Difficulty:

1. The player identifies exact members or an unambiguous bounded set.
2. The player states the exact contract-relevant group result being pursued.
3. The player explains how the selected Method and Narrative Theme reach every
   accepted member.
4. The GD confirms group boundary, target access, perception and comprehension,
   shared immediate situation, every accepted member's eligibility, Method fit,
   Outcome Contract fit, Scene Impact, Scope, and Additional Restriction.
5. The GD then selects Attribute and Difficulty.

Contract-specific declarations include one Declared Shared Course to establish
for Rally and one Declared Opposed Course to break for Overawe. The general
sequence does not remove or weaken the Rally-specific rules below.

The GD may require a narrower or revised group before Difficulty. Difficulty
may reflect internal disagreement, fear, confusion, competing priorities,
pressure, or the least receptive relevant member. If every declared member
cannot legally be affected, the group is narrowed or rejected before Difficulty
instead of producing hidden partial failure after success.

Once accepted and successful, every accepted member receives the outcome. There
is no per-member roll, majority-success interpretation, quietly exempted
dissenter, or invented partial success unless another rule explicitly creates
one. Accepted membership fixes when Difficulty is set: later arrivals, nearby
outsiders, replacements, followers of a leader, and people who merely hear
testimony do not inherit the outcome. Each accepted member remains an individual
actor; Small Group creates no shared turn, mind, action, or resource pool.

Declared Shared Course

Before Difficulty, the player states one clear shared course or collective
priority. It must be one coherent priority, understandable to every accepted
member, within each member's agency and the group's actual capability, possible
in the fiction, appropriate to Impact, and plausibly reachable through Rally.
It may include minimum context but cannot bundle independent commands.

Valid examples include "Stay together and evacuate through the east gate",
"Hold this position until the civilians are clear", "Keep the wounded protected
while we cross the courtyard", "Present a united refusal to the magistrate",
and "Finish the rescue before anyone pursues the thieves". "Hold the gate,
arrest the mayor, search every house, rescue the prisoner, and then follow every
order I give" is an invalid bundle. "Declared Shared Course" is runtime
terminology only; no declaredCourse, sharedCourse, groupMembers, selectedMembers,
rallyTargetIds, or equivalent field is stored on RoleplayAbility or added to
the Builder.


Establish Shared Resolve Outcome Contract Family

LOCKED FAMILY:

- ID: ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE
- Working v0 name: Establish Shared Resolve; eligible for the later naming pass.
- Outcome Lane: Help
- Intention / Method: Persuasion / Rally
- Scope: Small Group only
- Counter eligible: No at every tier
- First approved Small Group Outcome Contract. Large Group and Faction / Army
  remain unproven and receive no standard variant here.
- Core privilege: every accepted member genuinely adopts the same bounded
  shared course and makes a sincere good-faith effort to pursue it for the
  contract's duration.

Exact Impact variants and privilege-cost keys:

- MINOR / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_MINOR: "the selected group steadies around
  one simple immediate course and sincerely pursues it through the current
  meaningful exchange despite ordinary hesitation, confusion, or pressure"
- STANDARD / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_STANDARD: "the selected group adopts one
  clear shared course as its immediate priority for the rest of the current
  scene and sincerely pursues it despite meaningful fear, confusion,
  disagreement, or pressure"
- MAJOR / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_MAJOR: "the selected group commits to one
  difficult shared course for the rest of the current scene and sincerely
  pursues it despite serious fear, division, personal cost, or danger unless
  decisive circumstances or narrative resolution make that course no longer
  coherent"
- LEGENDARY / ESTABLISH_SHARED_RESOLVE_LEGENDARY: "the selected group forms one
  defining shared resolve, pledge, or cause whose consequences extend beyond
  the current scene and sincerely upholds it until it is fulfilled or
  narratively resolved"

"Sincerely pursues" means every accepted member genuinely treats the course as
a priority, makes a good-faith effort, does not merely pretend or deliberately
sabotage it, and does not exploit technical wording to defeat the accepted
intent. Members retain agency over tactics, positioning, timing within normal
opportunities, resources, responses to unexpected developments, and unrelated
beliefs and relationships. They may disagree about execution while sincerely
pursuing the same course. The contract adds no competence, guaranteed success,
resources, actions, synchronised turns, immunity from setbacks, identical
behaviour, or settlement of unrelated disagreements. A coherent emergency or
meaningful change may alter what sincere pursuit requires.

The GD cannot nullify success through instant unexplained loss of resolve,
secret refusal, deliberate sabotage, or casual abandonment because the outcome
inconveniences the planned plot.

Group Narrative Resolution uses the global rule and may occur for the whole
group or one member when genuinely fulfilled, impossible, or made incoherent by
an identifiable meaningful fictional change. One member's resolution does not
cancel every other member's outcome. Examples include completed evacuation, a
defended position ceasing to exist, an impossible rescue, decisive new
information changing the course's basis, one member becoming unable to
continue, mutual abandonment because the purpose no longer exists, or another
change making continuation incoherent. One unable member does not mean group
failure or loss of everyone else's resolve. Plot obstruction is not resolution.

Illustrative examples by tier:

- Minor: frightened civilians stay together through one escape; a confused
  patrol regroups; companions stop arguing for one immediate task; a rescue team
  steadies through one dangerous exchange.
- Standard: defenders hold for the scene; a group completes an evacuation; a
  divided council follows one immediate plan; witnesses maintain a united stance.
- Major: defenders continue a dangerous rescue; divided companions save captives
  before revenge; witnesses resist a powerful threat; a patrol maintains one
  course despite severe division.
- Legendary: a fellowship pledges a defining mission; a small council commits to
  lasting resistance; former rivals form a defining cause; a sworn band adopts
  a commitment extending beyond the scene.

Examples are illustrative, not automatically eligible. Intrinsic exclusions:

- Does not grant open-ended command authority over the group.
- Does not create permanent general obedience.
- Does not apply to unspecified future commands or courses.
- Does not establish more than one bounded shared course.
- Does not dictate identical tactics, actions, movement, or resource use.
- Does not remove individual identity, values, priorities, or judgement.
- Does not grant extra actions or measured movement.
- Does not grant quantified bonuses, penalties, or immunities.
- Does not remove quantified fear, Control stacks, conditions, attachments,
  fields, or active powers.
- Does not guarantee that the shared course succeeds.
- Does not automatically bind people outside the accepted group.
- Does not automatically bind later arrivals or replacement members.
- Does not automatically propagate through leadership, testimony, reputation,
  or institutional authority.
- Does not convert a Small Group into a Large Group, faction, or army.
- Does not settle unrelated conflicts or disagreements.
- Does not permit false participation, deliberate sabotage, or technical
  evasion of the accepted course.
- Does not make an ineligible course legal through high Difficulty or Legendary
  Impact.
- Does not prevent legitimate narrative resolution.
- Does not permit arbitrary cancellation because the outcome inconveniences the
  Game Director or planned plot.

No numeric costs, cooldowns, Scope multipliers, privilege values, restriction
discounts, or Small Group headcount are locked here.


Break Shared Resolve Outcome Contract Family

LOCKED FAMILY:

- ID: BREAK_SHARED_RESOLVE
- Working v0 name: Break Shared Resolve; eligible for the later naming pass.
- Outcome Lane: Hinder
- Intention / Method: Intimidation / Overawe
- Scope: Small Group only
- Counter eligible: No at every tier
- Core privilege: every accepted member ceases pursuing the same bounded
  Declared Opposed Course for the contract's duration.
- No Large Group or Faction / Army variant is created.

Challenge remains unchanged and owns DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION only. Challenge
answers who existing hostility, confrontation, or rivalry focuses upon; Overawe
answers what bounded shared course of opposition the group ceases pursuing.
Neither contract is compatible with both Methods.

Exact Impact variants and privilege-cost keys:

- MINOR / BREAK_SHARED_RESOLVE_MINOR: "the selected group breaks off one small
  immediate shared course of opposition for the current meaningful exchange;
  each member may choose another coherent response but does not pursue that
  course"
- STANDARD / BREAK_SHARED_RESOLVE_STANDARD: "the selected group abandons one
  clear shared course of opposition for the rest of the current scene; each
  member may choose another coherent response but does not pursue that course"
- MAJOR / BREAK_SHARED_RESOLVE_MAJOR: "the selected group abandons one central
  shared course of opposition for the rest of the current scene and does not
  resume it despite serious pressure, loyalty, personal cost, or command"
- LEGENDARY / BREAK_SHARED_RESOLVE_LEGENDARY: "the selected group adopts an
  enduring refusal to pursue one defining course of opposition whose
  consequences extend beyond the current scene and maintains that refusal until
  it is narratively resolved"

Declared Opposed Course

Before Attribute or Difficulty, the player states the one bounded shared course
of opposition the selected group must cease pursuing. It must be one coherent
course; be currently shared, actively pursued, or imminently intended by every
accepted member; be understandable to all; concern the current scene or an
Impact-appropriate ongoing conflict; lie within each member's agency; be
plausibly reachable through Overawe; fit Impact and Scope; and be specific enough
for the table to recognise whether it is being pursued. Members may have
different motives but must share the same course.

Opposition may be hostile, coercive, obstructive, persecutory,
confrontational, or another coherent course directly opposing the Ability user,
their allies, a protected target, or the declared objective. Valid examples
include stopping the storming of a barricade, abandoning pursuit of refugees,
stopping an attempt to burn an archive, refusing to carry out one arrest,
ending an attempt to silence a witness, breaking off a search for a hidden
family, or not completing one sacrifice. It cannot mean permanent enmity ending,
never opposing the user again, general obedience, multiple bundled demands,
all hostility everywhere, or an entire army abandoning a war.

Minimum identifying context is permitted; independent demands are not. Before
Difficulty, the GD may reject or narrow an overbroad, bundled, unshared,
impossible, or inaccessible declaration, or one that actually seeks
cooperation, obedience, movement, or another contract. Once accepted and
successful, the GD cannot evade the result by renaming the same course or using
cosmetic changes of tactic, formation, route, leader, or motive. A genuinely
distinct course remains possible; a semantic rewording does not.

Every accepted member receives the outcome without a separate defence or
resistance roll, majority success, hidden partial result, or secretly exempted
loyalist. Accepted membership remains fixed when Difficulty is set. Members may
retreat, surrender, negotiate, hide, seek safety, protect themselves or others,
pursue an unrelated duty or genuinely distinct objective, remain angry or
hostile, and choose their own movement, tactics, resources, and expression. The
contract controls only the accepted course and never selects the alternative.
They do not automatically become allies, surrender, or flee.

For example, a group that abandons storming a gate may retreat, scatter, seek
cover, negotiate, guard its wounded, wait for circumstances to change, or pursue
a genuinely different objective. It cannot storm another part of the same gate,
continue under another commander, or carry out the same attack individually as
a wording loophole. A group abandoning an attempt to silence a witness may
remain hostile or protect its secrets through a distinct eligible course, but
cannot continue the same silencing attempt through renamed agents or cosmetic
changes.

The global Narrative Resolution rule applies. Minor, Standard, and Major use
their authored exchange or scene duration unless fulfilled, genuinely
impossible, or made incoherent by an identifiable meaningful change. Legendary
may extend beyond the scene. Resolution may apply to the whole group or one
member when genuinely supported; one member's resolution never automatically
releases all others. Incapacity, permanent departure from the relevant conflict,
or another decisive personal change may resolve one individual's outcome.
Planned-encounter need, repeated orders, renamed tactics, inconvenience,
ordinary pressure, anger, shame, loyalty, or renewed encouragement do not cancel
the result, especially at Major or Legendary.

Timing and related contracts: Draw Hostile Attention redirects existing
hostility to the user and remains Challenge / One Target; it does not end
opposition. Deny Imminent Hostile Act makes one target's current or next hostile
action fail, is Major / One Target, and is Counter eligible. Break Shared
Resolve changes a Small Group's intent in a Roleplay Window before formal
hostile-action declaration. It cannot retroactively cancel a resolved action or
be introduced after the ordinary Response Window begins, because it is not a
Counter and does not replace Block, Dodge, Resist, Cleanse, or normal Response
Window rules. Secure Willing Cooperation and Establish Shared Resolve create
willing cooperation or a positive course; Break creates neither. Establish
False Belief uses Misdirect; Overawe cannot use false threats or authority.

Illustrative tier examples:

- Minor: a gang breaks off one rush; guards stop one brief search; a mob stops
  pounding one door; pursuers abandon one immediate seizure attempt.
- Standard: raiders abandon one barricade assault; a patrol abandons refugee
  pursuit; mercenaries cease one eviction attempt; conspirators stop trying to
  silence one witness for the scene.
- Major: cultists abandon the central sacrifice despite commands; soldiers stop
  a central village-burning attempt despite loyalty and punishment; a patrol
  abandons the crisis's principal pursuit; a council bloc ceases one central
  persecution.
- Legendary: a sworn band permanently refuses one defining hunt; a small cabal
  abandons a defining conspiracy; former zealots refuse one foundational
  persecution; an elite guard breaks a long-running course of oppression.

Examples remain subject to Method, access, Impact, Scope, and current fiction.
Intrinsic exclusions:

- Does not dictate the group's exact alternative response.
- Does not automatically force retreat, surrender, confession, cooperation,
  loyalty, alliance, or obedience.
- Does not end every hostile intention or objective held by the group.
- Does not prevent every future act of opposition.
- Does not affect more than one bounded Declared Opposed Course.
- Does not apply to unspecified future commands or conflicts.
- Does not remove individual identity, values, loyalties, emotions, priorities,
  or judgement.
- Does not create one shared mind, action, turn, or resource pool.
- Does not create wounds, damage, Injury, Fear, Control stacks, conditions,
  penalties, bonuses, movement, extra actions, or another quantified Power output.
- Does not remove existing quantified effects.
- Does not automatically make the Ability user or allies safe from unrelated actions.
- Does not automatically bind anyone outside the accepted group.
- Does not automatically bind later arrivals, replacements, followers, or intermediaries.
- Does not automatically propagate through leadership, command, reputation,
  testimony, or institutional authority.
- Does not convert Small Group into Large Group, Faction / Army, or a diffuse crowd.
- Does not make an impossible or incoherent threat effective.
- Does not permit deliberate lies or false authority through Overawe.
- Does not permit cosmetic rewording or tactical relabelling to evade the accepted course.
- Does not guarantee that a threatened consequence is later carried out.
- Does not allow high Difficulty or Legendary Impact to legalise an invalid
  group or overbroad course.
- Does not permit arbitrary cancellation because the result obstructs the
  planned plot or encounter.

Declared Opposed Course and membership are runtime terminology only. No
declaredOpposedCourse, opposedCourse, brokenCourse, groupOpposition,
selectedOpposition, opposedCourseText, intimidatedGroup, group-member storage,
or equivalent Builder field is added. No numeric cost, cooldown, privilege
value, Scope multiplier, saved resistance, or quantified Fear is locked here.


Establish False Belief Outcome Contract Family

LOCKED FAMILY:

- ID: ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF
- Working v0 name: Establish False Belief; eligible for the later naming pass.
- Outcome Lane: Hinder
- Intention / Method: Deception / Misdirect
- Scope: One Target only
- Counter eligible: No at every tier
- Core privilege: the selected target genuinely accepts one bounded false or
  materially misleading premise as true and treats it as true when making
  decisions to which that premise is relevant. The target retains independent
  judgement, values, priorities, and agency over how they respond.

Exact Impact variants and privilege-cost keys:

- MINOR / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_MINOR: "the target accepts one small and
  immediately plausible false premise as true for the current meaningful
  exchange and treats it as true when making relevant decisions"
- STANDARD / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_STANDARD: "the target genuinely accepts one
  plausible false premise relevant to the current situation as true for the
  rest of the current scene and treats it as true when making relevant
  decisions unless meaningful contradictory evidence resolves the belief"
- MAJOR / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_MAJOR: "the target genuinely accepts one
  central false premise shaping the current situation as true for the rest of
  the current scene and continues to treat it as true when making relevant
  decisions unless decisive contradictory evidence, direct experience, or
  narrative resolution ends the belief"
- LEGENDARY / ESTABLISH_FALSE_BELIEF_LEGENDARY: "the target genuinely accepts
  one defining false premise whose consequences extend beyond the current scene
  as true and treats it as true when making relevant decisions until it is
  decisively disproved or narratively resolved"

Declared Premise:

Before the GD selects Attribute or Difficulty, the player states the exact
premise they want the target to accept and explains how Misdirect and Narrative
Theme establish it. The GD judges clarity, boundedness, plausibility to this
target, conclusive contrary knowledge, Method fit, Impact significance and
persistence, target access, and Scope before selecting Attribute and Difficulty.
The GD may reject or request revision before Difficulty. Once accepted and
successful, the target genuinely accepts that premise; the GD cannot secretly
withhold belief, substitute a weaker or unrelated belief, or cancel it because
it obstructs the planned scene. Difficulty measures an eligible deception and
never legalises an impossible or ineligible premise. "Declared Premise" is
runtime terminology only: no declaredPremise, premise, falseBelief, beliefText,
or equivalent field is saved on RoleplayAbility or added to the Builder.

One bounded premise means one coherent conclusion, including only the minimum
context needed to understand it. "The disturbance upstairs is routine
maintenance already being handled" is bounded. "I am authorised, the captain
ordered you to leave, the prisoner is innocent, and the disturbance is
harmless" bundles independent beliefs and must be narrowed or resolved through
separate effects.

Belief and agency: "The target treats the accepted premise as true when making
decisions to which it is relevant, but retains independent judgement,
priorities, values, and agency." A guard who believes the character is
authorised may still deny entry during emergency lockdown; a commander who
believes an ally betrayed them may prioritise civilians; a ruler who accepts a
rightful-heir premise may remain politically opposed; a target who believes a
bridge unsafe may cross when staying is more dangerous. The GD cannot nullify
success through secret disbelief, casual disregard where directly relevant,
instant suspicion without an identifiable fictional reason, or behaviour that
treats the premise as false while claiming belief. The contract controls belief,
not every decision or reaction caused by it.

Contradiction and resolution: suspicion, inconvenience, ordinary doubt, or plot
pressure do not cancel the belief. Credible contradictory evidence appropriate
to Impact, direct disproof through experience, credible confession, exposure,
decisive inconsistency, the authored duration ending, or global Narrative
Resolution may resolve it. Minor is fragile and limited to the current
meaningful exchange. Standard persists for the scene unless meaningful
contradictory evidence resolves it. Major persists for the scene and requires
decisive contradictory evidence, direct experience, or narrative resolution.
Legendary may extend beyond the scene and requires decisive disproof or
narrative resolution. Meaningful evidence materially challenges the premise;
decisive evidence makes it no longer coherently sustainable. Resolution must
follow an identifiable meaningful fictional change explainable to the table.

Illustrative examples by tier:

- Minor: the noise came from outside; the package belongs to someone else; the
  character may wait here briefly; a minor procedural mistake was corrected.
- Standard: the character is an authorised visitor; the suspect used another
  exit; the meeting moved; the current danger has a harmless explanation; an
  ally is uninvolved in the immediate crime.
- Major: a trusted lieutenant betrayed the target; another faction caused the
  current crisis; the target's orders were superseded; a protected person caused
  the current threat; the user has legitimate authority central to the conflict.
- Legendary: the user is the rightful heir; a long-trusted ally belongs to the
  enemy; a foundational historical event happened differently; the target's
  oath rests on a false betrayal; a defining identity, allegiance, origin, or
  relationship is not what the target believed.

Examples remain subject to target knowledge, plausibility, Method, access,
Impact, and current fiction. Intrinsic exclusions:

- Does not compel a specific action.
- Does not control how the target responds to the premise.
- Does not remove the target's independent priorities, values, or judgement.
- Does not rewrite memories.
- Does not establish more than one bounded premise.
- Does not establish unspecified future lies.
- Does not make an impossible or internally incoherent premise believable.
- Does not override conclusive knowledge already possessed by the target.
- Does not automatically survive contradictory evidence sufficient for its
  Impact.
- Does not create supporting proof, evidence, credentials, disguises, authority,
  or documents.
- Does not force third parties to share the belief.
- Does not automatically propagate through testimony, reputation, command, or
  institutional authority.
- Does not guarantee that the target's reaction benefits the Ability user.
- Does not manufacture love, loyalty, devotion, consent, or obedience.
- Does not permit arbitrary cancellation after a successful accepted
  declaration.
- Does not allow Difficulty or Legendary Impact to legalise an ineligible
  premise.

No numeric costs, cooldowns, privilege values, or restriction discounts are
locked here.


Secure Willing Cooperation Outcome Contract Family

LOCKED FAMILY:

- ID: SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION
- Working v0 name: Secure Willing Cooperation; eligible for the later naming
  pass.
- Outcome Lane: Help
- Intention / Method: Persuasion / Appeal
- Scope: One Target only
- Counter eligible: No at every tier
- Core privilege: the target makes a willing choice to cooperate with one clear
  request accepted by the GD before Difficulty is set.

Exact Impact variants and privilege-cost keys:

- MINOR / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_MINOR: "the target willingly complies with
  one small immediate request requiring negligible sacrifice, risk, or
  commitment"
- STANDARD / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_STANDARD: "the target willingly agrees
  to and sincerely carries out one meaningful request involving inconvenience,
  social cost, or modest personal risk"
- MAJOR / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_MAJOR: "the target willingly commits to
  and sincerely pursues one difficult request involving substantial effort,
  personal cost, reputational danger, or physical risk"
- LEGENDARY / SECURE_WILLING_COOPERATION_LEGENDARY: "the target willingly makes
  one defining commitment, alliance, or promise whose consequences extend
  beyond the current scene and sincerely upholds it until it is fulfilled or
  narratively resolved"

Request eligibility:

- The exact request is stated before Attribute or Difficulty.
- It is clear to target and table, one bounded request/commitment, within the
  target's agency, actual authority, and capability, possible in current
  fiction, appropriate to Impact, and plausibly reachable through Appeal and
  Narrative Theme.
- Impact defines maximum burden, not universal permission. The GD may reject
  even a Legendary request when no coherent willing choice could arise from the
  target's values, interests, relationships, loyalties, duties, emotions, or
  understanding.
- Certain or near-certain death, extreme self-sacrifice, betrayal of a defining
  bond, irreversible abandonment of identity, or destruction of everything the
  target values needs an independently coherent basis in existing values and
  circumstances.
- Authority secures the target's sincere use of existing authority; it does not
  compel independently acting subordinates, citizens, soldiers, allies, or
  institution members.

Invalid requests include obeying every command forever, becoming a puppet,
agreeing to all future requests, surrendering independent judgement,
unconditional love, unlimited consent, or becoming capable of the impossible.
Potentially valid Legendary requests include a defined wartime alliance,
protecting a named community, opposing a corrupt institution, committing
existing political/military authority, or a defined peace with an enemy.

"Willingly" makes cooperation the target's accepted fictional choice, not
supernatural domination. "Sincerely" requires good-faith effort without
deliberate sabotage, technical loopholes against the accepted intent, or
pretence. It does not add competence, guarantee task success, prevent setbacks,
remove reasonable judgement about execution, extend beyond the accepted
request, or bind third parties. Ongoing commitments use the global Narrative
Resolution rule.

Illustrative examples by tier:

- Minor: wait briefly, pass a harmless message, allow a quick low-risk
  inspection, or hand over a negligible item.
- Standard: grant a meeting/temporary access, accept meaningful inconvenience,
  support a modest proposal publicly, or assist with limited risk.
- Major: shelter someone at substantial risk, testify against a dangerous
  patron, commit significant time/resources, or accept serious danger.
- Legendary: join an alliance for war, protect a community, commit a ruler's
  available support, or reconcile and uphold a defined peace.

Intrinsic exclusions:

- Does not grant open-ended authority over the target.
- Does not create permanent general obedience.
- Does not apply to unspecified future requests.
- Does not manufacture love, devotion, intimacy, or consent.
- Does not remove the target's identity or independent judgement.
- Does not make an impossible request possible.
- Does not create comprehension, agency, authority, or capability.
- Does not automatically compel anyone other than the selected target.
- Does not guarantee successful completion of the requested task.
- Does not permit deliberate sabotage, false cooperation, or technical evasion
  of the accepted request.
- Does not extend beyond the one request accepted before Difficulty was set.
- Does not make an ineligible request legal through high Difficulty or
  Legendary Impact.
- Does not prevent legitimate narrative resolution.
- Does not permit arbitrary cancellation merely because cooperation becomes
  inconvenient for the Game Director or planned plot.

No numeric costs, cooldowns, privilege values, or restriction discounts are
locked here.


Uncover Concealed Truth Outcome Contract Family

LOCKED FAMILY:

- ID: UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH
- Name: Uncover Concealed Truth
- Outcome Lane: Help
- Intention / Method: Perception / Discern Truth
- Scope: One Target only
- Counter eligible: No at every tier
- Core privilege: the Ability user gains reliable concealed information about
  the selected target. The target is not compelled to speak, confess,
  cooperate, or reveal anything deliberately.

Explicit Impact variants, exact Success Outcomes, and privilege-cost keys:

- MINOR / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_MINOR: "you learn whether the target is
  concealing something relevant to the immediate situation and, if so, its
  general nature; if no qualifying concealed truth exists, you learn that
  nothing relevant is being concealed"
- STANDARD / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_STANDARD: "you learn one useful concealed
  truth about the target relevant to the immediate situation; if no qualifying
  concealed truth exists, you learn that nothing relevant is being concealed"
- MAJOR / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_MAJOR: "you learn a central concealed truth
  about the target that is shaping the current situation; if no qualifying
  concealed truth exists, you learn that nothing relevant is being concealed"
- LEGENDARY / UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH_LEGENDARY: "you learn a defining concealed
  truth about the target whose significance extends beyond the current scene;
  if no qualifying concealed truth exists, you learn that nothing relevant is
  being concealed"

A concealed truth qualifies only when it is true in the current fiction;
concerns the selected target; is not already known or plainly apparent to the
Ability user; meets the relevance and significance of the chosen Impact; and
can plausibly be reached through the selected Method, Narrative Theme, target
access, and current fiction.

On success, if one or more qualifying truths exist, the GD reveals one. When
several qualify, the GD chooses, but the chosen truth must genuinely satisfy
the variant. It may not be vague, useless, speculative, false, or deliberately
misleading. After accepting the declaration and resolving success, the GD may
not use the no-truth result to conceal a qualifying truth. If none qualifies,
the character reliably learns that nothing relevant is being concealed; this
is useful information, not a failed or wasted success. The GD may reject a
declaration before the roll when Method or Narrative Theme cannot plausibly
reach the target or subject. Once accepted and successful, the contract must be
honoured.

When the player declares a specific subject of investigation and the GD accepts
it before setting Difficulty, the successful truth or no-truth result must
directly address that subject. For example, after accepting "Is the Mayor
helping the criminal syndicate with the kidnappings?", success reveals one
qualifying concealed truth concerning that subject or reliably states that
nothing relevant to that subject is being concealed. An unrelated qualifying
secret is not an answer. With an accepted broad subject, the GD retains the
existing authority to select one qualifying truth within that breadth.

Illustrative examples include noticing that a witness hides fear of a
particular person; realising a negotiator is deliberately stalling; discovering
an official secretly protects someone; uncovering the concealed allegiance
shaping current actions; or learning a defining identity, origin, oath, or
loyalty whose significance extends beyond the scene. Examples are illustrative,
not exhaustive.

Intrinsic exclusions:

- Does not compel speech, confession, cooperation, or testimony.
- Does not force the target deliberately to reveal information.
- Does not read thoughts or memories.
- Does not publicly expose the truth.
- Does not create physical evidence or proof.
- Does not force anyone else to believe the information.
- Does not reveal every secret held by or connected to the target.
- Does not reveal every implication or surrounding detail of the learned truth.
- Does not reveal unrelated facts merely because they are secret.
- Does not mechanically alter the target.
- Does not grant quantified bonuses or penalties.
- Does not bypass normal target access, the Discern Truth Method, Narrative
  Theme, or fictional plausibility.
- Does not permit a false, speculative, vague, or deliberately misleading
  answer.
- Does not allow the player to choose among several qualifying truths; the GD
  selects one that satisfies the chosen variant.
- Does not permit the GD to return the no-truth result when a qualifying
  concealed truth exists.
- Does not permit the Game Director to answer an accepted specific subject of
  investigation with an unrelated truth.

No numeric costs, cooldowns, or final privilege values are locked here.


Reveal Exploitable Weakness Outcome Contract Family

LOCKED FAMILY:

- ID: REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS
- Name: Reveal Exploitable Weakness (accepted v0 working name; eligible for the
  later naming pass)
- Outcome Lane: Help
- Intention / Method: Perception / Discern Truth
- Scope: One Target only
- Counter eligible: No at every tier
- Core privilege: on success, the Ability user learns one true and actionable
  weakness, opening, route, pattern, dependency, or leverage point concerning
  the accepted target and Declared Aim, appropriate to the selected Impact.

No new Method is created. Discern Truth already covers observation, deduction,
evidence, physical detail, intuition, contradictions, connected clues, and
other perceptive insight. Spot weakness is a purchased result, not a distinct
reusable approach. Perception / Discern Truth / One Target therefore offers two
compatible standard families in registry order: UNCOVER_CONCEALED_TRUTH then
REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS. Uncover answers "What hidden fact is true?" Reveal
answers "What actionable leverage concerning this target can be used?" The
same fiction may qualify for both, but the selected contract defines the
entitlement. They are never merged, substituted, or silently switched.

Explicit Impact variants, exact Success Outcomes, and privilege-cost keys:

- MINOR / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_MINOR: "you identify one small
  immediately useful weakness, opening, or opportunity concerning the target
  for the current meaningful exchange; if no qualifying exploitable
  opportunity exists, you learn that none is presently accessible"
- STANDARD / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_STANDARD: "you identify one useful
  exploitable weakness, route, pattern, dependency, or leverage point
  concerning the target for the current situation; if no qualifying
  exploitable opportunity exists, you learn that none is presently accessible"
- MAJOR / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_MAJOR: "you reveal one central
  exploitable vulnerability or opportunity concerning the target that is
  shaping the current scene and can materially change how it is approached; if
  no qualifying exploitable opportunity exists, you learn that none is
  presently accessible"
- LEGENDARY / REVEAL_EXPLOITABLE_WEAKNESS_LEGENDARY: "you reveal one defining
  vulnerability, hidden route, dependency, or leverage point concerning the
  target whose significance extends beyond the current scene; if no qualifying
  exploitable opportunity exists, you learn that none is presently accessible"

For a Roleplay Ability, target means the bounded subject to which the selected
contract applies. Where contract and fiction support it, One Target may be one
character, creature, object, mechanism, bounded location or structure, plan,
ritual, environmental feature, or another coherent and specifically identified
scene subject. A fortress gate, identified ritual mechanism, or specific
chamber can be one target. A diffuse crowd, multiple unrelated subjects, entire
battlefield, city, faction, army, whole war, every member of an organisation,
or abstract collection cannot become One Target by being given one label. Scope
still governs breadth. The GD judges boundary and accessibility before choosing
Attribute or Difficulty. Selected subject is runtime interpretation only and
adds no Recipient Type or saved subject field.

A qualifying opportunity must concern the accepted target; answer the accepted
Declared Aim; be true or fictionally coherent; fit the selected Impact; be
reachable through Discern Truth, Narrative Theme, target access, and current
fiction; provide meaningful actionable information rather than trivia; identify
something the players can use, plan around, investigate further, or respond to;
and not already be known and understood in the same actionable form. It may be
previously established, inferred from evidence, an existing mechanical property,
or a previously unspecified coherent detail introduced by the GD as the
successful Scene Manifestation. Such a latent detail cannot contradict
established fiction, invalidate a locked premise, be impossible or incoherent,
exceed Impact or Scope, or create a separate unpurchased outcome.

The opportunity need not have appeared in the GD's notes before the roll. The
GD cannot return no qualifying opportunity merely because none was prepared,
the opportunity changes the expected encounter, the players might use it
successfully, or revelation inconveniences the planned plot. The no-opportunity
result is legal only when established fiction genuinely provides no qualifying
opportunity accessible through the accepted declaration.

Before Attribute or Difficulty, the player identifies the target, what they are
examining, the practical question or objective, and how Discern Truth and Theme
reach it. Legal examples include "How can we stop this ritual?", "Where is the
weakest part of the gate?", "What keeps this portal stable?", "How can we get
civilians through this district safely?", "What behaviour can we exploit?",
and "What does this plan depend upon?" Once accepted, success must answer that
practical subject directly. An unrelated secret or trivia cannot satisfy it.
The GD chooses among qualifying opportunities; the player does not dictate the
answer. The GD may reject or narrow an invalid, impossible, overbroad, or
inaccessible Aim before Difficulty. Difficulty cannot legalise it.

Impact examples are illustrative, not automatic legality:

- Minor: a loose fastening, momentary attention shift, concealed vent, unstable
  foothold, or guard repeatedly checking one doorway.
- Standard: an exposed ritual focus, service route, repeated attack opening,
  gate-mechanism jam point, or plan dependent on one messenger.
- Major: a central battlefield support, visible coordination signal, principal
  magical anchor, decisive logistical dependency, or scene-transforming route.
- Legendary: an oath-bound flaw, fortress-defining hidden route, foundational
  dependency behind apparent invulnerability, long-running plan's leverage
  point, or vulnerability significant beyond the scene.

The GD explains what the opportunity is, what bounded subject it concerns, why
it matters, and how it can inform a meaningful next choice. Actionable does not
mean automatically exploited. Future actions use normal rules and fictional
requirements. The contract may identify an existing quantified property, such
as a creature's existing vulnerability to sacred fire, but neither creates nor
changes that property or its numeric value.

Intrinsic exclusions:

- Does not automatically exploit the revealed opportunity.
- Does not guarantee that a future attempt to use the opportunity succeeds.
- Does not automatically defeat, destroy, control, disable, or bypass the target.
- Does not create wounds, healing, Block, Dodge, Resist, Control stacks,
  conditions, penalties, bonuses, actions, movement, or another quantified
  Power output.
- Does not create or modify an existing mechanical vulnerability.
- Does not create an impossible or internally incoherent weakness, route,
  dependency, pattern, or leverage point.
- Does not contradict established fiction.
- Does not reveal every weakness, route, dependency, or possible solution.
- Does not provide a complete plan for exploiting the result.
- Does not automatically provide required equipment, access, authority,
  capability, evidence, or resources.
- Does not automatically expose the information publicly or force anyone else
  to believe it.
- Does not allow one nominal target to substitute for a diffuse crowd, multiple
  unrelated subjects, a faction, an army, or another broader Scope.
- Does not bypass target access, Discern Truth, Narrative Theme, Scene Impact,
  Scope, or fictional plausibility.
- Does not permit the GD to answer an accepted practical subject with an
  unrelated secret or piece of trivia.
- Does not permit the GD to return no qualifying opportunity merely because no
  opportunity was prepared or because the result inconveniences planned plot.
- Does not allow Difficulty or Legendary Impact to legalise an invalid target
  or impossible investigation.

Target, selected subject, Declared Aim, and discovered opportunity are runtime
context only. No subjectType, investigationSubject, targetSubject,
weaknessTarget, selectedSubject, declaredInvestigation, declaredObjective,
weaknessText, revealedWeakness, exploitableWeakness, opportunityText, or
equivalent saved field is added. No numeric cost, cooldown, privilege value, or
restriction discount is locked here.


Draw Hostile Attention Outcome Contract Family

Outcome Contract families may contain multiple explicit variants. The current
Intention, Method, Scene Impact, and Scope select the variant. Each variant
may own an exact Success Outcome, Counter eligibility, examples, exclusions,
and future privilege-cost adjustment. Selecting a higher Impact does not grant
permission to scale prose generically: every legal tier requires deliberately
authored wording. Keep one stable family ID when the purchased privilege stays
recognisably the same while its persistence or scale changes.

LOCKED FAMILY:

- ID: DRAW_HOSTILE_ATTENTION
- Name: Draw Hostile Attention
- Outcome Lane: Hinder
- Intention / Method: Intimidation / Challenge
- Scope: One Target only
- Counter eligible: No at every current tier
- Core privilege: redirect existing hostility toward the Ability user. It does
  not create hostility or force the target to perform a hostile act.

Explicit Impact variants and exact Success Outcomes:

- MINOR: "the next time the target acts with hostility, it must direct that
  hostility at you, if you are a valid target"
- STANDARD: "until the end of the target's next turn in combat, or the end of
  the current meaningful exchange outside combat, whenever the target acts
  with hostility, it must direct that hostility at you, if you are a valid
  target"
- MAJOR: "for the rest of the current scene, whenever the target acts with
  hostility, it must direct that hostility at you, if you are a valid target"
- LEGENDARY: "the target recognises you as its personal rival until the rivalry
  is narratively resolved"

Legendary creates a persistent narrative relationship, not indefinite forced
targeting. Narrative resolution may include death, decisive defeat,
reconciliation, fulfilled vengeance, a broken oath, earned mutual respect,
either rival abandoning the conflict, or another clear fictional resolution
accepted by the GD.

For this contract, hostility may be physical, social, political, legal, or
another form of directed opposition. Examples include an attack, threat,
accusation, hostile questioning, pursuit, an attempt to expose or humiliate,
political opposition, an attempt to undermine or obstruct, or a courtroom
argument directed against another person. The GD decides whether the target is
acting with hostility in context. Redirection applies only when hostility
already exists, the Ability user is a valid target for that hostility, and the
redirection remains fictionally and legally possible.

Intrinsic contract exclusions, which do not earn Restriction discounts:

- Does not create hostility where none exists.
- Does not compel the target to take a hostile action.
- Does not make the Ability user a valid target.
- Does not force movement or create range/reach.
- Does not force impossible or illegal actions.
- Does not alter attack rolls, damage, defence, or quantified output.
- Does not create Control stacks or another Power effect.
- Does not prevent an area or multi-target action from affecting others where
  normally legal.
- Does not prevent immediate self-preservation.
- Does not automatically override duties, loyalties, or objectives unrelated
  to the redirected hostility.
- Does not affect a group.
- Minor through Major do not persist beyond their exact authored windows.
- Legendary creates rivalry, not permanent mechanical domination.

Additional Restrictions earn discounts only when they narrow an Ability beyond
the Outcome Contract's intrinsic applicability and exclusions. Repeating a
contract boundary earns no discount. "Only works when the target is hostile"
for Draw Hostile Attention, "Only works on hostile actions" for Deny Imminent
Hostile Act, and "Only works when the target is in danger" for Hide from
Immediate Danger are no-discount restatements. Potentially meaningful added
restrictions include only Agents of Morgoth, only a sworn rival, only after the
target harms an ally, or only while defending a named place or person. Numeric
discounts are not locked here.
