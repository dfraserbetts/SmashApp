Roleplay System Roadmap

The roleplay side of Incarnate still needs building. It is smaller than the
combat side, but still substantial.

Future roleplay-system work includes:

- out-of-combat Trials
- out-of-combat Clashes
- Characteristics
- Traits
- roleplay-facing reward loops
- character-sheet tracking for roleplay resources
- Game Director guidance for rewards, consequences, and limits


Optional Character Archetypes

OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION.

Potential shape:

- Players may opt into an Archetype.
- Archetypes impose mechanical and / or narrative restrictions.
- Archetypes grant major bonuses in certain fields.
- Archetypes include a failure clause or consequence model.

Captured example only, not final locked design:

Hero!

- Restriction: must always tell the truth, no matter the outcome.
- Bonuses: good people are inclined to believe them, even in implausible
  situations; good people are likely to offer aid, shelter, or resources if
  able; +1 success level to roleplay checks when telling the truth.
- Failure clause: if they lie, they may be scorned, treated as an outcast, lose
  hospitality / trust, and redemption may be difficult or impossible depending
  on the lie.
- Failure penalty: -1 success level to roleplay checks.


Character Backstory Points

OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION.

Potential shape:

- Earned through defining moments tied to character backstory.
- Granted by the Game Director.
- Spent to influence major story moments such as sudden return of allies,
  sudden power-up, sudden rescue, or another meaningful narrative intervention.
- Needs limits, anti-abuse guidance, and Game Director permission rules.


Personal Goals

  increases after prerequisite quests, or alternative / forced stat growth based

Signature Move Uniqueness

OPEN / NEEDS ARCHITECT DECISION.

Purpose:

- Ensure the Signature Move feels like the character's iconic move, not merely
  a normal power with a larger point budget.

Risk:

- If Signature Moves are differentiated only by raw points, players may treat
  them as optimization slots rather than defining character expressions.

Current early design directions:

1. Signature-only permission.
   Signature Moves may be allowed to combine effects or reach dramatic shapes
   normal powers cannot.
2. Signature-only scaling.
   Signature Moves may get special upgrade hooks at certain character levels.
3. Signature-only narrative weight.
   Signature Moves should be named, previewed, printed, and treated as the
   character's iconic move.
4. Signature-only cooldown/friction rules.
   Signature Moves may hit harder because the system knows they are major
   moments with special usage friction.
5. Main-power concentration limits.
   Normal powers may need limits so they cannot easily become more
   signature-like than the Signature Move.
6. Limit Break / over-limit restriction.
   The Limit Break / over-limit / cinematic push mechanic could potentially be
   restricted to Signature Moves only, which may be a simple way to make
   Signature Moves feel unique.

Do not stack all uniqueness protections by default. Future design should find
the smallest uniqueness package that makes Signature Moves feel iconic.

This item connects to Character Creator, Power Builder, Limit Break /
over-limit design, naming, handbook presentation, and product feel.
